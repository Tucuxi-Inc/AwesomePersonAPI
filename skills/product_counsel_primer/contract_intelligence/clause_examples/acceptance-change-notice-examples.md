# Acceptance, Change Management, and Notice Clause Examples

## Acceptance Testing

### Customer-Favorable (Rigorous Testing)

**Example 1 - Detailed Acceptance Process**
```
ACCEPTANCE TESTING

(a) DELIVERY. Upon completion of each Deliverable, Vendor shall deliver
    the Deliverable to Customer with documentation demonstrating
    conformance to the Specifications.

(b) TESTING PERIOD. Customer shall have [30] days from delivery to
    test the Deliverable against the Acceptance Criteria in Exhibit [X]
    ("Acceptance Period").

(c) ACCEPTANCE OR REJECTION. Customer shall provide written notice of
    acceptance or rejection. Rejection shall specify in reasonable
    detail the deficiencies and the Acceptance Criteria not met.

(d) CURE. If rejected, Vendor shall correct the deficiencies within
    [15] days and resubmit. Customer shall have [15] additional days
    to retest.

(e) REPEATED FAILURE. If the Deliverable fails acceptance testing after
    [2] cure attempts, Customer may:
    (i) accept with an equitable price reduction;
    (ii) require Vendor to engage additional resources at Vendor's expense;
    (iii) terminate the applicable SOW and receive a full refund.

(f) DEEMED ACCEPTANCE. Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon:
    (i) Customer's written acceptance;
    (ii) use in production for [5] consecutive business days;
    (iii) expiration of the Acceptance Period without rejection.
```

---

### Vendor-Favorable (Streamlined Acceptance)

**Example 2 - Simplified Acceptance**
```
ACCEPTANCE

Customer shall test Deliverables within [15] days of delivery.

Deliverables are deemed accepted unless Customer provides written
rejection specifying: (a) the specific Specification requirement not
met; (b) documentation of the failure.

Vendor shall have [30] days to cure rejected Deliverables. Vendor's
sole obligation is to use reasonable efforts to correct deficiencies.

Use of the Deliverable in production, or failure to provide detailed
rejection within the testing period, constitutes acceptance.
```

## Change Management

### Balanced Change Control

**Example 3 - Standard Change Order Process**
```
CHANGE ORDERS

(a) CHANGE REQUEST. Either party may request changes by submitting a
    written Change Request describing: the proposed change, reason for
    the change, and requested implementation date.

(b) IMPACT ASSESSMENT. Within [10] business days, Vendor shall provide
    a written Impact Assessment including: technical feasibility;
    impact on scope, schedule, and fees; estimated additional costs.

(c) CHANGE ORDER. No change is effective until documented in a written
    Change Order signed by authorized representatives of both parties.
    The Change Order shall specify: change description; revised
    Specifications (if applicable); fee adjustment; schedule adjustment.

(d) PRICING. Changes shall be priced at Vendor's then-current hourly
    rates as set forth in Exhibit [X]. Fixed-price changes may be
    proposed at Customer's request.

(e) SCHEDULE IMPACT. Approved changes extending scope shall automatically
    extend affected milestone dates by a period proportional to the
    additional scope.

(f) NO UNAUTHORIZED WORK. Vendor shall not perform out-of-scope work
    without a signed Change Order, except in emergencies with Customer's
    written authorization (including email).
```

---

### Customer-Favorable (Change Control)

**Example 4 - Customer-Controlled Changes**
```
CHANGES

Customer may direct changes at any time by written notice.

(a) PRICING. Vendor shall provide pricing within [5] business days.
    If parties cannot agree, Vendor shall proceed at T&M rates capped
    at Vendor's estimate plus [10%].

(b) SCOPE REDUCTION. Customer may reduce scope. Vendor shall provide
    an equitable price reduction within [10] days.

(c) MINOR CHANGES. Customer may make minor clarifications that do not
    materially affect scope without a formal Change Order.

(d) DISPUTES. Pricing disputes shall not delay the change. Vendor
    shall proceed and the parties shall resolve pricing per the
    dispute resolution procedures.
```

---

### Vendor-Favorable (Change Protection)

**Example 5 - Vendor-Protective Change Terms**
```
CHANGES

All changes require a signed Change Order before work begins.

(a) NO OBLIGATION. Vendor is not obligated to accept changes that
    materially impact project architecture, timeline, or resources.

(b) PRICING. Changes shall be priced at Vendor's then-current rates
    plus [15%] for expedited implementation.

(c) SCHEDULE. Schedule impacts shall be addressed case-by-case. Vendor
    does not guarantee original timelines when scope changes.

(d) CHANGE CAP. If aggregate Change Orders exceed [25%] of original
    SOW value, either party may request renegotiation of terms.
```

## Notice Provisions

### Standard Notice Clause

**Example 6 - Comprehensive Notice Provision**
```
NOTICES

All notices and other communications required or permitted under this
Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given:

(a) upon personal delivery;
(b) one (1) business day after deposit with a nationally recognized
    overnight courier;
(c) three (3) business days after deposit in the U.S. mail, certified
    or registered, return receipt requested, postage prepaid;
(d) upon confirmed receipt by email (with read receipt or reply),
    provided a copy is sent by another method within 24 hours.

Notices shall be addressed to the addresses set forth in the signature
block, or to such other address as a party may designate by notice
pursuant to this Section.
```

---

### Modern Email-Forward

**Example 7 - Email-Primary Notice**
```
NOTICES

Notices may be given by email to the designated addresses below.
Notice is effective upon transmission unless the sender receives a
delivery failure notification within [24] hours.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, notices of termination, breach, or
legal proceedings shall additionally be sent by overnight courier.

Each party shall maintain current email addresses and promptly notify
the other of changes.

If to Customer: legal@customer.com
With copy to: contracts@customer.com

If to Vendor: legal@vendor.com
```

### Timing Requirements

**Example 8 - Notice Periods Summary**
```
ADVANCE NOTICE REQUIREMENTS

The following advance notice periods apply:

| Notice Type | Period |
|-------------|--------|
| Termination for convenience | 90 days |
| Termination for cause | 30 days (with cure period) |
| Price increases | 60 days |
| Service modifications | 30 days |
| Scheduled maintenance | 7 days (48 hours emergency) |
| Change of control | 30 days |
| Address changes | 10 days |
| Breach notice | Prompt (within 5 business days) |

"Business days" excludes weekends and federal holidays.
```

## Procedural Best Practices

### Example 9 - Change Log Requirement
```
CHANGE DOCUMENTATION

All changes shall be documented in a Change Log including:
- Change Request date and number
- Description of requested change
- Impact Assessment date
- Change Order number and date
- Approval signatures
- Implementation status

The Change Log shall be reviewed at each project status meeting and
maintained as part of the project records.
```

---

### Example 10 - Notice Cure for Defects
```
NOTICE DEFECTS

Actual receipt of notice that substantially complies with this Section
shall be effective notice regardless of technical defects in delivery
method.

Failure to provide notice shall not waive any right unless the non-
notifying party is materially prejudiced by such failure.
```
