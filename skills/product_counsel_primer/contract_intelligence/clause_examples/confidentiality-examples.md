# Confidentiality Clause Examples

## Overview

This skill provides real clause language examples for confidentiality provisions extracted from actual contracts. Examples are organized by component and party position (discloser-favorable, balanced, recipient-favorable).

## Confidential Information Definition

### Discloser-Favorable (Broad Definition)

**Example 1 - Comprehensive Definition with Categories**
```
"Customer Confidential Information" means any confidential or
proprietary information disclosed to Vendor (including, without
limitation, to Vendor's employees, consultants, subcontractors or
other agents; collectively the "Service Personnel") by Customer,
either directly or indirectly in writing, graphically, electronically,
orally or by inspection of tangible objects (including, without
limitation, information learned while working on a project, speaking
to others, or by observing Customer's equipment, instruments,
facilities or processes) while such Service Personnel are present
at Customer's facilities, provided in each case such information is
of such nature that a reasonable person would recognize it and
believe it to be confidential or proprietary.

Such Customer Confidential Information may include, without limitation:
- any compound, extract, media, vector, gene, oligonucleotide, target,
  protein, sequence, cell, cell line, formulation, sample or other
  composition, including any structural information and/or any methods
  of synthesis relating to the foregoing;
- any assay, procedure, algorithm, software program, discovery,
  invention, model, formula, data, result, idea or technique;
- any trade secret, trade dress, copyright, patent or other
  intellectual property right;
- any information related to research, development, purchasing,
  manufacturing, engineering, marketing, servicing, sales, financing,
  legal or other business or corporate financial activities;
- any present or future products, design details and specifications,
  prices, plans, forecasts, suppliers, clients, customers, employees,
  consultants or investors.
```

**Why This Favors Discloser:**
- Covers all disclosure methods (written, oral, visual, inspection)
- Reasonable person standard (no marking required)
- Extensive non-exhaustive list of covered categories
- Includes observed information at facilities
- Covers information from third-party personnel

---

**Example 2 - Information Deemed Confidential**
```
"Customer Confidential Information" shall also include any information
disclosed or otherwise obtained by Vendor or made available to Vendor
by Customer either directly or indirectly in writing, orally or
otherwise that:

(a) if disclosed in writing is marked as confidential at the time
    of disclosure, and

(b) if disclosed in any other manner is identified, described in
    reasonable detail and designated as confidential in a letter
    or other writing delivered to Vendor within 30 days for such
    disclosure.
```

**Marking Requirement:** This example requires marking/designation, which is more recipient-favorable than a "reasonable person" standard.

---

### Balanced Position

**Example 3 - Mutual Definition with Reasonable Standard**
```
"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by one party
("Discloser") to the other party ("Recipient") that is designated as
confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential
given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation:
- technical information, including inventions, trade secrets, know-how,
  software, databases, and designs;
- business information, including operations, plans, strategies,
  financial data, and customer information;
- the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

Confidential Information does not include information that:
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach by Recipient;
(b) was known to Recipient before disclosure, as documented;
(c) is independently developed by Recipient without use of
    Confidential Information;
(d) is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction.
```

---

### Recipient-Favorable (Narrow Definition)

**Example 4 - Strict Marking Requirement**
```
"Confidential Information" means information that is:

(a) disclosed in writing or other tangible form and is clearly marked
    "Confidential" or "Proprietary" at the time of disclosure; or

(b) disclosed orally and identified as confidential at the time of
    disclosure, provided that such oral disclosure is summarized in
    writing and delivered to Recipient within ten (10) days of the
    oral disclosure.

Information not marked or identified as required above shall not be
treated as Confidential Information regardless of its content.
```

**Why This Favors Recipient:**
- Strict marking requirement
- Short window for oral confirmation
- No "reasonable person" standard
- Clear boundaries on what's protected

## Confidentiality Obligations

### Discloser-Favorable (Strong Protection)

**Example 5 - Comprehensive Obligations**
```
Vendor shall not use any Customer Confidential Information for any
purpose whatsoever other than the performance of the services on
behalf of Customer, or disclose Customer Confidential Information
to any third party.

Vendor agrees that Customer Confidential Information shall remain
Customer's sole property.

Vendor further agrees to take all reasonable precautions to prevent
any unauthorized disclosure, duplication or use of Customer
Confidential Information.

Vendor's obligation of confidentiality with respect to a given item
of Customer Confidential Information shall remain in effect during
the term of this Agreement and for a period of seven (7) years
following termination of this Agreement.
```

**Strong Protection Elements:**
- Strict use limitation (only for contracted services)
- No third-party disclosure
- Ownership confirmation
- Affirmative duty to prevent unauthorized disclosure
- Extended survival period (7 years)

---

### Balanced Position

**Example 6 - Standard Mutual Obligations**
```
CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

Each party agrees to:

(a) hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict
    confidence using at least the same degree of care it uses to
    protect its own confidential information, but in no event less
    than reasonable care;

(b) not use the other party's Confidential Information except as
    necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights
    under this Agreement;

(c) not disclose the other party's Confidential Information to any
    third party except: (i) to employees, contractors, and agents
    who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality
    obligations at least as protective as these; (ii) as required
    by law or court order, provided the Recipient provides reasonable
    notice to enable the Discloser to seek protective treatment;

(d) notify the Discloser promptly upon discovery of any unauthorized
    use or disclosure.
```

---

### Recipient-Favorable (Limited Obligations)

**Example 7 - Standard of Care with Carve-Outs**
```
Recipient shall protect Confidential Information using the same
degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information,
but not less than reasonable care.

Recipient may disclose Confidential Information:
(a) to its employees, affiliates, contractors, and advisors with
    a need to know, without requiring separate confidentiality
    agreements if Recipient's policies provide equivalent protection;
(b) as required by law, regulation, or legal process;
(c) to potential acquirers in connection with due diligence for
    a change of control transaction;
(d) with Discloser's prior consent.
```

**Recipient-Favorable Elements:**
- "Same degree of care" (may be low for some recipients)
- Broad affiliate sharing
- No requirement for separate NDAs with personnel
- Change of control carve-out

## Standard Exclusions

### Example 8 - Complete Exclusions Set
```
EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Vendor's obligations hereunder shall not apply to information which:

(i) is known to Vendor at the time of disclosure to Vendor by Customer
    as evidenced by competent written records of Vendor;

(ii) has become publicly known and made generally available through
     no fault of Vendor;

(iii) is rightfully received by Vendor from a third party that was
      not obligated to keep such information confidential;

(iv) is developed by Vendor independently without reference to, or
     use of, Customer Confidential Information, as demonstrated by
     documentary evidence;

(v) is disclosed by Vendor with the Customer's prior written approval; or

(vi) is disclosed as required by law or compulsory legal process,
     provided Vendor gives Customer prompt notice and reasonable
     opportunity to seek protective order.
```

## Compelled Disclosure

### Example 9 - Detailed Legal Process Provision
```
COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

If Recipient is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information,
Recipient shall:

(a) provide Discloser with prompt written notice (to the extent
    legally permitted) to allow Discloser to seek a protective order
    or other appropriate remedy;

(b) cooperate with Discloser, at Discloser's expense, in seeking
    such protective order;

(c) disclose only that portion of Confidential Information that
    is legally required to be disclosed;

(d) use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for
    any Confidential Information so disclosed.

Disclosure pursuant to legal compulsion shall not, by itself,
terminate the confidential status of such information.
```

## Return/Destruction of Information

### Example 10 - Return and Certification
```
RETURN OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon
Customer's earlier request, Vendor will deliver to Customer all
Confidential Information and all tangible embodiments thereof in
Vendor's possession or control.

Vendor shall certify in writing within ten (10) days of such
request that all Confidential Information has been returned or
destroyed and that no copies have been retained except as required
for regulatory compliance or backup purposes, provided such retained
copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.
```

---

### Example 11 - Destruction Option with Certification
```
Upon termination of this Agreement or upon Discloser's request,
Recipient shall, at Discloser's option:

(a) return all Confidential Information and all copies thereof; or

(b) destroy all Confidential Information and all copies thereof
    and certify such destruction in writing signed by an officer.

Recipient may retain one archival copy solely for legal compliance
purposes, subject to continuing confidentiality obligations.
Confidential Information in electronic backup systems shall be
destroyed in accordance with Recipient's standard backup rotation
procedures.
```

## Ownership and Results

### Example 12 - Customer Ownership of Work Product
```
OWNERSHIP

All results and data generated from use of the Customer's instruments
by or for Customer under this Agreement shall be owned exclusively
by Customer and shall be treated as Customer Confidential Information
hereunder.

Customer may use, disclose and otherwise exploit such results and
data, and products, services and intellectual property based on such
results and data, without restriction and without obligation to Vendor.
```

## Injunctive Relief

### Example 13 - Equitable Relief Provision
```
EQUITABLE RELIEF

Each party acknowledges that a breach of the confidentiality
obligations may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages
would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Discloser shall be entitled
to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific
performance, without the requirement of posting bond or proving
actual damages, in addition to any other remedies available at
law or in equity.
```

## Summary: Key Variations by Position

| Element | Discloser Position | Recipient Position |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Definition | Broad, reasonable person | Strict marking required |
| Standard of Care | Highest degree | Same as own (or reasonable) |
| Survival | 5-7+ years or perpetual | 2-3 years |
| Permitted Disclosure | Named individuals only | Affiliates, advisors broadly |
| Return | Mandatory with certification | Destruction option, archival copy |
| Residuals | No residuals clause | Residuals permitted |
