# Dispute Resolution Clause Examples

## Escalation Procedures

**Example 1 - Multi-Step Escalation**
```
DISPUTE RESOLUTION - ESCALATION

(a) INFORMAL RESOLUTION. The parties shall first attempt to resolve
    any dispute through good faith negotiations between the project
    managers or designated representatives.

(b) MANAGEMENT ESCALATION. If not resolved within [15] days, the
    dispute shall be escalated to each party's senior management
    (VP level or above) who shall meet and confer within [15] days.

(c) EXECUTIVE ESCALATION. If still not resolved, the dispute shall
    be escalated to the CEO or designated executive officer of each
    party, who shall meet within [15] days.

(d) FORMAL DISPUTE RESOLUTION. If the dispute is not resolved after
    completion of the escalation process, either party may proceed
    to [mediation/arbitration/litigation] as provided below.

The escalation process is a condition precedent to formal dispute
resolution, except for claims seeking injunctive relief.
```

## Mediation

**Example 2 - Mandatory Pre-Arbitration Mediation**
```
MEDIATION

Before initiating arbitration, the parties shall attempt to resolve
the dispute through non-binding mediation.

(a) INITIATION. Either party may initiate mediation by written notice
    to the other party describing the dispute.

(b) MEDIATOR. The parties shall select a mutually acceptable mediator
    within [15] days. If unable to agree, a mediator shall be appointed
    by [JAMS/AAA] from its panel of commercial mediators.

(c) PROCEDURE. Mediation shall be conducted in [City] in accordance
    with [JAMS/AAA] Commercial Mediation Rules. The mediation shall
    be completed within [60] days of initiation unless extended by
    mutual agreement.

(d) COSTS. Each party shall bear its own costs and share equally the
    mediator's fees.

(e) CONFIDENTIALITY. All mediation proceedings and communications
    are confidential and inadmissible in subsequent proceedings.

(f) FAILURE. If mediation does not resolve the dispute within [60] days,
    either party may proceed to arbitration.
```

## Arbitration Clauses

### Vendor-Favorable (Streamlined Arbitration)

**Example 3 - Single Arbitrator, Expedited**
```
ARBITRATION

Any dispute not resolved through negotiation shall be finally resolved
by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration
Association ("AAA") under its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

(a) ARBITRATOR. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single
    arbitrator selected in accordance with AAA rules.

(b) LOCATION. The arbitration shall take place in [Vendor's City, State].

(c) PROCEDURE. The arbitrator shall apply the substantive law of
    [State] without regard to conflicts of law. Discovery shall be
    limited to document requests. The arbitrator shall render a
    decision within [90] days of the final hearing.

(d) AWARD. The arbitrator may award monetary damages but may not
    award punitive, consequential, or exemplary damages. The
    arbitrator shall not have authority to modify the Agreement.

(e) CONFIDENTIALITY. The arbitration proceedings and award shall
    be confidential.

(f) COSTS. Each party shall bear its own costs. The arbitrator shall
    allocate AAA fees and arbitrator compensation.

(g) JUDGMENT. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of
    competent jurisdiction.
```

---

### Customer-Favorable (Three Arbitrators, Full Discovery)

**Example 4 - Panel Arbitration with Discovery**
```
ARBITRATION

Any dispute not resolved through mediation shall be finally resolved
by binding arbitration in accordance with the AAA Commercial
Arbitration Rules.

(a) ARBITRATORS. The arbitration shall be conducted by a panel of
    three arbitrators. Each party shall select one arbitrator, and
    the two selected arbitrators shall select the third.

(b) LOCATION. The arbitration shall take place in [Neutral City] or
    as mutually agreed.

(c) DISCOVERY. The parties shall have the right to conduct reasonable
    discovery, including document requests, interrogatories, and
    depositions (up to [3] per side). The arbitrators may expand or
    limit discovery as appropriate.

(d) LAW AND REMEDIES. The arbitrators shall apply the substantive
    law of [State]. The arbitrators may award any remedy available
    at law or equity, including injunctive relief and specific
    performance.

(e) REASONED AWARD. The arbitrators shall issue a reasoned written
    award explaining their decision.

(f) COSTS. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its
    reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party.
```

## Litigation and Jurisdiction

### Example 5 - Exclusive Jurisdiction
```
JURISDICTION AND VENUE

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement that is not
subject to arbitration shall be brought exclusively in the federal
or state courts located in [City, State], and each party irrevocably
submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of such courts.

Each party waives any objection it may have to the laying of venue
in such courts and any claim that such forum is inconvenient.

Service of process may be effected by any means permitted by law.
```

---

### Example 6 - Non-Exclusive Jurisdiction
```
JURISDICTION

Each party consents to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the federal
and state courts located in [City, State] for any action arising out
of this Agreement.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may bring an action in
any court of competent jurisdiction to enforce a judgment or to
seek injunctive or other equitable relief.
```

## Jury Waiver

**Example 7 - Mutual Jury Trial Waiver**
```
WAIVER OF JURY TRIAL

EACH PARTY HEREBY IRREVOCABLY AND UNCONDITIONALLY WAIVES, TO THE
FULLEST EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW, ANY RIGHT IT MAY HAVE
TO A TRIAL BY JURY IN ANY LEGAL PROCEEDING DIRECTLY OR INDIRECTLY
ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE TRANSACTIONS
CONTEMPLATED HEREBY.

EACH PARTY CERTIFIES THAT NO REPRESENTATIVE OF THE OTHER PARTY HAS
REPRESENTED THAT SUCH OTHER PARTY WOULD NOT SEEK TO ENFORCE THIS
WAIVER. EACH PARTY ACKNOWLEDGES THAT IT HAS MADE THIS WAIVER
KNOWINGLY AND VOLUNTARILY.
```

## Carve-Outs for Injunctive Relief

**Example 8 - Injunction Carve-Out**
```
INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

Notwithstanding any provision requiring arbitration or mediation,
either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any
court of competent jurisdiction to:

(a) protect its Confidential Information;
(b) protect its intellectual property rights;
(c) prevent irreparable harm pending resolution of the dispute.

A request for injunctive relief shall not waive the requirement to
arbitrate the underlying dispute. The arbitrators may grant interim
or final injunctive relief.
```

## Class Action Waiver

**Example 9 - Class/Collective Action Waiver**
```
CLASS ACTION WAIVER

All claims and disputes within the scope of this arbitration
agreement must be arbitrated on an individual basis and not on a
class or collective basis. Only individual relief is available.

The arbitrator may not consolidate more than one person's claims
or otherwise preside over any form of class or representative
proceeding.

If this class action waiver is found to be unenforceable, then the
entirety of the arbitration agreement shall be null and void.
```

## Continuing Obligations

**Example 10 - Performance During Dispute**
```
CONTINUATION OF PERFORMANCE

Pending resolution of any dispute, each party shall continue to
perform its obligations under this Agreement in good faith, except:

(a) a party may suspend performance if the dispute relates to
    non-payment and the amount is undisputed;

(b) either party may seek injunctive relief as provided herein.

A party's continued performance shall not waive any claims or rights.
```

## Fee Shifting

**Example 11 - Prevailing Party Fees**
```
ATTORNEYS' FEES

In any action or proceeding to enforce this Agreement or arising out
of a breach hereof, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover
its reasonable attorneys' fees, expert witness fees, and costs from
the non-prevailing party.

"Prevailing party" means the party that achieves substantially the
relief sought, as determined by the court or arbitrator.
```
