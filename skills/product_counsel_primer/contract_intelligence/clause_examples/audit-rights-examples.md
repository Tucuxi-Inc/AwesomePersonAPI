# Audit Rights Clause Examples

## License/Usage Audit

### Licensor-Favorable (Strong Rights)

**Example 1 - Comprehensive License Audit**
```
AUDIT RIGHTS

Licensor may audit Licensee's use of the Software to verify compliance
with the license terms.

(a) FREQUENCY: Audits may be conducted no more than once per [12] month
    period, provided that if an audit reveals material non-compliance,
    Licensor may conduct additional audits to verify remediation.

(b) NOTICE: Licensor shall provide [30] days advance written notice of
    any audit, specifying the scope and proposed dates.

(c) CONDUCT: Audits shall be conducted during normal business hours and
    shall not unreasonably interfere with Licensee's operations.

(d) ACCESS: Licensee shall provide Licensor (or its designated auditor)
    with reasonable access to: (i) facilities where Software is installed;
    (ii) systems and records related to Software usage; (iii) personnel
    with knowledge of usage.

(e) COSTS: Licensor bears audit costs unless the audit reveals underpayment
    exceeding [5%] of amounts due, in which case Licensee bears all
    reasonable audit costs.

(f) UNDERPAYMENT: If audit reveals underpayment, Licensee shall pay:
    (i) the underpayment; (ii) interest at [1.5%] per month from the
    original due date; (iii) Licensor's audit costs if underpayment
    exceeds [5%].
```

### Licensee-Favorable (Limited Rights)

**Example 2 - Constrained Audit Rights**
```
AUDIT RIGHTS

Licensor may audit Licensee's use of the Software under the following
conditions:

(a) LIMITATION: Audits shall be conducted no more than once per calendar
    year and only upon reasonable suspicion of material non-compliance.

(b) AUDITOR: Audits shall be conducted only by an independent third-party
    auditor (not Licensor's employees or competitors), mutually agreed
    upon by the parties.

(c) SCOPE: Audits shall be limited to the specific license metrics in
    dispute and shall not extend to unrelated systems or information.

(d) CONFIDENTIALITY: The auditor shall execute a confidentiality agreement.
    Audit results shall be shared only on a need-to-know basis.

(e) COSTS: Licensor bears all audit costs regardless of findings.
    Licensee is responsible only for underpayments identified.

(f) THRESHOLD: No additional fees are due unless underpayment exceeds
    [10%] of amounts due or $[50,000], whichever is greater.
```

## Security Audit

### Customer-Favorable (Strong Rights)

**Example 3 - Comprehensive Security Audit**
```
SECURITY AUDIT

Customer may audit Vendor's security practices and controls to verify
compliance with the Security Requirements and Data Processing Addendum.

(a) SCOPE: Audits may include review of: policies and procedures;
    technical controls; access logs; incident reports; certifications;
    penetration test results; and personnel security measures.

(b) CONDUCT: Audits may be conducted by Customer's employees or a
    qualified third-party auditor. Vendor shall:
    - Provide access to relevant facilities and personnel;
    - Respond to security questionnaires within [10] business days;
    - Make relevant documentation available for review.

(c) FREQUENCY: Customer may conduct audits upon reasonable notice:
    - Annually as part of regular vendor assessment;
    - Following any Security Incident affecting Customer Data;
    - Upon material changes to Vendor's security practices;
    - As required by Customer's regulators.

(d) COOPERATION: Vendor shall cooperate fully with reasonable audit
    requests. Vendor may not charge Customer for audit cooperation.

(e) REMEDIATION: Vendor shall remediate any material deficiencies
    identified within [30] days and provide evidence of remediation.
```

### Vendor-Favorable (Attestation Alternative)

**Example 4 - Third-Party Attestation**
```
SECURITY VERIFICATION

In lieu of direct audits, Vendor shall provide Customer with:

(a) CERTIFICATIONS: Current copies of:
    - SOC 2 Type II report (annually);
    - ISO 27001 certification (upon certification/renewal);
    - Penetration test executive summary (annually).

(b) QUESTIONNAIRES: Responses to Customer's reasonable security
    questionnaires (SIG, CAIQ, or similar) within [15] business days.

(c) ATTESTATIONS: Written attestation of compliance with the Security
    Requirements signed by Vendor's security officer (annually).

Customer shall accept such third-party attestations as evidence of
compliance with security requirements, except:

(d) INCIDENT TRIGGER: Following a Security Incident affecting Customer
    Data, Customer may conduct a targeted audit of the affected systems
    and controls upon [5] business days notice.

(e) REGULATORY REQUIREMENT: If Customer's regulator requires a direct
    audit, Vendor shall cooperate subject to reasonable confidentiality
    protections and scheduling coordination.
```

## Financial/Royalty Audit

**Example 5 - Revenue-Based Audit**
```
ROYALTY AUDIT

Licensor may audit Licensee's records to verify royalty payments.

(a) RECORDS: Licensee shall maintain accurate books and records of
    all sales, revenue, and usage relevant to royalty calculations
    for [3] years after the applicable period.

(b) ACCESS: Upon [30] days notice, Licensor (or its independent
    accountant) may examine such records during business hours.

(c) FREQUENCY: No more than one audit per year, covering periods not
    previously audited.

(d) UNDERPAYMENT: If audit reveals underpayment:
    - Licensee shall pay underpaid royalties within [30] days;
    - If underpayment exceeds [5%], Licensee pays audit costs;
    - If underpayment exceeds [10%], Licensee pays interest at [12%]
      per annum from original due date.

(e) OVERPAYMENT: If audit reveals overpayment, Licensor shall credit
    the excess against future royalties or refund within [30] days.

(f) CONFIDENTIALITY: Audit information shall be treated as Licensee's
    Confidential Information and used only for verification purposes.
```

## Compliance Audit

**Example 6 - Regulatory Compliance Audit**
```
COMPLIANCE AUDIT

Either party may audit the other's compliance with applicable laws
and the requirements of this Agreement.

(a) SCOPE: Compliance audits may cover:
    - Data protection and privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA);
    - Export control compliance;
    - Anti-corruption and anti-bribery compliance;
    - Industry-specific regulations;
    - Contractual obligations and certifications.

(b) NOTICE: [30] days notice except: (i) [5] days for suspected
    material breach; (ii) no notice for fraud investigation.

(c) COOPERATION: The audited party shall provide reasonable access
    to records, systems, facilities, and personnel.

(d) REGULATOR ACCESS: Each party shall cooperate with reasonable
    requests from the other party's regulators, subject to applicable
    confidentiality protections.

(e) REMEDIATION: Material compliance deficiencies shall be remediated
    within [30] days. Failure to remediate may constitute material
    breach.
```

## Self-Certification Alternative

**Example 7 - Self-Certification**
```
SELF-CERTIFICATION

In lieu of audit, upon Vendor's written request (no more than annually),
Customer shall certify in writing:

(a) The number of Authorized Users accessing the Software;
(b) The number of installations or deployments;
(c) Usage levels for metered components;
(d) Compliance with license restrictions.

False certification constitutes material breach and entitles Vendor to:
(i) terminate the Agreement; (ii) conduct a full audit at Customer's
expense; (iii) pursue all available remedies.

Customer's good faith errors in self-certification that are promptly
corrected upon discovery shall not constitute material breach.
```

## Data Center/Physical Audit

**Example 8 - Facility Inspection**
```
FACILITY INSPECTION

Customer may inspect Vendor's data center facilities upon [30] days
notice, subject to the following:

(a) FREQUENCY: One inspection per year unless a Security Incident
    triggers additional inspection rights.

(b) ESCORT: Inspections shall be escorted by Vendor personnel and
    shall follow Vendor's security protocols.

(c) SCOPE: Inspections may include: physical security measures;
    environmental controls; access logging; disaster recovery
    capabilities; co-location tenant segregation.

(d) RESTRICTIONS: Customer shall not: access other tenants' areas;
    photograph or record sensitive areas; interfere with operations.

(e) VIRTUAL ALTERNATIVE: If physical inspection is impractical,
    Vendor shall provide virtual tours, third-party facility
    assessments, or detailed documentation.
```
