# Payment Terms Clause Examples

## Standard Payment Terms

### Vendor-Favorable (Net 30, Strong Remedies)

**Example 1 - Comprehensive Payment Terms**
```
PAYMENT

Payment terms are net 30 days from date of Vendor's invoice to Customer.

If payment is not received by the due date, Vendor may assess and
Customer agrees to pay a late payment charge at the rate of 1% per
month (12% per year) or the maximum legal rate, whichever is less,
of the amount due from the due date to the date of payment.

If Vendor retains a collection agency and/or attorney to collect unpaid
amounts, Vendor may invoice Customer for, and Customer will pay, all
costs of collection, including without limitation reasonable attorneys'
fees.
```

---

### Customer-Favorable (Net 60, Dispute Rights)

**Example 2 - Customer-Favorable Terms**
```
PAYMENT

Customer shall pay undisputed invoices within sixty (60) days of receipt.
Vendor shall submit invoices with reasonable supporting documentation.

Customer may withhold payment of any amount disputed in good faith
pending resolution. Customer shall notify Vendor of disputed amounts
within [30] days of invoice receipt, specifying the basis for the
dispute. The parties shall work in good faith to resolve disputes.

No late fees shall accrue on disputed amounts during the dispute
resolution period.

Vendor shall not suspend Services or terminate this Agreement for
non-payment unless: (a) the amount is undisputed; (b) Vendor has
provided written notice of non-payment; and (c) [30] days have elapsed
without payment.
```

## Milestone Payments

**Example 3 - Project Milestone Structure**
```
PAYMENT SCHEDULE

Fees shall be paid upon completion and acceptance of milestones:

- Project Initiation:     20% upon SOW execution
- Design Complete:        20% upon design acceptance
- Development Complete:   30% upon UAT commencement
- Final Acceptance:       30% upon final acceptance

Customer shall pay milestone invoices within [30] days of acceptance.
Vendor shall not commence the next phase until prior milestone payment
is received.
```

## Subscription/Recurring Fees

**Example 4 - SaaS Subscription Billing**
```
FEES AND PAYMENT

Subscription Fees. Customer shall pay the fees specified in the Order
Form. Unless otherwise stated:
(a) Fees are invoiced annually in advance;
(b) Fees are non-refundable except as expressly provided;
(c) Payment is due within [30] days of invoice.

Auto-Renewal. Fees for renewal terms will be at Vendor's then-current
rates. Vendor shall provide notice of any fee increase at least [60]
days before the renewal date. Fee increases shall not exceed [7%]
annually without Customer's consent.

Usage Fees. If applicable, usage fees are invoiced monthly in arrears
based on actual usage during the prior month.
```

## Late Payment Provisions

**Example 5 - Interest and Suspension Rights**
```
LATE PAYMENT

Overdue amounts shall accrue interest at the lesser of [1.5%] per month
or the maximum rate permitted by law.

If any undisputed payment is more than [30] days overdue, Vendor may,
upon [10] days written notice:
(a) suspend performance of Services until payment is received;
(b) require advance payment for future Services;
(c) terminate the Agreement for material breach.

Suspension shall not relieve Customer of payment obligations and shall
not constitute a breach by Vendor.
```

## Taxes

**Example 6 - Comprehensive Tax Provision**
```
TAXES

All fees are exclusive of taxes. Customer shall pay all sales, use,
value-added, withholding, and other taxes arising from this Agreement,
except for taxes based on Vendor's net income.

If Customer is required to withhold taxes, Customer shall:
(a) deduct such taxes from payment;
(b) pay the withheld amount to the appropriate authority;
(c) provide Vendor with official receipts or other evidence.

Customer shall provide valid tax exemption certificates upon request.
If an exemption is later disallowed, Customer shall pay the resulting
tax plus any interest or penalties.
```

## Price Adjustments

**Example 7 - Annual Price Increase**
```
PRICE ADJUSTMENTS

Vendor may increase fees annually upon [60] days prior written notice.
Increases shall not exceed the greater of: (a) [5%]; or (b) the
increase in the Consumer Price Index for the prior year.

Customer may reject any price increase by providing notice within
[30] days, in which case: (a) the existing fees remain in effect
through the end of the then-current term; and (b) either party may
elect not to renew.
```

## Refunds and Credits

**Example 8 - Pro-Rata Refund**
```
REFUNDS

Upon termination by Customer for Vendor's material breach, Vendor
shall refund to Customer the pro-rata portion of any pre-paid fees
attributable to the period after the effective date of termination.

Upon termination by Vendor for Customer's breach, no refund shall be
due and all amounts shall become immediately due and payable.

Upon termination for convenience by Customer, no refund shall be due
for Services already rendered. [Alternative: a pro-rata refund shall
be provided less a termination fee of [X%].]
```
