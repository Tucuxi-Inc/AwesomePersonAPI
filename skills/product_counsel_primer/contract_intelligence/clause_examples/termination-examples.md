# Termination Clause Examples

## Overview

This skill provides real clause language examples for termination provisions extracted from actual contracts. Examples are organized by termination type and party position.

## Term and Renewal

### Auto-Renewal with Notice

**Example 1 - Standard Auto-Renewal**
```
TERM

Subject to the right of the parties to terminate this Agreement, the
initial term of this Agreement shall be three (3) years.

The term of this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive
periods of two (2) years unless the Agreement is terminated as set
forth herein.

Customer may elect to add or delete certain services from this
Agreement by providing Vendor with notice of such additions or
deletions at least thirty (30) days prior to the end of the then
current term, provided any such addition shall be subject to Vendor's
consent.
```

---

### Example 2 - SaaS Subscription Term
```
SUBSCRIPTION TERM

The initial Subscription Term begins on the Effective Date and
continues for the period specified in the Order Form.

Unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least
[60] days before the end of the then-current term, the Subscription
Term will automatically renew for successive periods equal to the
initial term (or one year, if shorter).

Fees for renewal terms will be at Vendor's then-current rates,
provided that any fee increase shall not exceed [5%] over the
prior term without Customer's consent.
```

## Termination for Convenience

### Vendor-Favorable (Limited Convenience)

**Example 3 - Notice-Based Termination**
```
TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE

Customer may terminate this Agreement or any Service for any or no
reason upon thirty (30) days prior written notice to Vendor.

Vendor may terminate this Agreement upon [90] days prior written
notice to Customer.

Upon termination by Customer for convenience, Customer shall pay
Vendor: (a) all fees for Services performed through the effective
date of termination; (b) all non-cancelable expenses incurred by
Vendor; and (c) a termination fee equal to [50%] of the remaining
fees that would have been due through the end of the then-current
term.
```

**Vendor Protection Elements:**
- Customer can terminate but must pay termination fee
- Vendor has longer notice period
- Full payment for work performed
- Recovery of committed expenses

---

### Customer-Favorable

**Example 4 - Unrestricted Convenience Termination**
```
TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE

Either party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW at any time
for any reason or no reason upon [30] days written notice.

Upon termination for convenience:
(a) Vendor shall cease work and deliver all work product completed
    to date;
(b) Customer shall pay only for Services actually performed through
    the effective date of termination;
(c) No termination fee, penalty, or wind-down charge shall apply;
(d) Any prepaid fees for Services not yet performed shall be refunded
    within [30] days.
```

## Termination for Cause

### Balanced Position

**Example 5 - Mutual Termination for Cause**
```
TERMINATION FOR CAUSE

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written
notice if the other party:

(a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach
    within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying
    the breach in reasonable detail;

(b) becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of
    creditors, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy,
    or has a receiver or trustee appointed for substantially all of
    its assets, and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty
    (60) days;

(c) ceases to conduct business in the normal course.

Termination for cause shall not limit either party's right to pursue
any other remedies available at law or in equity, including recovery
of damages.
```

---

### Example 6 - Detailed Cause Definition
```
TERMINATION FOR CAUSE

Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice
if Customer:

(i) transfers any covered instruments to another location not covered
    by Vendor's service area;

(ii) causes defects or necessitates repairs through causes excluded
     from coverage, and Vendor reasonably believes it will be unable
     to repair such defects;

(iii) fails to pay any undisputed amount when due and such failure
      continues for [30] days after written notice;

(iv) uses the Services or Products in violation of applicable law
     or this Agreement.

Customer may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice
if Vendor:

(i) materially breaches any representation, warranty, or covenant
    and fails to cure within [30] days of notice;

(ii) fails to perform Services in accordance with the Service Levels
     for [3] consecutive months;

(iii) is acquired by or merges with a direct competitor of Customer;

(iv) discontinues the Product line without providing [12] months notice.
```

## Termination for Insolvency

### Example 7 - Comprehensive Insolvency Trigger
```
INSOLVENCY

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon notice
if the other party:

(a) files a voluntary petition in bankruptcy or is adjudicated
    bankrupt or insolvent;

(b) files any petition or answer seeking reorganization, arrangement,
    composition, readjustment, liquidation, dissolution, or similar
    relief under any present or future law;

(c) seeks, consents to, or acquiesces in the appointment of any
    trustee, receiver, custodian, or liquidator;

(d) makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors;

(e) admits in writing its inability to pay debts as they become due;

(f) has any proceeding instituted against it seeking any of the
    foregoing and such proceeding is not dismissed within [60] days.
```

## Effects of Termination

### Example 8 - Comprehensive Effects
```
EFFECTS OF TERMINATION

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) PAYMENT. Customer shall pay all amounts due for Services
    performed through the termination date. If Customer terminates
    for Vendor's material breach, Customer may withhold payment for
    disputed amounts pending resolution.

(b) WORK PRODUCT. Vendor shall deliver to Customer all completed
    and in-progress work product for which Customer has paid.

(c) TRANSITION. Upon Customer's request, Vendor shall provide
    reasonable transition assistance for up to [90] days at Vendor's
    then-current rates.

(d) DATA. Vendor shall, at Customer's option, return or destroy all
    Customer Data within [30] days and certify such return or
    destruction in writing.

(e) CONFIDENTIALITY. Each party shall return or destroy the other
    party's Confidential Information and certify compliance.

(f) ACCESS. Customer's access to the Services shall terminate on
    the effective date of termination, except that Vendor shall
    provide read-only access for [30] days for data extraction.

(g) LICENSES. All licenses granted hereunder shall terminate,
    except that Customer's license to use Deliverables for which
    Customer has paid shall survive.
```

### Example 9 - Service Agreement Wind-Down
```
TERMINATION CONSEQUENCES

Upon termination by Customer prior to the end of a term:

(a) Vendor shall calculate at its sole discretion the total price
    of services actually performed and expenses actually and
    reasonably incurred from the effective date until the
    Termination Date;

(b) Customer's total payment obligation shall equal: (1) the amount
    calculated above; or (2) the prorated price from the effective
    date until the Termination Date, whichever is greater, not to
    exceed the total amount paid;

(c) Any payments made by Customer in excess of this amount shall be
    credited to Customer's account within thirty (30) days toward
    future purchases;

(d) Any unpaid portion shall be immediately due upon Customer's
    receipt of an invoice from Vendor.

Upon termination by Vendor:

Vendor will credit or refund to Customer one hundred percent (100%)
of the fee paid by Customer, provided that if the term is more than
one year, Vendor will refund the amount paid for all periods after
the most recent anniversary date.
```

## Survival Provisions

### Example 10 - Explicit Survival
```
SURVIVAL

The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration of
this Agreement:

(a) Section [X] (Definitions) - to the extent necessary to interpret
    surviving provisions;

(b) Section [X] (Confidentiality) - for [5] years following
    termination or perpetually for trade secrets;

(c) Section [X] (Intellectual Property) - perpetually;

(d) Section [X] (Indemnification) - for claims arising prior to
    termination or from surviving obligations;

(e) Section [X] (Limitation of Liability) - perpetually;

(f) Section [X] (Warranties) - for the applicable warranty period;

(g) Section [X] (Payment) - until all amounts due are paid;

(h) Section [X] (Dispute Resolution) - perpetually;

(i) Section [X] (General Provisions) - perpetually.

Additionally, any provision that by its nature should survive
termination shall so survive.
```

---

### Example 11 - General Survival Clause
```
SURVIVAL

Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect any
rights, obligations, or liabilities of either party that:

(a) accrued prior to such termination or expiration;

(b) are expressly stated to survive termination;

(c) by their nature should survive termination, including without
    limitation confidentiality, indemnification, limitation of
    liability, intellectual property ownership, and dispute
    resolution provisions.
```

## Termination Assistance

### Example 12 - Detailed Transition Services
```
TERMINATION ASSISTANCE

Upon termination or expiration, or during the [180] days prior to
expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable termination assistance
to enable Customer to transition to a successor provider, including:

(a) continuing to provide the Services at the rates then in effect;

(b) providing documentation and knowledge transfer to Customer or
    its successor;

(c) extracting and delivering Customer Data in a standard,
    machine-readable format;

(d) cooperating in the orderly transfer of responsibilities;

(e) maintaining systems and data during the transition period.

Termination assistance shall be provided for up to [6] months at
Vendor's then-current rates. Vendor shall not withhold termination
assistance due to any dispute between the parties.
```

## Summary: Termination Elements by Position

| Element | Vendor Position | Customer Position |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Convenience | Customer pays termination fee | No penalty, pro-rata refund |
| Cure Period | Long cure (60+ days) | Short cure (15-30 days) |
| Notice | Short notice for vendor | Long notice for vendor |
| Effects | Limited refund | Full refund of prepaid fees |
| Transition | At vendor's rates | Included or at cost |
| Data | Standard deletion | Export assistance required |
| Survival | Limited survival | Broad survival |
