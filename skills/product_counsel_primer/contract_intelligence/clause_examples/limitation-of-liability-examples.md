# Limitation of Liability Clause Examples

## Overview

This skill provides real clause language examples for limitation of liability provisions extracted from actual contracts. Examples are organized by component (consequential exclusion, cap, carve-outs) and party position.

## Consequential Damages Exclusion

### Vendor-Favorable (Broad Exclusion)

**Example 1 - Comprehensive Mutual Exclusion**
```
EXCEPT FOR [PARTY A'S] OBLIGATIONS IN SECTIONS [INDEMNIFICATION] AND
FOR DAMAGES ARISING FROM THE GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT
OF [PARTY A], IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE RESPONSIBLE OR LIABLE,
WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT, WARRANTY OR UNDER ANY STATUTE OR ON ANY
OTHER BASIS, FOR SPECIAL, INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, MULTIPLE, PUNITIVE OR
CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR IN CONNECTION WITH THE
PERFORMANCE OR FAILURE TO PERFORM SERVICES OR OTHERWISE, EVEN IF
ADVISED IN ADVANCE OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.
```

**Key Elements:**
- Mutual exclusion (both parties protected)
- Covers all legal theories (contract, tort, warranty, statute)
- Lists all damage types (special, indirect, incidental, punitive, consequential)
- "Even if advised" language closes foreseeability argument
- Carve-out for indemnification and gross negligence/willful misconduct

---

**Example 2 - Enumerated Exclusions**
```
IN NO EVENT WILL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT,
SPECIAL, INCIDENTAL, EXEMPLARY, PUNITIVE OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES
(INCLUDING, BUT NOT LIMITED TO, DAMAGES FOR LOSS OF BUSINESS PROFITS,
BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, LOSS OF BUSINESS INFORMATION, COST OF COVER,
OR ANY OTHER PECUNIARY LOSS), REGARDLESS OF THE FORM OF ACTION,
WHETHER IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE) OR OTHERWISE,
EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.
```

**Why Enumeration Matters:** Specifically listing excluded damage types (lost profits, business interruption, etc.) provides clarity and reduces disputes about what's covered.

---

### Balanced Position

**Example 3 - Mutual with Specific Carve-Outs**
```
NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED
TO THIS AGREEMENT, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS
OF PROFITS, LOSS OF BUSINESS, OR LOSS OF DATA, EVEN IF A PARTY HAS
BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

THE FOREGOING LIMITATIONS SHALL NOT APPLY TO:
(A) EITHER PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS;
(B) EITHER PARTY'S BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY;
(C) CUSTOMER'S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS;
(D) DAMAGES ARISING FROM GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT.
```

**Balance Elements:** Mutual protection with reasonable carve-outs that protect both parties' core interests.

---

### Customer-Favorable Position

**Example 4 - Vendor-Only Exclusion with Customer Carve-Outs**
```
VENDOR SHALL NOT BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, SPECIAL, INCIDENTAL, OR
CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF THIS AGREEMENT OR THE USE OF
THE PRODUCTS OR SERVICES, EXCEPT THAT THIS LIMITATION SHALL NOT APPLY
TO DAMAGES ARISING FROM:

(A) VENDOR'S BREACH OF ITS CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS;
(B) VENDOR'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS;
(C) VENDOR'S GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT;
(D) PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH CAUSED BY VENDOR;
(E) VENDOR'S INFRINGEMENT OF CUSTOMER'S INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY.

CUSTOMER SHALL REMAIN LIABLE FOR ALL DAMAGES RESULTING FROM ITS
BREACH OF THIS AGREEMENT.
```

**Customer-Favorable Elements:**
- Exclusion applies only to Vendor (not Customer)
- Multiple carve-outs exposing Vendor to consequential damages
- Customer retains full recovery rights for key concerns

## Liability Caps

### Vendor-Favorable (Low Cap)

**Example 5 - Fees-Based Cap**
```
WITHOUT LIMITING THE FOREGOING, EXCEPT SOLELY FOR ANY PAYMENTS MADE
UNDER VENDOR'S INDEMNITY, OR FOR DAMAGES ARISING FROM THE GROSS
NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT OF VENDOR, VENDOR'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE
LIABILITY IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT, INCLUDING WITHOUT
LIMITATION SERVICES RENDERED THEREUNDER, IN CONTRACT, TORT, WARRANTY
OR OTHERWISE, WILL NOT EXCEED TWICE THE AMOUNT OF FEES PAID TO VENDOR
FOR THE UNDERLYING SERVICES.
```

**Common Cap Formulas:**
- Fees paid (vendor-favorable)
- 12 months fees (moderate)
- 24 months fees or 2x fees (balanced)
- Total contract value (customer-favorable)
- Higher multiple or uncapped for critical obligations

---

**Example 6 - Fixed Dollar Cap**
```
VENDOR'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED
ONE MILLION DOLLARS ($1,000,000) IN THE AGGREGATE.
```

**When to Use Fixed Caps:**
- Large enterprise agreements
- When fees don't reflect risk exposure
- Government contracts
- When insurance limits dictate

---

### Balanced Position

**Example 7 - Tiered Cap Structure**
```
LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

(a) General Cap. Subject to subsection (b), each party's total
    aggregate liability arising out of or related to this Agreement
    shall not exceed the greater of (i) the fees paid or payable
    under this Agreement during the twelve (12) months preceding
    the claim, or (ii) $500,000.

(b) Elevated Cap. The following shall be subject to a cap equal to
    two (2) times the amount in subsection (a):
    (i)   Breach of confidentiality obligations
    (ii)  Data breach involving Customer Data
    (iii) Indemnification for third-party IP claims

(c) Uncapped. The following shall not be subject to any cap:
    (i)   Fraud or intentional misconduct
    (ii)  Indemnification for bodily injury or death
    (iii) Gross negligence or willful misconduct
    (iv)  Customer's payment obligations
```

**Why Tiered Caps Work:**
- Acknowledges different risks warrant different exposure
- Provides vendor certainty on most claims
- Gives customer meaningful recovery for serious breaches

---

### Customer-Favorable Position

**Example 8 - High Cap with Broad Carve-Outs**
```
EXCEPT AS PROVIDED BELOW, NEITHER PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY SHALL EXCEED
THREE (3) TIMES THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THIS AGREEMENT.

THE FOREGOING LIMITATION SHALL NOT APPLY TO:
(A) VENDOR'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS;
(B) VENDOR'S BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY OR DATA PROTECTION OBLIGATIONS;
(C) VENDOR'S INFRINGEMENT OF CUSTOMER'S INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY;
(D) FRAUD, GROSS NEGLIGENCE, OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT;
(E) PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH;
(F) VENDOR'S VIOLATION OF APPLICABLE LAW.
```

## Warranty Limitations

### Example 9 - Limited Warranty Remedy
```
VENDOR WARRANTS THAT IT WILL PROVIDE ITS SERVICES AT LEAST IN
ACCORDANCE WITH GENERALLY ACCEPTED STANDARDS PREVAILING IN THE
INSTRUMENT REPAIR INDUSTRY AT THE TIME AND PLACE PERFORMED.

WARRANTY CLAIMS MUST BE MADE WITHIN NINETY (90) DAYS AFTER SERVICES
ARE PERFORMED.

VENDOR MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES OF ANY KIND WHATSOEVER, EXPRESS OR
IMPLIED, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO WARRANTIES OF FITNESS FOR A
PARTICULAR PURPOSE OR MERCHANTABILITY WITH RESPECT TO ITS SERVICES,
WHICH WARRANTIES ARE EXPRESSLY DISCLAIMED.

VENDOR'S SOLE LIABILITY AND RESPONSIBILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT FOR
BREACH OF WARRANTY IS RE-PERFORMANCE OF THE SERVICES WITHIN A
REASONABLE TIME OR RETURN OF THE FEE PAID FOR THE DEFECTIVE SERVICES
AT VENDOR'S OPTION. THESE ARE CUSTOMER'S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE REMEDIES
FOR ANY BREACH OF WARRANTY.
```

**Vendor Protection Elements:**
- Limited warranty standard (industry practice)
- Short claim window (90 days)
- Express disclaimer of implied warranties
- Exclusive remedy (re-performance or refund)
- Vendor chooses the remedy

## All Caps Requirement

### Example 10 - UCC-Compliant Disclaimer
```
THE WARRANTIES SET FORTH IN THIS SECTION ARE THE SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE
WARRANTIES GIVEN BY VENDOR. VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES,
EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION THE IMPLIED WARRANTIES
OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, TITLE, AND
NON-INFRINGEMENT. NO ORAL OR WRITTEN INFORMATION OR ADVICE GIVEN BY
VENDOR SHALL CREATE A WARRANTY OR IN ANY WAY INCREASE THE SCOPE OF
THIS WARRANTY.

SOME JURISDICTIONS DO NOT ALLOW THE EXCLUSION OF IMPLIED WARRANTIES,
SO THE ABOVE EXCLUSION MAY NOT APPLY TO CUSTOMER. IN THAT EVENT, SUCH
IMPLIED WARRANTIES ARE LIMITED IN DURATION TO THE WARRANTY PERIOD.
```

**Why ALL CAPS:**
- UCC Section 2-316 requires disclaimers to be "conspicuous"
- ALL CAPS is the standard way to meet this requirement
- Failure to use conspicuous language may invalidate disclaimer

## Combined Limitation Provisions

### Example 11 - Complete Limitation Section
```
LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

(a) EXCLUSION OF DAMAGES. IN NO EVENT WILL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE
    FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE
    DAMAGES, OR DAMAGES FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST DATA, BUSINESS
    INTERRUPTION, OR COST OF COVER, ARISING OUT OF OR IN CONNECTION
    WITH THIS AGREEMENT, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR THE
    THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF
    SUCH DAMAGES.

(b) LIABILITY CAP. EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (c), EACH PARTY'S
    TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS
    AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE TO VENDOR
    DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE
    TO LIABILITY.

(c) EXCEPTIONS. THE LIMITATIONS IN SUBSECTIONS (a) AND (b) SHALL
    NOT APPLY TO:
    (i)   A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION [X];
    (ii)  A PARTY'S BREACH OF ITS CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS;
    (iii) A PARTY'S GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT;
    (iv)  CUSTOMER'S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS;
    (v)   PERSONAL INJURY CAUSED BY A PARTY'S NEGLIGENCE.

(d) ALLOCATION OF RISK. THE PARTIES ACKNOWLEDGE THAT THE LIMITATIONS
    OF LIABILITY IN THIS SECTION REFLECT THE ALLOCATION OF RISK
    BETWEEN THE PARTIES AND THAT THE FEES CHARGED REFLECT THIS
    ALLOCATION. THE PARTIES WOULD NOT ENTER INTO THIS AGREEMENT
    WITHOUT THESE LIMITATIONS.
```

**Best Practice Elements:**
- Clear structure (exclusion, cap, exceptions)
- Mutual application
- Reasonable carve-outs
- Risk allocation statement (aids enforceability)

## Summary: Cap Benchmarks by Risk Level

| Risk Level | Cap Formula | When Appropriate |
|------------|-------------|------------------|
| Low Risk | 12 months fees | Commodity services, low data sensitivity |
| Standard | 2x annual fees | Most B2B SaaS, professional services |
| Elevated | 3x fees or $5M+ | Data-intensive, mission-critical |
| High Risk | Total contract value or higher | Healthcare, financial services |
| Uncapped | N/A (carve-outs) | Fraud, willful misconduct, bodily injury |
