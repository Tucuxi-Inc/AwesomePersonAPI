# Non-Compete and Restrictive Covenant Clause Examples

## Non-Competition (Employment Context)

### Employer-Favorable (Broad)

**Example 1 - Comprehensive Non-Compete**
```
NON-COMPETITION

During employment and for [18] months following termination for any
reason (the "Restricted Period"), Employee shall not, directly or
indirectly, for Employee's own account or for the benefit of any other:

(a) Engage in, own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, provide
    services to, or have a financial interest in any Competing Business
    anywhere within the Territory;

(b) Assist any person or entity in competing with the Company in any
    manner described above;

(c) Develop, design, produce, market, sell, or distribute any products
    or services competitive with those of the Company.

"Competing Business" means any business that develops, manufactures,
markets, or sells products or services that are substantially similar
to or competitive with the Company's products or services.

"Territory" means the United States and any other country in which the
Company conducts business as of the date of termination.

Exceptions: Employee may own up to [2%] of any publicly traded company.
```

---

### Employee-Favorable (Narrow)

**Example 2 - Limited Non-Compete**
```
NON-COMPETITION

During employment and for [6] months following termination, Employee
shall not work as a [specific role title] for a Direct Competitor
listed in Exhibit A within [City/State].

"Direct Competitor" means an entity deriving [50%] or more of its
revenue from [specific product/service category].

This restriction does not prevent Employee from:
(a) Working for a non-competing division of a large company that also
    has a competing division;
(b) Providing services in a role unrelated to Employee's position at
    Company;
(c) Starting a business that does not directly compete.

Company agrees to pay Employee [6] months base salary as consideration
for compliance with this restriction following termination without Cause.
```

## Non-Solicitation of Customers

**Example 3 - Customer Non-Solicitation**
```
NON-SOLICITATION OF CUSTOMERS

During the Restricted Period, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Solicit, divert, or take away any Customer for the purpose of
    providing products or services competitive with those of the Company;

(b) Encourage or induce any Customer to terminate or reduce its business
    relationship with the Company;

(c) Accept business from any Customer if such business competes with the
    Company's business.

"Customer" means any person or entity that: (i) was a customer of the
Company during the [24] months preceding termination; and (ii) with whom
Employee had direct contact, supervised personnel who had contact, or
about whom Employee received confidential information during employment.

General advertising and responses to unsolicited inquiries are not
prohibited.
```

## Non-Solicitation of Employees

**Example 4 - Employee Non-Solicitation**
```
NON-SOLICITATION OF EMPLOYEES

During employment and for [12] months following termination, neither
party shall, directly or indirectly:

(a) Solicit, recruit, or hire any employee of the other party who was
    involved in the performance of this Agreement;

(b) Encourage or induce any such employee to leave employment with the
    other party.

This restriction does not apply to:
(a) General public advertisements not targeted at the other party's
    employees;
(b) Unsolicited applications or inquiries;
(c) Employees who have been terminated by the other party for at least
    [6] months;
(d) Hiring of employees through a recruiting firm acting independently
    without direction to target the other party's employees.
```

## Exclusivity (Commercial Context)

**Example 5 - Exclusive Dealing**
```
EXCLUSIVITY

(a) SUPPLIER EXCLUSIVITY: During the term, Supplier shall not, directly
    or indirectly, sell, license, or distribute the Products to any
    third party within the Territory except through Distributor.

(b) DISTRIBUTOR EXCLUSIVITY: During the term, Distributor shall not,
    directly or indirectly, distribute, resell, or promote any products
    that compete with the Products within the Territory.

(c) EXCEPTIONS: The exclusivity restrictions do not apply to:
    (i) Sales made prior to the Effective Date;
    (ii) Sales to customers specifically listed in Exhibit [X];
    (iii) Products outside the defined Product categories.

(d) TERMINATION OF EXCLUSIVITY: If Distributor fails to meet the
    Minimum Purchase Commitment for [2] consecutive quarters, Supplier
    may convert this Agreement to non-exclusive upon [90] days notice.
```

## M&A Context (Seller Non-Compete)

**Example 6 - Seller Non-Compete (Strong)**
```
SELLER NON-COMPETE

For [5] years following the Closing Date, Seller and each of its
principals (individually and collectively, "Restricted Parties")
shall not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Engage in, own, manage, operate, control, or provide services to
    any business that competes with the Business as conducted at Closing
    anywhere within the Territory;

(b) Solicit any customers, suppliers, or employees of the Business;

(c) Disparage the Business or Buyer in any manner.

This covenant is a material inducement to Buyer and is supported by
adequate consideration (the Purchase Price). The parties agree this
covenant is reasonable in scope, duration, and geography.

The Restricted Period shall be extended by any period during which a
Restricted Party is in breach of this covenant.
```

## Partnership/JV Context

**Example 7 - Partner Non-Compete**
```
PARTNER NON-COMPETE

During the term and for [2] years following dissolution, no Partner
shall:

(a) Engage in a business competitive with the Partnership within the
    Territory;

(b) Solicit the Partnership's customers;

(c) Hire Partnership employees;

(d) Use Partnership Confidential Information to compete.

Partners may engage in businesses outside the Partnership's defined
scope provided such activities do not materially interfere with Partner's
obligations to the Partnership or create a conflict of interest.
```

## Enforceability Provisions

**Example 8 - Blue Pencil / Reformation**
```
ENFORCEABILITY

The parties agree that the scope, duration, and geographic limitations
of these restrictions are reasonable and necessary to protect legitimate
business interests, including Confidential Information, customer
relationships, and goodwill.

If any court or arbitrator determines that any restriction is
unenforceable as written, the parties authorize and request the court
or arbitrator to reform such provision to the maximum extent enforceable,
and as reformed, such provision shall remain in full force and effect.

If reformation is not permitted under applicable law, the unenforceable
provision shall be severed without affecting the enforceability of the
remaining provisions.
```

---

**Example 9 - Injunctive Relief**
```
INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

Employee acknowledges that a breach of the restrictive covenants in
this Agreement would cause immediate, irreparable harm to the Company
for which monetary damages would be inadequate.

Accordingly, the Company shall be entitled to seek temporary,
preliminary, and permanent injunctive relief, specific performance,
and other equitable remedies to enforce these covenants, without the
requirement of posting bond or proving actual damages, in addition to
any other remedies available at law or in equity.

The Company's pursuit of injunctive relief shall not waive its right
to seek monetary damages.
```

## Consideration

**Example 10 - Garden Leave**
```
CONSIDERATION FOR NON-COMPETE

In consideration of the non-compete obligations:

(a) If Employee voluntarily resigns, no additional consideration is
    required (the non-compete is supported by continued employment
    and compensation during employment);

(b) If Company terminates Employee without Cause, Company shall continue
    to pay Employee's base salary during the Restricted Period ("Garden
    Leave Pay");

(c) If Company terminates Employee for Cause, the Restricted Period
    shall be reduced to [6] months and no Garden Leave Pay is required;

(d) Employee may shorten or eliminate the Restricted Period by refunding
    the pro-rata portion of Garden Leave Pay.
```

## State-Specific Carve-Out

**Example 11 - California Exception**
```
CALIFORNIA EMPLOYEES

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, the
non-competition provisions of this Section shall not apply to
employees who are residents of California or whose primary work
location is in California, to the extent such provisions are
unenforceable under California Business & Professions Code § 16600.

The non-solicitation provisions shall apply to the extent permitted
by California law.

All other provisions of this Agreement remain in full force and effect.
```
