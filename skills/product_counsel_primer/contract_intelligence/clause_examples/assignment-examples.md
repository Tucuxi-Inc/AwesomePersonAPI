# Assignment and Change of Control Clause Examples

## Standard Assignment Restrictions

### Mutual Anti-Assignment

**Example 1 - Standard Mutual Restriction**
```
ASSIGNMENT

Neither party may transfer or assign any of its rights or delegate
any of its obligations, in whole or in part, whether voluntarily or
by operation of law, without the prior written consent of the other
party, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

Any purported transfer, assignment, or delegation by either party
without such prior written consent shall be null and void ab initio
and of no force or effect.
```

---

### Vendor-Favorable (Asymmetric)

**Example 2 - Vendor Free Assignment**
```
ASSIGNMENT

Customer may not assign this Agreement or any rights hereunder without
Vendor's prior written consent, which may be withheld in Vendor's sole
discretion.

Vendor may assign this Agreement without restriction, including to an
affiliate, successor, or acquirer. Vendor shall notify Customer of any
such assignment.

Any attempted assignment by Customer without consent shall be void.
```

---

### Customer-Favorable (Asymmetric)

**Example 3 - Customer-Favorable Assignment**
```
ASSIGNMENT

Vendor may not assign this Agreement without Customer's prior written
consent.

Customer may assign this Agreement without consent to:
(a) an Affiliate;
(b) a successor by merger, consolidation, or acquisition;
(c) a purchaser of all or substantially all of Customer's assets or
    the business unit to which this Agreement relates.

Vendor's obligations shall bind any successor or assignee.
```

## Corporate Transaction Exception

**Example 4 - M&A Carve-Out**
```
ASSIGNMENT

Notwithstanding the assignment restrictions above, either party may,
upon written notice to the other party, assign this Agreement and its
rights and obligations to any successor of such party by way of merger,
consolidation, or the acquisition of all or substantially all of such
party's assets or business to which this Agreement relates, provided
the assignee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

PROVIDED, HOWEVER, that Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately
if Customer's successor:
(i) transfers any covered instruments to a location not serviced by
    Vendor; or
(ii) transfers instruments without Vendor's assistance and fails to
     pay Vendor's then-current reinstallation fees.
```

## Change of Control Provisions

**Example 5 - Detailed Change of Control Definition**
```
CHANGE OF CONTROL

"Change of Control" means:
(a) a sale, transfer, or disposition of all or substantially all of
    a party's assets;
(b) a merger, consolidation, or reorganization in which the party is
    not the surviving entity or in which its equity holders before
    the transaction hold less than [50%] of voting power after;
(c) a sale, transfer, or disposition of more than [50%] of voting
    securities; or
(d) a change in board composition such that individuals who were
    directors at the beginning of any 12-month period cease to
    constitute a majority.

Upon a Change of Control:
(a) The party shall provide written notice within [10] days;
(b) The other party may terminate this Agreement upon [30] days notice
    if the acquirer is a direct competitor;
(c) Fees and terms shall remain unchanged through the current term.
```

## Competitor Restrictions

**Example 6 - Competitor Change of Control**
```
COMPETITOR ACQUISITION

If Vendor undergoes a Change of Control in which the acquiring entity
is a direct competitor of Customer, Customer may:

(a) Terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice;

(b) Receive a pro-rata refund of pre-paid fees;

(c) Require Vendor to demonstrate that Customer's Confidential
    Information will not be accessible to the acquiring entity;

(d) Require return or certified destruction of all Customer
    Confidential Information.

"Direct competitor" means an entity that derives [25%] or more of its
revenue from products or services that compete with Customer's primary
products or services.
```

## Successors and Assigns

**Example 7 - Binding Effect**
```
SUCCESSORS AND ASSIGNS

This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the
parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns.

No assignment shall relieve the assigning party of its obligations
unless the non-assigning party agrees in writing to a novation
releasing the assignor.

Any assignee or successor shall be bound by all terms and conditions
of this Agreement and shall assume all obligations of the assigning
party.
```

## Consent Standards

**Example 8 - Consent Not Unreasonably Withheld**
```
ASSIGNMENT CONSENT

Consent to assignment shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned,
or delayed.

Consent may be withheld if the proposed assignee:
(a) lacks financial capability to perform the assigning party's
    obligations;
(b) is a direct competitor of the non-assigning party;
(c) has a material adverse reputation in the industry;
(d) is subject to sanctions or export restrictions.

If the non-assigning party does not respond to a written consent
request within [30] days, consent shall be deemed granted.
```

## Financing Carve-Out

**Example 9 - Assignment for Financing**
```
FINANCING ASSIGNMENT

Notwithstanding the assignment restrictions, Customer may assign its
rights (but not obligations) under this Agreement to a financing
source as collateral security, provided:

(a) Vendor's rights and obligations are not affected;
(b) Customer provides Vendor with written notice;
(c) The financing source agrees to be bound by this Agreement's
    confidentiality provisions.

Such assignment shall not relieve Customer of any obligations.
```

## Government Contract Novation

**Example 10 - Government Contractor Assignment**
```
GOVERNMENT CONTRACTOR NOVATION

Vendor acknowledges that Customer is a federal government contractor.

Upon Customer's request in connection with a change of ownership or
reorganization, Vendor shall:
(a) Execute a novation agreement in a form required by applicable
    government regulations (FAR/DFARS);
(b) Cooperate with Customer in obtaining government approval;
(c) Provide information reasonably required for the novation process.

Vendor shall not unreasonably withhold or delay its cooperation.
```

## Partial Assignment

**Example 11 - Limited Assignment Rights**
```
PARTIAL ASSIGNMENT

(a) PAYMENT RIGHTS. Either party may assign its right to receive
    payment under this Agreement without consent, provided notice
    is given and the assignment does not affect the other party's
    payment obligations or rights.

(b) SERVICE OBLIGATIONS. Vendor may not delegate its service
    obligations without Customer's prior consent.

(c) LICENSE RIGHTS. Customer may not sublicense its rights to the
    Software except as expressly permitted in the license terms.
```
