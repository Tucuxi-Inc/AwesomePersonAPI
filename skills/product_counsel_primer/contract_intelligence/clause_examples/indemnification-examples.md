# Indemnification Clause Examples

## Overview

This skill provides real clause language examples for indemnification provisions extracted from actual contracts. Examples are organized by indemnification type and party position (vendor-favorable, balanced, customer-favorable).

## IP Indemnification

### Vendor/Licensor Position (Standard Protection)

**Example 1 - Services IP Indemnity (Vendor-Favorable)**
```
IP INDEMNITY FOR SERVICES. Vendor shall (i) defend Customer against any
third party claim that the Services and the related materials provided by
Vendor to Customer infringe a patent, or a copyright enforceable in a
country that is a signatory to the Berne Convention, and (ii) pay the
resulting costs and damages finally awarded against Customer by a court
of competent jurisdiction or the amounts stated in a written settlement
signed by Vendor.

The foregoing obligations are subject to the following: Customer
(a) notifies Vendor promptly in writing of such claim, (b) grants Vendor
sole control over the defense and settlement thereof, (c) reasonably
cooperates in response to a Vendor request for assistance, and (d) is not
in material breach of this Agreement.

Should such a claim be made, or in Vendor's opinion be likely to be made,
Vendor may, at its option and expense, (1) procure for Customer the right
to make continued use thereof, (2) replace or modify such so that it
becomes non-infringing, (3) request return of the subject material, or
(4) discontinue the Service and refund the portion of any pre-paid Service
fee that corresponds to the period of Service discontinuation.
```

**Key Vendor Protections:**
- Limited to patents and copyrights (not all IP)
- Geographic scope limited to Berne Convention countries
- Sole control of defense and settlement
- Customer must provide prompt notice
- Exclusive remedy stated
- Multiple cure options at vendor's discretion

---

**Example 2 - Product IP Indemnity with Depreciation**
```
Vendor shall (i) defend Customer against any third party claim that a
Product or Service infringes a patent, or a copyright enforceable in a
country that is a signatory to the Berne Convention, and (ii) pay the
resulting costs and damages finally awarded against Customer.

Should any such Product become, or in Vendor's opinion be likely to
become, the subject of such a claim, Vendor may, at its option and
expense: (1) procure for Customer the right to make continued use;
(2) replace or modify such so that it becomes non-infringing;
(3) request return of the Product and, upon receipt thereof, refund
the price paid by Customer, less straight-line depreciation based on
a four year useful life for Equipment and a three year useful life
for Software.
```

**Key Vendor Protection:** Depreciation reduces refund obligation based on customer's use.

### Balanced Position

**Example 3 - Mutual IP Indemnity (Services)**
```
Vendor shall (i) defend Customer against any third party claim that the
Services and the related materials provided by Vendor to Customer infringe
a patent, copyright, or trademark, and (ii) pay the resulting costs and
damages finally awarded against Customer by a court of competent
jurisdiction or the amounts stated in a written settlement signed by Vendor.

Customer shall (i) defend Vendor against any third party claim that the
materials provided by Customer or its agents for use by Vendor infringe a
patent, copyright, or trademark, and (ii) pay the resulting costs and
damages finally awarded against Vendor by a court of competent jurisdiction
or the amounts stated in a written settlement signed by Customer.

The foregoing obligations are subject to the following: the indemnitee
(a) notifies the indemnitor promptly in writing of such claim,
(b) grants the indemnitor sole control over the defense and settlement,
(c) reasonably cooperates in response to an indemnitor request, and
(d) is not in material breach of this Agreement.
```

**Key Balance Element:** Mutual indemnification recognizes both parties contribute IP.

### Customer-Favorable Position

**Example 4 - Broad IP Indemnity**
```
Vendor shall indemnify, defend and hold Customer and its officers,
directors, employees, agents, and Affiliates harmless from and against
any claim by a third party that the sale, manufacture, or use of any
software, instruments, products, parts or other equipment provided to
Customer hereunder infringes a patent, copyright, trademark, trade
secret or other proprietary rights of such third party, provided that
Customer gives Vendor prompt written notice of the claim and cooperates
fully in the defense or settlement.

Vendor shall have sole control of the defense or settlement of such
claims. Customer may participate in the defense of the claim, at its
own expense, with counsel of its own choosing.

In addition, Vendor shall, at its expense and sole option: (i) secure
for Customer the right to continue to use the allegedly infringing
Product; (ii) modify or replace it with a functional equivalent so
that it is non-infringing; or (iii) require Customer to return Product
and refund to Customer all amounts Customer has paid hereunder upon
return of such Product.
```

**Customer-Favorable Elements:**
- Covers ALL IP types (patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets)
- Includes "hold harmless" language
- Covers affiliates and personnel
- Full refund (no depreciation)
- Customer can participate with own counsel

## Exclusions (Apportionment Clauses)

### Standard Exclusions (Vendor-Favorable)

**Example 5 - Comprehensive Exclusions**
```
Vendor shall have no liability under this Section to the extent that
the alleged infringement arises out of or relates to:

(A) the use or combination of a Product or Service with third party
    products or services;
(B) use for a purpose or in a manner for which the Product or Service
    was not designed;
(C) any modification made by anyone other than Vendor or its authorized
    representatives;
(D) any modifications to a Product or Service made by Vendor pursuant
    to Customer's specific instructions;
(E) any technology owned or licensed by Customer from third parties;
(F) any Evaluation Products;
(G) use of any older version of the Software when use of a newer
    Software Release made available to Customer would have avoided
    the infringement.
```

**Why These Exclusions Matter:**
- (A) Vendor isn't liable for Customer's integration choices
- (B) Misuse is Customer's responsibility
- (C) Customer modifications = Customer's risk
- (D) Following Customer instructions = Customer's responsibility
- (E) Customer's third-party tech = Customer's problem
- (F) Evaluation products are provided as-is
- (G) Incentivizes Customer to stay current

## General/Conduct Indemnification

### Example 6 - Services Indemnity (Balanced)
```
Vendor will indemnify and hold Customer harmless from and against any
and all claims for injury or death of persons, or damage to tangible
property, occurring while Vendor personnel are on Customer's premises
performing services pursuant to this Agreement to the extent caused by
the negligent acts or negligent omissions of Vendor, provided Vendor
is given prompt notice of any such claim and the opportunity to control
the defense and settlement of same.
```

---

### Example 7 - Broad Conduct Indemnity (Customer-Favorable)
```
Vendor shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless Customer, its officers,
directors, employees, agents, successors and assigns from and against
any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including
reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to:

(a) any breach of Vendor's representations, warranties, or obligations;
(b) the negligence, gross negligence, or willful misconduct of Vendor
    or its personnel;
(c) any claim that Vendor's services infringe third-party rights;
(d) Vendor's failure to comply with applicable laws.
```

## Exclusive Remedy Language

### Example 8 - Sole and Exclusive Remedy
```
THIS SECTION STATES CUSTOMER'S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE REMEDY AND VENDOR'S
ENTIRE LIABILITY FOR THIRD PARTY INFRINGEMENT CLAIMS.
```

**Why This Matters:** Prevents Customer from seeking additional damages through separate tort claims beyond the indemnity.

## Procedural Requirements

### Example 9 - Detailed Notice and Control Provisions
```
The party seeking indemnification shall:

(a) provide the indemnifying party with prompt written notice of any
    claim, demand, suit, or proceeding for which indemnification is
    sought (provided, however, that failure to provide prompt notice
    shall not relieve the indemnifying party of its obligations except
    to the extent such failure materially prejudices the defense);

(b) grant the indemnifying party sole control of the defense and any
    related settlement negotiations, provided that the indemnifying
    party shall not settle any claim that imposes any obligation on
    the indemnified party without prior written consent;

(c) provide reasonable cooperation and assistance, at the indemnifying
    party's expense, in the defense of such claim;

(d) not make any admission or take any action that may prejudice the
    defense without the indemnifying party's prior written consent.
```

## Cross-Indemnification (Mutual Conduct)

### Example 10 - Mutual General Indemnity
```
MUTUAL INDEMNIFICATION

Each party ("Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold
harmless the other party and its officers, directors, employees, and
agents ("Indemnified Party") from and against any third-party claims,
damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees)
arising out of or resulting from:

(a) the Indemnifying Party's breach of this Agreement;
(b) the Indemnifying Party's gross negligence or willful misconduct;
(c) the Indemnifying Party's violation of applicable law.

The foregoing indemnification obligations are subject to the Indemnified
Party providing prompt notice, granting control of defense, and
reasonably cooperating in the defense.
```

## Key Negotiation Points Summary

| Element | Vendor Position | Customer Position |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| IP Types | Patents, copyrights only | All IP including trade secrets |
| Geographic | Berne Convention countries | Worldwide |
| Language | "Defend and pay" | "Indemnify, defend, hold harmless" |
| Parties Covered | Customer only | Customer + affiliates + personnel |
| Notice | Strict requirement | Failure doesn't forfeit rights |
| Control | Sole control | Customer may participate |
| Exclusions | Comprehensive list | Minimal exclusions |
| Remedy | Sole and exclusive | Additional remedies preserved |
| Refund | Depreciated | Full refund |
