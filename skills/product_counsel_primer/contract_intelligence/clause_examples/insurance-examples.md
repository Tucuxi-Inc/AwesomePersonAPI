# Insurance Requirements Clause Examples

## Standard Insurance Requirements

### Customer-Favorable (Comprehensive Requirements)

**Example 1 - Full Insurance Requirements**
```
INSURANCE

During the term and for [2] years thereafter, Vendor shall maintain
the following insurance coverages:

(a) COMMERCIAL GENERAL LIABILITY: $2,000,000 per occurrence /
    $4,000,000 aggregate, covering bodily injury, property damage,
    personal and advertising injury, and products/completed operations.

(b) PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY (E&O): $5,000,000 per claim / $5,000,000
    aggregate, covering claims arising from negligent acts, errors,
    or omissions in the performance of professional services.

(c) CYBER LIABILITY: $5,000,000 per claim, covering data breach
    response costs, regulatory fines, network security liability,
    privacy liability, and technology E&O.

(d) WORKERS' COMPENSATION: Statutory limits as required by law, with
    Employer's Liability of $1,000,000 per accident / $1,000,000
    disease per employee / $1,000,000 disease policy limit.

(e) AUTOMOBILE LIABILITY: $1,000,000 combined single limit for all
    owned, non-owned, and hired vehicles.

(f) UMBRELLA/EXCESS LIABILITY: $10,000,000 excess of CGL, Auto, and
    Employer's Liability.

(g) CRIME/FIDELITY: $2,000,000 covering employee theft, computer
    fraud, and funds transfer fraud.
```

### Vendor-Favorable (Minimal Requirements)

**Example 2 - Basic Insurance Requirements**
```
INSURANCE

Vendor shall maintain commercially reasonable insurance coverages
appropriate for its business, including:

(a) General liability insurance with limits of at least $1,000,000
    per occurrence;

(b) Professional liability insurance with limits of at least $1,000,000
    per claim;

(c) Workers' compensation as required by law.

Additional coverages may be required by mutual agreement for specific
high-risk engagements.

Insurance coverage limits shall not limit Vendor's liability under
this Agreement.
```

## Additional Insured Requirements

**Example 3 - Additional Insured Status**
```
ADDITIONAL INSURED

Customer and its officers, directors, employees, agents, and affiliates
shall be named as additional insureds on Vendor's:

(a) Commercial General Liability policy;
(b) Umbrella/Excess Liability policy;
(c) Automobile Liability policy.

Such additional insured coverage shall:
(a) Be primary and non-contributory (Customer's insurance shall be
    excess and non-contributing);
(b) Apply to claims arising out of Vendor's performance under this
    Agreement;
(c) Include completed operations coverage.

Vendor shall provide endorsements evidencing additional insured status
upon request.
```

## Waiver of Subrogation

**Example 4 - Subrogation Waiver**
```
WAIVER OF SUBROGATION

Vendor shall cause its insurers to waive all rights of subrogation
against Customer, its officers, directors, employees, agents, and
affiliates for losses covered by the required insurance policies.

Vendor shall obtain endorsements from its insurers confirming this
waiver and shall provide copies upon request.

This waiver shall apply to the extent of Customer's negligence except
for gross negligence or willful misconduct.
```

## Certificate of Insurance

**Example 5 - Certificate Requirements**
```
CERTIFICATES OF INSURANCE

(a) DELIVERY: Vendor shall provide Customer with certificates of
    insurance evidencing the required coverages:
    - Upon execution of this Agreement;
    - Upon each policy renewal;
    - Within [10] days of Customer's request.

(b) CONTENT: Certificates shall:
    - Identify all required policies with limits;
    - Confirm additional insured status;
    - Confirm waiver of subrogation;
    - Specify the Agreement to which coverage applies.

(c) CANCELLATION NOTICE: Certificates shall provide that Customer will
    receive at least [30] days prior written notice of cancellation,
    non-renewal, or material change in coverage (or [10] days for
    non-payment of premium).

(d) DISCLAIMER: Certificates are for informational purposes only and
    do not amend, extend, or alter the coverage provided by the
    underlying policies.
```

## Insurer Requirements

**Example 6 - Carrier Quality Standards**
```
INSURER REQUIREMENTS

All required insurance shall be placed with insurers that:

(a) Are licensed to do business in [State/all states where Services
    are performed];

(b) Have an A.M. Best rating of A- (Excellent) VII or higher, or
    equivalent rating from Standard & Poor's or Moody's;

(c) Are authorized to issue the types of coverage required;

(d) Are reasonably acceptable to Customer.

If any insurer's rating falls below the required minimum, Vendor shall
promptly notify Customer and obtain replacement coverage from a
qualified insurer.
```

## Claims-Made Coverage

**Example 7 - Claims-Made Tail Coverage**
```
CLAIMS-MADE POLICIES

For policies written on a claims-made basis (Professional Liability,
Cyber Liability):

(a) RETROACTIVE DATE: The retroactive date shall be no later than the
    Effective Date of this Agreement.

(b) TAIL COVERAGE: If any claims-made policy is cancelled, not renewed,
    or replaced with a policy with a later retroactive date, Vendor
    shall purchase extended reporting period ("tail") coverage of at
    least [3] years.

(c) NOTICE: Vendor shall notify Customer within [30] days if any
    claims-made policy is cancelled or not renewed.

(d) ALTERNATIVE: If tail coverage is unavailable or prohibitively
    expensive, Vendor shall notify Customer and the parties shall
    negotiate alternative arrangements.
```

## Deductibles and Self-Insurance

**Example 8 - Deductible Limitations**
```
DEDUCTIBLES AND SELF-INSURED RETENTIONS

(a) LIMITS: Deductibles and self-insured retentions shall not exceed:
    - CGL: $[25,000]
    - Professional Liability: $[100,000]
    - Cyber Liability: $[50,000]

(b) APPROVAL: Higher deductibles require Customer's prior written
    approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld if Vendor
    demonstrates adequate financial resources to cover such amounts.

(c) VENDOR RESPONSIBILITY: Vendor is responsible for all deductibles
    and self-insured retentions. Customer shall have no obligation to
    pay any portion of a deductible.

(d) SELF-INSURANCE: Self-insurance programs are acceptable only with
    Customer's prior approval and demonstration of adequate reserves.
```

## Industry-Specific Insurance

**Example 9 - Technology/SaaS Insurance**
```
TECHNOLOGY INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS

Vendor shall maintain:

(a) TECHNOLOGY E&O: $5,000,000 per claim, covering:
    - Negligent acts in technology services;
    - Software/system failure;
    - Data loss or corruption;
    - Failure to deliver technology services.

(b) CYBER LIABILITY: $5,000,000 per claim, covering:
    - First-party breach response (notification, monitoring, forensics);
    - Third-party liability for privacy and security failures;
    - Regulatory defense and penalties;
    - Media liability;
    - Business interruption from cyber events.

(c) CLOUD/HOSTING: Coverage shall specifically extend to cloud-hosted
    services and data stored in third-party data centers.

Coverage shall be primary for technology and cyber claims regardless
of any similar coverage maintained by Customer.
```

## Insurance as Condition

**Example 10 - Maintenance and Verification**
```
INSURANCE MAINTENANCE

(a) CONDITION: Maintenance of the required insurance is a material
    condition of this Agreement.

(b) FAILURE: If Vendor fails to maintain required insurance:
    - Customer may, but is not required to, purchase coverage on
      Vendor's behalf and deduct the cost from payments due;
    - Customer may suspend Vendor's access to Customer premises or
      systems until coverage is restored;
    - Failure constitutes material breach subject to termination.

(c) NO LIMITATION: Insurance requirements do not limit Vendor's
    liability under this Agreement. Vendor is liable for amounts
    in excess of policy limits and for uninsured claims.

(d) VERIFICATION: Customer may verify insurance status at any time
    by contacting Vendor's insurers or requesting updated certificates.
```
