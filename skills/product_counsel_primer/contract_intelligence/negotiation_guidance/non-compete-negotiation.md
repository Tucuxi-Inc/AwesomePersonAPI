## Regulatory Landscape (S1)

### State-by-State Overview

| State | Non-Compete Status | Key Considerations |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **California** | Void (B&P §16600) | Non-solicitation also limited |
| **Oklahoma** | Generally void | Narrow exceptions |
| **North Dakota** | Generally void | Very limited enforcement |
| **Minnesota** | Banned (2023) | Existing agreements may be void |
| **Washington** | Restricted | Minimum income threshold |
| **Massachusetts** | Restricted | Garden leave required |
| **Illinois** | Restricted | Income and duration limits |
| **Colorado** | Restricted | Highly compensated only |
| **New York** | Restrictive | Blue pencil available |
| **Texas** | Enforceable | Must be ancillary to valid agreement |
| **Florida** | Enforceable | Blue pencil, burden on employer |
| **Delaware** | Enforceable | Reasonableness standard |

### Choice of Law Considerations

```
NON-COMPETE CHOICE OF LAW

(a) This Agreement shall be governed by [State] law.

(b) Notwithstanding the foregoing, if Employee's primary work
    location is in California, the non-competition provisions
    shall not apply to the extent unenforceable under California
    Business & Professions Code § 16600.

(c) If any provision is unenforceable in Employee's jurisdiction,
    the parties authorize reformation to the maximum extent
    enforceable. Unenforceable provisions do not affect the
    enforceability of other provisions.
```

## Scope Negotiations

### Activity Restrictions

**Employer-Favorable (Broad):**
```
Employee shall not directly or indirectly:
(a) Engage in any business competitive with the Company;
(b) Own, manage, operate, control, or work for any Competitor;
(c) Provide services to any Competitor in any capacity;
(d) Have any financial interest in any Competitor.

"Competitor" means any business that develops, manufactures,
markets, sells, or distributes products or services competitive
with any Company products or services.
```

**Employee-Favorable (Narrow):**
```
Employee shall not:
(a) Work as a [specific job title] performing substantially
    the same duties as performed for Company;
(b) For a Direct Competitor listed in Exhibit A;
(c) In the specific business unit/product area Employee worked.

"Direct Competitor" means entities deriving more than [50%]
of revenue from [specific product category].

Employee may work for diversified companies with competitive
divisions, provided Employee does not work in such divisions.
```

### Geographic Limitations

| Scope | Typical Context | Enforceability |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| Worldwide | Global business, executive | Challenging |
| United States | National presence | Case-by-case |
| Multi-state region | Regional business | More likely |
| Single state | State-level operations | Generally OK |
| Metropolitan area | Local business | Generally OK |
| Customer territory | Sales roles | Generally OK |

**Best Practice:**
```
Territory shall be limited to: [City/State/Region] where
Employee primarily provided services, OR any geographic area
where Employee had customer responsibility, OR any location
where Company has established business operations.
```

## Duration Negotiations

### Duration Standards

| Context | Typical Range | Maximum Enforceable |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| Employment | 6-24 months | 24 months typical max |
| Executive | 12-24 months | 36 months possible |
| M&A (seller) | 3-5 years | 7 years max typical |
| Partnership | 2-3 years | Varies by state |
| Franchise | 2-3 years | Industry standard |

### Duration Language

**Employer Position:**
```
The Restricted Period shall be [24] months following the
termination of employment for any reason.

If Employee violates this covenant, the Restricted Period
shall be extended by the period of violation.
```

**Employee Counter:**
```
The Restricted Period shall be [6] months following termination.

If Employee is terminated without Cause, the Restricted Period
shall be reduced to [3] months.

The Restricted Period shall not be extended for any reason.
```

## Consideration Requirements

### Employment Context

**At-Hire:**
- Continued employment generally sufficient consideration
- Better practice: specific consideration recited

**Mid-Employment:**
- New consideration required in most states
- Options: promotion, raise, bonus, stock
- Amount may affect enforceability

**Post-Employment:**
```
GARDEN LEAVE

In consideration of the non-compete obligations:

(a) If Employee is terminated without Cause, Company shall
    pay Garden Leave equal to [100%] of base salary for the
    Restricted Period;

(b) If Employee resigns, the non-compete is supported by
    compensation and benefits received during employment;

(c) Employee may waive the Restricted Period by forfeiting
    any unpaid Garden Leave.
```

### M&A Context

```
SELLER NON-COMPETE CONSIDERATION

Seller acknowledges that the non-compete covenant is:
(a) Material inducement to Buyer;
(b) Supported by adequate consideration (Purchase Price);
(c) Allocated $[X] of Purchase Price for tax purposes;
(d) Reasonable and necessary to protect Buyer's investment.
```

## Non-Solicitation Alternatives

### Customer Non-Solicitation

```
NON-SOLICITATION OF CUSTOMERS

For [12] months following termination, Employee shall not:
(a) Solicit any Customer for competitive products/services;
(b) Accept business from any Customer for competitive purposes.

"Customer" means any customer with whom Employee had personal
contact or responsibility during the [24] months preceding
termination.

General advertising not targeted at Customers is permitted.
Responding to unsolicited inquiries is permitted.
```

### Employee Non-Solicitation

```
NON-SOLICITATION OF EMPLOYEES

For [12] months following termination, neither party shall:
(a) Solicit or recruit any employee of the other party;
(b) Hire any employee who was solicited in violation of (a).

This restriction applies to employees with whom the party
had contact during the engagement.

Permitted: General advertising, unsolicited applications,
employees terminated 6+ months ago.
```

## Enforceability Provisions

### Blue Pencil / Reformation

```
ENFORCEABILITY

If any court finds any provision overly broad, the parties
authorize the court to: (a) reform the provision to the
maximum extent enforceable; (b) enforce as reformed;
(c) sever unenforceable provisions without affecting others.

The parties intend the maximum enforceable protection.
```

### Injunctive Relief

```
INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

Employee acknowledges that breach would cause irreparable harm
for which monetary damages are inadequate. Company may obtain:
(a) Temporary restraining order without notice;
(b) Preliminary and permanent injunction;
(c) Specific performance;

without posting bond and without proving actual damages, in
addition to all other remedies available at law or equity.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Employer Leverage

**When:**
- Executive/senior roles with strategic knowledge
- Unique competitive information access
- M&A context (seller bound to protect value)
- High compensation justifying restrictions

**Can Demand:**
- 18-24 month duration
- Broader geographic scope
- Comprehensive activity restrictions
- Strong enforcement provisions

### High Employee/Seller Leverage

**When:**
- Key person essential to business
- Competitive job market
- California or other restrictive state
- Mid-level position with alternatives

**Can Demand:**
- 6-12 month maximum duration
- Narrow scope (specific competitors)
- Garden leave compensation
- Carve-outs for non-competitive work
- No tolling provisions

## Industry-Specific Considerations

### Technology

```
TECHNOLOGY CARVE-OUT

Notwithstanding the non-compete, Employee may:
(a) Work on open-source projects;
(b) Contribute to industry standards bodies;
(c) Accept employment using general programming skills;
(d) Work in technology sectors where Company does not compete.

"Competitive" is limited to [specific technology/product area].
```

### Professional Services

```
PROFESSIONAL SERVICES RESTRICTION

Employee shall not:
(a) Provide [specific service type] to Clients of the Company;
(b) Solicit any Client for such services;

for [12] months following termination.

Employee may provide other professional services and may
provide the restricted services to non-Clients.
```

### Healthcare

```
HEALTHCARE PRACTITIONER

Non-compete shall not prevent Employee from:
(a) Practicing in a different specialty;
(b) Treating emergency patients;
(c) Practicing at facilities that do not compete with Company;
(d) Working as required by licensing obligations.

State law restrictions on healthcare non-competes apply.
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Employee Seeking Narrower Scope

> "I understand you want to protect your business, but a 2-year
> nationwide non-compete would effectively prevent me from
> working in my field. I'm proposing: 12 months, limited to the
> metropolitan area where I'll be working, and only for direct
> competitors in our specific product segment. I'm also asking
> for garden leave if you terminate me without cause."

### Employer Protecting Business

> "Given your role and access to our strategic plans, customer
> relationships, and proprietary technology, we need meaningful
> protection. We're willing to narrow the scope to companies
> that are actual competitors rather than the entire industry,
> and we'll consider garden leave, but we need at least 18
> months and coverage in our key markets."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's structure this with a 12-month duration, limited to
> the top 10 direct competitors we'll name in an exhibit. You
> can work for larger companies as long as you're not in their
> competing division. If we terminate you without cause, we'll
> provide 6 months of garden leave. For cause termination or
> resignation, the restriction applies without garden leave."

## California-Specific Guidance

### What's Enforceable in California

| Provision | Enforceable? |
|-----------|-------------|
| Non-compete | No |
| Customer non-solicit | Very limited |
| Employee non-solicit | Limited |
| Confidentiality | Yes |
| Trade secret protection | Yes |
| Non-disclosure | Yes |

### California Alternative

```
CALIFORNIA EMPLOYEE ACKNOWLEDGMENT

Employee acknowledges and agrees:
(a) Not to use or disclose Company trade secrets;
(b) Not to use Company confidential information to compete;
(c) To assign inventions per the CIIA;
(d) To return all Company materials upon termination.

[Non-compete provisions do not apply to California employees
pursuant to Business & Professions Code § 16600.]
```
