## Resolution Mechanism Comparison

### Quick Comparison

| Factor | Litigation | Arbitration | Mediation |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Cost** | Variable (can be high) | High upfront, lower overall | Low |
| **Speed** | Slow (2-5 years) | Faster (6-18 months) | Fast (days-weeks) |
| **Appeal** | Yes | Very limited | N/A |
| **Discovery** | Full | Limited | Minimal |
| **Confidentiality** | Public record | Private | Private |
| **Expertise** | Judge/jury | Chosen arbitrator | Chosen mediator |
| **Enforceability** | Judgment | Award (NY Convention) | Settlement agreement |

### Strategic Considerations

**Prefer Litigation When:**
- Seeking precedent or deterrence
- Need full discovery
- Larger party against smaller (leverage)
- Public accountability valuable
- Appeal rights important

**Prefer Arbitration When:**
- Confidentiality important
- Industry expertise needed
- International enforcement required
- Speed valuable
- Predictability preferred

**Prefer Mediation When:**
- Preserving relationship
- Early-stage disputes
- Cost sensitivity
- Flexible outcomes desired

## Arbitration Negotiations

### Customer-Favorable Arbitration

```
ARBITRATION

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall
be resolved by binding arbitration administered by [JAMS/AAA]
under its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

(a) PANEL. Disputes under $[1,000,000] shall be decided by a
    single arbitrator. Larger disputes by a panel of three.

(b) SELECTION. The parties shall mutually select the arbitrator.
    If unable to agree within [15] days, [JAMS/AAA] shall appoint.

(c) LOCATION. Arbitration shall take place in [Customer's City].

(d) DISCOVERY. Each party entitled to [3] depositions and
    document requests.

(e) AWARD. The arbitrator may award any remedy available at
    law or equity, including injunctive relief.

(f) COSTS. The prevailing party shall recover reasonable
    attorneys' fees and arbitration costs.
```

### Vendor-Favorable Arbitration

```
ARBITRATION

Disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in [Vendor's
City] under [AAA] Rules, before a single arbitrator selected
by [AAA].

Discovery is limited to document exchange. The arbitrator may
not award punitive damages or damages exceeding the liability
cap. Each party bears its own costs.

By agreeing to arbitration, the parties waive the right to
jury trial and class action participation.
```

### Key Arbitration Variables

| Variable | Customer Position | Vendor Position | Balanced |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|----------|
| Location | Customer's location | Vendor's location | Neutral or defendant's |
| Rules | JAMS (often more formal) | AAA (often faster) | Mutually agreed |
| Panel | 3 arbitrators | Single arbitrator | Based on amount |
| Discovery | Broader rights | Limited | Moderate (depositions) |
| Fees | Prevailing party | Each own | Prevailing for claims >$X |
| Remedies | Full | Limited | Per agreement |

## Mediation Requirements

### Mandatory Pre-Arbitration Mediation

```
MEDIATION

Before initiating arbitration, the parties shall attempt to
resolve the dispute through mediation:

(a) INITIATION. Either party may initiate mediation by written
    notice describing the dispute.

(b) SELECTION. Within [10] days, parties shall select a mediator.
    If unable to agree, [JAMS] shall appoint.

(c) PROCESS. Mediation shall occur within [30] days of mediator
    selection. Each party shall send a representative with
    settlement authority.

(d) DURATION. Mediation shall continue for at least [1] full
    day unless the mediator determines earlier termination.

(e) COSTS. Mediation costs split equally. Each party bears its
    own attorneys' fees.

(f) TERMINATION. If not resolved within [45] days of initiation,
    either party may proceed to arbitration.
```

### Optional Mediation

```
MEDIATION

Either party may request mediation at any time. The other party
shall participate in good faith if requested. Mediation is not
a condition precedent to arbitration or litigation.
```

## Escalation Procedures

### Tiered Escalation

```
DISPUTE ESCALATION

The parties shall attempt to resolve disputes as follows:

(a) OPERATIONAL LEVEL. Project managers shall attempt resolution
    within [10] business days.

(b) MANAGEMENT LEVEL. If unresolved, department heads shall meet
    within [10] business days.

(c) EXECUTIVE LEVEL. If unresolved, executives shall meet within
    [15] business days.

(d) MEDIATION. If unresolved after executive meeting, the parties
    shall engage in mediation.

(e) ARBITRATION. If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved
    by arbitration per Section [X].

Time periods run concurrently where possible. Escalation does
not apply to requests for injunctive relief.
```

### Benefits of Escalation

- Filters out disputes that can be resolved operationally
- Creates cooling-off period
- Ensures appropriate decision-makers involved
- Documents good faith efforts
- Reduces litigation/arbitration costs

## Injunctive Relief Carve-Outs

### Standard Carve-Out

```
INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

Notwithstanding the dispute resolution procedures above, either
party may seek temporary, preliminary, or permanent injunctive
relief, or other equitable remedies, in any court of competent
jurisdiction to:

(a) Protect confidential information or trade secrets;
(b) Enforce intellectual property rights;
(c) Prevent irreparable harm pending dispute resolution;
(d) Enforce any covenant not to compete or solicit.

The parties consent to the jurisdiction of [courts] for such
relief. Seeking injunctive relief does not waive arbitration
rights for other claims.
```

## Cost Allocation

### Fee-Shifting Options

**Prevailing Party:**
```
The substantially prevailing party shall recover its reasonable
attorneys' fees and costs from the other party.
```

**American Rule (Each Own):**
```
Each party shall bear its own attorneys' fees and costs,
regardless of outcome.
```

**Modified Fee-Shifting:**
```
ATTORNEYS' FEES

If any dispute results in an award or judgment:
(a) Less than $[100,000]: Each party bears own fees;
(b) $[100,000] or more: Prevailing party recovers fees;
(c) For claims rejected entirely: Rejecting party recovers fees.
```

### Cost Considerations

| Cost Element | Typical Range | Notes |
|--------------|--------------|-------|
| Arbitrator fees | $500-1,500/hour | Split equally typical |
| AAA/JAMS admin | $3,000-15,000 | Based on claim amount |
| Hearing room | $500-2,000/day | If provided by institution |
| Discovery | Variable | Limited in arbitration |
| Attorneys | Variable | Major cost driver |

## Leverage-Based Analysis (BI2)

### Larger Party Advantages

**In Litigation:**
- Can afford extended litigation
- Full discovery favors resourced party
- Appeal delays benefit party with time
- Public pressure on smaller opponent

**In Arbitration:**
- Cost sharing mitigates advantage
- Speed benefits both parties
- Limited discovery levels playing field
- Expertise reduces uncertainty

### Smaller Party Considerations

**May Prefer:**
- Arbitration (lower costs)
- Mediation (relationship preservation)
- Fee-shifting (if confident in position)
- Local forum (reduce travel costs)

## International Disputes

### Cross-Border Arbitration

```
INTERNATIONAL ARBITRATION

(a) RULES. Arbitration under ICC Rules / UNCITRAL Rules.

(b) SEAT. The seat of arbitration shall be [Singapore/London/
    New York], which determines procedural law.

(c) LANGUAGE. Proceedings conducted in English.

(d) GOVERNING LAW. Substantive disputes governed by [law] per
    Section [X]. Arbitration agreement governed by [seat] law.

(e) ENFORCEMENT. Award enforceable under New York Convention.
```

### New York Convention

**Key Points:**
- 170+ countries party
- Awards enforceable internationally
- Limited grounds for refusal
- More reliable than foreign judgments

## Class Action Waivers

### Vendor-Favorable (Consumer/Employment)

```
CLASS ACTION WAIVER

Customer agrees to resolve all disputes on an individual basis.
Customer waives any right to participate in class actions,
collective actions, or representative actions. The arbitrator
may not consolidate claims or preside over class proceedings.

If this waiver is found unenforceable, the parties agree that
any class claim shall proceed in court rather than arbitration.
```

### Enforceability Considerations

- Generally enforceable in commercial B2B
- Scrutinized in consumer and employment contexts
- Some states/jurisdictions restrict
- Must be conspicuous

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Court Option

> "Our preference is to preserve the right to litigate. Arbitration
> can be expensive, and we value the appeal rights and precedential
> value of court decisions. We're willing to agree to mandatory
> mediation first, and we'll accept a venue in a neutral location."

### Vendor Seeking Arbitration

> "Arbitration provides efficiency and confidentiality that benefits
> both parties. Our standard AAA arbitration clause with a single
> arbitrator keeps costs down while providing a neutral forum.
> We include a mediation requirement first, which resolves most
> disputes without the cost of formal proceedings."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's structure this with mandatory mediation first - that
> resolves most disputes without formal proceedings. If mediation
> fails, we'll use arbitration for disputes under $500K and
> preserve litigation rights for larger claims. Each party pays
> own fees unless one party prevails on all claims."

## Decision Matrix

| Scenario | Recommended Mechanism | Key Considerations |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Standard B2B | Mediation → Arbitration | Efficiency, confidentiality |
| High-value strategic | Litigation preserved | Appeal rights, precedent |
| International | ICC/UNCITRAL arbitration | Enforcement via NY Convention |
| Relationship-critical | Mediation emphasis | Preserve relationship |
| IP-heavy | Arbitration + injunction carve-out | Speed, expertise |
| Consumer-facing | Check state law | Enforceability varies |
