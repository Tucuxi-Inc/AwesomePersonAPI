## License Audit Negotiations

### Licensor-Favorable (Strong Rights)

```
LICENSE AUDIT

Licensor may audit Licensee's use of the Software to verify
compliance with license terms.

(a) FREQUENCY. Once per [12] month period; more frequently if
    prior audit revealed material non-compliance.

(b) NOTICE. [15] days advance written notice.

(c) ACCESS. Access to: facilities where Software is installed;
    systems and usage records; personnel with knowledge.

(d) CONDUCT. Auditor may be Licensor's employees or designated
    independent auditor. Audits during business hours.

(e) COSTS. Licensee bears audit costs if underpayment exceeds
    [5%] of amounts due. Otherwise Licensor bears costs.

(f) UNDERPAYMENT. If audit reveals underpayment:
    - Licensee pays underpaid amounts within [30] days
    - Plus interest at [1.5%] per month from original due date
    - Plus audit costs if underpayment exceeds [5%]
```

### Licensee-Favorable (Limited Rights)

```
LICENSE VERIFICATION

(a) SELF-CERTIFICATION. Upon Licensor's written request (no
    more than annually), Licensee shall certify usage levels.

(b) AUDIT TRIGGER. Audit permitted only upon reasonable belief
    of material non-compliance, based on specific facts.

(c) AUDITOR. Independent third-party auditor only (not Licensor
    employees or competitors), mutually agreed.

(d) SCOPE. Limited to specific license metrics in dispute.

(e) CONFIDENTIALITY. Auditor shall execute NDA. Results shared
    only as pass/fail and amount of any underpayment.

(f) COSTS. Licensor bears all costs regardless of findings.

(g) THRESHOLD. No payment due unless underpayment exceeds [10%]
    or $[50,000], whichever is greater.
```

## Security Audit Negotiations

### Customer-Favorable (Direct Access)

```
SECURITY AUDIT

Customer may audit Vendor's security practices and controls.

(a) SCOPE. Policies, procedures, technical controls, access
    logs, incident reports, certifications, penetration test
    results, personnel security measures.

(b) FREQUENCY.
    - Annually as routine assessment
    - Following any Security Incident affecting Customer Data
    - As required by Customer's regulators
    - Upon material change to Vendor's security

(c) CONDUCT. Customer or qualified third-party auditor.
    Vendor shall provide access to facilities, systems,
    personnel, and documentation.

(d) COST. No charge for audit cooperation. Vendor shall not
    charge Customer for reasonable audit support.

(e) REMEDIATION. Material deficiencies remediated within [30]
    days with evidence provided to Customer.
```

### Vendor-Favorable (Attestation Model)

```
SECURITY VERIFICATION

In lieu of direct audits, Vendor shall provide Customer with:

(a) SOC 2 Type II report (annually);
(b) ISO 27001 certification (upon renewal);
(c) Penetration test executive summary (annually);
(d) Responses to security questionnaires within [15] days.

Customer shall accept such attestations as evidence of
compliance, except:

(e) Following a Security Incident affecting Customer Data,
    Customer may conduct targeted audit with [5] days notice;

(f) If required by Customer's regulator, Vendor shall
    cooperate with direct audit subject to confidentiality
    protections and scheduling coordination.
```

## Cost Allocation

### Audit Cost Models

| Model | Description | When Used |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Auditing party pays | Auditor bears all costs | Vendor leverage |
| Audited party pays | Subject bears all costs | Customer leverage |
| Threshold-based | Costs shift if issues found | Balanced |
| Shared | Split equally | Mutual interest |

### Threshold-Based Allocation

```
AUDIT COSTS

(a) Routine audits: Auditing party bears its own costs.

(b) If audit reveals:
    - No material issues: Auditing party bears all costs
    - Material non-compliance: Audited party bears all costs
    - Minor issues: Each party bears its own costs

(c) "Material" means: (i) underpayment exceeding [5%]; or
    (ii) security deficiency creating significant risk; or
    (iii) compliance failure exposing auditing party to
    regulatory action.
```

## Frequency Limitations

### Negotiating Frequency

| Audit Type | Standard | Customer Request | Vendor Position |
|------------|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| License | Annual | Quarterly | Annual |
| Security | Annual | Continuous | Annual + incident |
| Financial | Annual | Semi-annual | Annual |
| Compliance | Annual | As needed | Annual |

### Frequency Language

**Customer-Favorable:**
```
Customer may conduct audits: (a) annually as routine
assessment; (b) upon reasonable belief of non-compliance;
(c) following any incident; (d) as required by regulators.

No limit on incident-triggered or regulator-required audits.
```

**Vendor-Favorable:**
```
Audits limited to once per calendar year. Additional audits
require mutual agreement except where mandated by law.

Audit requests shall not unreasonably interfere with Vendor's
operations or other customer commitments.
```

## Scope Limitations

### Narrowing Audit Scope

**Vendor Protections:**
```
AUDIT SCOPE LIMITATIONS

(a) LIMITED TO AGREEMENT. Audits limited to compliance with
    this Agreement, not general business practices.

(b) NO COMPETITORS. Auditor shall not be Customer's competitor
    or employed by Customer's competitor.

(c) CONFIDENTIALITY. All audit information treated as Vendor's
    Confidential Information.

(d) OTHER CUSTOMERS. No access to other customers' data or
    dedicated systems/resources.

(e) PROPRIETARY. No access to Vendor's proprietary technology,
    source code, or trade secrets beyond what is necessary.

(f) NOTICE. Specific scope identified in advance notice.
```

## Regulatory-Driven Audits

### Regulator Access Provisions

```
REGULATORY AUDIT

(a) REGULATOR ACCESS. If Customer's regulator requires audit
    of Vendor's systems or practices, Vendor shall cooperate.

(b) SCOPE. Cooperation includes: access to relevant systems,
    documentation, and personnel; participation in regulatory
    examinations; responses to regulatory inquiries.

(c) NOTICE. Customer shall provide reasonable advance notice
    where permitted by the regulator.

(d) CONFIDENTIALITY. Vendor may request confidential treatment
    to extent permitted.

(e) NO CHARGE. No charge for regulatory audit cooperation.
```

## Alternatives to Direct Audit

### Attestation Framework

```
COMPLIANCE ATTESTATION

Vendor shall provide Customer with the following attestations
in lieu of direct audit:

(a) CERTIFICATIONS (annually):
    - SOC 2 Type II report
    - ISO 27001 certificate
    - Industry-specific certifications

(b) QUESTIONNAIRES (upon request, not more than quarterly):
    - SIG (Standardized Information Gathering)
    - CAIQ (Cloud Security Alliance)
    - Customer's security questionnaire

(c) ATTESTATION LETTER (annually):
    - Signed by security officer
    - Certifying compliance with security requirements

Customer accepts these attestations as satisfaction of audit
requirements except following Security Incident.
```

### Pooled Audit

```
POOLED AUDIT

Vendor may satisfy audit requirements through participation
in pooled audits by qualified third parties (e.g., shared
assessments), provided:

(a) Scope covers Customer's requirements;
(b) Customer receives full audit results;
(c) Audit conducted within past [12] months;
(d) Customer may request supplemental information.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Can Demand:**
- Direct audit rights
- Unlimited frequency for incidents
- Vendor pays if issues found
- Broad scope
- Short notice periods
- Remediation deadlines
- Regulator cooperation

### High Vendor Leverage

**Can Maintain:**
- Attestation-only model
- Annual frequency maximum
- Customer always pays
- Narrow scope
- Long notice periods
- Third-party auditor only
- Reasonable cooperation

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Broader Rights

> "Given the sensitivity of our data and our regulatory
> obligations, we need direct audit rights, not just attestation.
> We're required to have the ability to audit our service
> providers. We're willing to use a mutually acceptable
> third-party auditor and give reasonable notice, but we need
> the right to verify compliance directly."

### Vendor Protecting Position

> "We undergo rigorous third-party audits - SOC 2 Type II and
> ISO 27001 - specifically so we can provide assurance to all
> customers without the operational disruption of individual
> audits. We can provide our audit reports, respond to security
> questionnaires, and cooperate with regulatory examinations.
> For hundreds of customers, individual audits aren't practical."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's use your SOC 2 and ISO certifications as the baseline.
> We'll accept those for routine compliance verification. In
> addition, following any security incident affecting our data,
> we'll have the right to conduct a targeted audit of the
> affected systems. For regulatory examinations, you'll
> cooperate as required. We'll bear our own audit costs unless
> we find material issues."
