## Core Negotiation Variables

### 1. Cap Amount

**Vendor Starting Position:**
- Cap at fees paid in 12 months prior to claim
- Total agreement cap (all claims combined)

**Customer Counter:**
- Higher multiple (2x-3x annual fees)
- Per-claim cap vs. aggregate cap
- Uncapped for certain breaches

**Compromise Positions:**
- 12-month fees for general claims
- 24-month fees for data breaches
- Higher caps for larger deal values

### 2. Exclusion of Damages

**Vendor Position (Broad Exclusions):**
```
NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS
OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS
OF CAUSE OR THEORY OF LIABILITY.
```

**Customer Counter (Limited Exclusions):**
```
The foregoing exclusion shall not apply to:
(a) breaches of confidentiality obligations;
(b) indemnification obligations;
(c) willful misconduct or gross negligence;
(d) breaches of data protection obligations.
```

## Carve-Out Negotiations

### Standard Carve-Outs (Typically Accepted)

1. **Indemnification obligations** - usually uncapped or higher cap
2. **Confidentiality breaches** - 2-3x standard cap
3. **IP infringement** - often uncapped
4. **Gross negligence/willful misconduct** - uncapped
5. **Payment obligations** - uncapped (money owed is not "damages")

### Contested Carve-Outs

| Carve-Out | Vendor Position | Customer Position | Resolution |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Data breach | Include in general cap | Uncapped or high cap | 2-3x cap typical |
| Security failure | Standard cap | Uncapped | Higher cap + insurance |
| Regulatory fines | Exclude entirely | Customer's fines covered | Share based on fault |
| Lost profits | Always excluded | Carve-out for certain claims | Exclude except for IP |

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Indicators:**
- Large deal value (>$1M annual)
- Strategic importance to vendor
- Competitive alternatives available
- Sophisticated procurement team

**Customer Can Demand:**
- 3x+ annual fees cap
- Separate super-caps for critical risks
- Narrower consequential damage exclusions
- Unlimited liability for data breaches

### High Vendor Leverage

**Indicators:**
- Unique/proprietary solution
- Strong market position
- Customer urgency
- Switching costs from existing vendor

**Vendor Can Maintain:**
- 12-month fee cap
- Broad damage exclusions
- Minimal carve-outs
- Per-claim vs. aggregate cap

### Balanced Leverage

**Typical Outcomes:**
- 12-24 month fee cap
- Standard carve-outs (IP, confidentiality, indemnity)
- Consequential damages excluded except carve-outs
- Separate data breach cap at 2x

## Industry Standards (BI3)

### By Industry Vertical

| Industry | Typical Cap | Key Carve-Outs |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| SaaS/Technology | 12-24 month fees | Data, IP, confidentiality |
| Financial Services | 24-36 month fees | Regulatory, data, security |
| Healthcare | 24-36 month fees | HIPAA, data, patient harm |
| Government | Often uncapped | Broad carve-outs |
| Enterprise Software | 12-24 month fees | IP, maintenance failures |

### By Deal Size

| Annual Value | Typical Cap Multiple |
|--------------|---------------------|
| <$100K | 12 months (1x) |
| $100K-$500K | 12-18 months |
| $500K-$2M | 18-24 months |
| $2M-$10M | 24-36 months |
| >$10M | Negotiated; often higher |

## Risk Allocation Strategy (S3)

### Vendor Risk Mitigation

1. **Insurance backstop** - Require coverage matching carve-outs
2. **Mutual caps** - Ensure customer is equally limited
3. **Claim procedures** - Time limits for bringing claims
4. **Mitigation obligations** - Customer must minimize damages
5. **Exclusive remedy** - Caps apply to all theories of recovery

### Customer Risk Mitigation

1. **Carve-outs** - Protect against catastrophic scenarios
2. **Insurance requirements** - Vendor carries adequate coverage
3. **Indemnity floor** - Minimum coverage before caps apply
4. **Super-caps** - Higher limits for critical breaches
5. **Survival** - Caps survive termination

## Total Cost Analysis (BI5)

### Cap Economics

**For Vendors:**
- Higher caps = higher insurance premiums
- Carve-outs drive E&O/cyber coverage costs
- Pricing should reflect risk retained
- Revenue protection via caps

**For Customers:**
- Lower caps = more retained risk
- May need own cyber/business interruption coverage
- Evaluate vendor financial stability
- Consider vendor insurance as backstop

### Insurance Alignment

| Liability Type | Vendor Insurance | Cap Recommendation |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| General liability | CGL $2M | Equal to coverage |
| Professional liability | E&O $5M | Within coverage |
| Cyber/data breach | Cyber $5M | Match policy limits |
| IP infringement | E&O/IP $5M | Consider unlimited |

## Future-Proofing (S7)

### Growth Considerations

**Problem:** Fixed caps become inadequate as relationship grows

**Solutions:**
- Percentage of fees rather than fixed amount
- Annual cap adjustment provisions
- Renegotiation triggers at volume thresholds
- Inflation adjustment clauses

### Example - Scalable Cap

```
The aggregate liability cap shall be the greater of:
(a) fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim; or
(b) $[minimum floor amount];
adjusted annually by the Consumer Price Index.

If aggregate fees exceed $[threshold] in any 12-month period,
the parties shall negotiate in good faith an appropriate cap
adjustment.
```

## Common Negotiation Mistakes

### Vendor Mistakes

1. Agreeing to unlimited liability for any category
2. Failing to include mutual limitations
3. Not aligning caps with insurance coverage
4. Forgetting to cap indemnification obligations
5. Unclear whether caps apply to all claims combined

### Customer Mistakes

1. Accepting caps that don't cover realistic damages
2. Not negotiating carve-outs for critical risks
3. Ignoring vendor's financial ability to pay
4. Failing to require insurance minimums
5. Not addressing how caps apply to renewals

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Opening

> "While we understand the need for liability caps, a 12-month fee
> cap doesn't adequately protect us against the risks we're taking
> on. Given the sensitive data involved and our regulatory exposure,
> we need at minimum a 24-month cap with higher limits for data
> breaches and security failures."

### Vendor Response

> "We can discuss adjusting the cap structure. Our standard position
> reflects our pricing model and insurance costs. We're open to
> exploring a tiered approach - maintaining the 12-month cap for
> general operational issues while considering higher limits for
> specific scenarios like data breaches, provided we can adjust
> pricing to reflect the additional risk."

### Resolution Path

> "Let's structure this with a base cap of 18 months' fees for
> general liability, with a separate super-cap of 36 months' fees
> for data protection breaches. We'll carve out IP indemnification
> entirely and ensure your cyber insurance minimums align with
> these caps."

## Decision Matrix

| Scenario | Cap Recommendation | Carve-Out Approach |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Standard SaaS | 12-18 months | Standard (IP, confidentiality, data) |
| High-value enterprise | 24-36 months | Expanded (security, regulatory) |
| Data-intensive | 24 months + super-cap | Robust data breach carve-out |
| Regulated industry | 24-36 months | Regulatory compliance carve-out |
| Mission-critical | Higher caps or unlimited | Broad carve-outs |
