## Core Assignment Models

### Standard Anti-Assignment

```
ASSIGNMENT

Neither party may assign this Agreement or any rights or
obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the
other party, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld,
conditioned, or delayed.

Any attempted assignment without consent is void.
```

### Asymmetric Assignment

**Customer-Favorable:**
```
ASSIGNMENT

(a) VENDOR. Vendor may not assign without Customer's prior
    written consent, which may be withheld in Customer's
    sole discretion.

(b) CUSTOMER. Customer may assign without Vendor's consent to:
    (i) any affiliate; (ii) a successor by merger or
    acquisition; (iii) a purchaser of substantially all
    Customer's assets.
```

**Vendor-Favorable:**
```
ASSIGNMENT

(a) CUSTOMER. Customer may not assign without Vendor's prior
    written consent.

(b) VENDOR. Vendor may assign to any affiliate or successor,
    or may assign for financing purposes, without consent.
    Vendor may subcontract obligations without restriction.
```

## Change of Control Negotiations

### Defining Change of Control

**Broad Definition (Capturing More Events):**
```
"Change of Control" means:
(a) Acquisition of [50%] or more of voting securities;
(b) Merger, consolidation, or reorganization;
(c) Sale of substantially all assets;
(d) Change in majority of board of directors;
(e) Any other transaction that results in a person or group
    acquiring control.
```

**Narrow Definition (Fewer Triggers):**
```
"Change of Control" means:
(a) Acquisition of more than [50%] of outstanding voting stock
    resulting in a change in ultimate controlling party; or
(b) Sale of all or substantially all assets.

Internal restructuring, financing transactions, and
transactions with existing shareholders do not constitute
Change of Control.
```

### Change of Control Rights

**Customer Rights on Vendor CoC:**
```
CHANGE OF CONTROL - VENDOR

If Vendor undergoes Change of Control:

(a) NOTICE. Vendor shall notify Customer within [30] days.

(b) TERMINATION RIGHT. Customer may terminate within [60] days
    of notice if:
    - Acquirer is Customer's direct competitor; or
    - Customer reasonably determines CoC materially adversely
      affects Customer's interests.

(c) EFFECT. Termination without penalty; pro-rata refund of
    prepaid fees.

(d) ASSISTANCE. Vendor shall provide reasonable transition
    assistance for [90] days following termination notice.
```

**Vendor Rights on Customer CoC:**
```
CHANGE OF CONTROL - CUSTOMER

If Customer undergoes Change of Control:

(a) Agreement continues with successor subject to:
    - Successor agrees to be bound;
    - Successor meets creditworthiness standards;
    - No assignment to Vendor's competitor.

(b) Vendor may re-negotiate pricing upon Customer CoC to
    reflect successor's actual usage and risk profile.
```

## Consent Standards

### Consent Standard Options

| Standard | Meaning | When Used |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Sole discretion | May refuse for any reason | High leverage |
| Not unreasonably withheld | May refuse only for good reason | Balanced |
| Not unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed | Full reasonableness | Customer-favorable |
| Deemed consent if no response | Silence = consent | Assignor-favorable |

### Reasonable Withholding

**Grounds for Reasonable Refusal:**
```
Consent may be reasonably withheld if the proposed assignee:
(a) Is a direct competitor of the non-assigning party;
(b) Has materially inferior creditworthiness;
(c) Has a history of material breaches with the non-assigning party;
(d) Is subject to sanctions or export restrictions;
(e) Cannot meet regulatory or security requirements.
```

### Deemed Consent

```
If a party fails to respond to an assignment request within
[30] days, consent shall be deemed granted.

Request must: (a) be in writing; (b) identify the assignee;
(c) describe the transaction; (d) include assignee's agreement
to assume obligations; (e) state consent will be deemed if
no response within [30] days.
```

## Affiliate Assignments

### Standard Affiliate Exception

```
AFFILIATE ASSIGNMENT

Either party may assign to an affiliate without consent,
provided:
(a) The assigning party remains liable for performance;
(b) The affiliate agrees to be bound by Agreement terms;
(c) Assignment does not result in reduced service quality;
(d) Written notice provided within [15] days.

"Affiliate" means any entity controlling, controlled by, or
under common control with a party.
```

### Limiting Affiliate Assignments

**Customer Concern:**
```
Vendor may not assign to an affiliate if assignment would:
(a) Result in performance from a jurisdiction with inadequate
    data protection;
(b) Cause Customer to violate export control laws;
(c) Result in support from a different geographic region;
(d) Materially change the personnel performing Services.
```

## Competitor Restrictions

### Anti-Competitor Assignment

```
COMPETITOR RESTRICTION

(a) Neither party may assign this Agreement to a direct
    competitor of the other party without consent.

(b) "Direct Competitor" means [defined list or criteria].

(c) If Change of Control results in agreement with competitor:
    - Non-assigning party may terminate within [60] days
    - Without penalty or liability
    - With pro-rata refund of prepaid amounts
```

### Defining Competitors

| Approach | Language |
|----------|----------|
| Named list | "Companies listed in Exhibit A" |
| Criteria | "Entities deriving [50%]+ revenue from [X]" |
| Industry | "Entities primarily engaged in [industry]" |
| Mutual | "Entities that either party designates in writing" |

## Successor Obligations

### Binding Successors

```
BINDING EFFECT

This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit
of the parties and their respective successors and permitted
assigns.

Any assignee shall assume all obligations under this Agreement
by written instrument delivered to the non-assigning party
prior to the effective date of assignment.
```

### Release of Assignor

**Assignor Position:**
```
Upon a permitted assignment where the assignee assumes all
obligations, the assigning party shall be released from all
future obligations under this Agreement.
```

**Non-Assigning Party Counter:**
```
Assignment does not release the assigning party from any
obligation arising before assignment or from any obligation
that the assignee fails to perform.

[OR]

Assigning party remains secondarily liable for assignee's
performance for [24] months following assignment.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Can Demand:**
- Consent in sole discretion for vendor assignment
- Broad termination rights on vendor CoC
- Competitor restrictions on vendor assignment
- No vendor affiliate assignments to offshore entities
- Vendor remains liable after assignment

### High Vendor Leverage

**Can Maintain:**
- Unrestricted assignment to affiliates
- Assignment for financing without consent
- CoC does not trigger termination rights
- Customer cannot assign without consent
- Release upon assignment

## M&A Considerations

### Buyer Diligence Items

**Assignment Provisions to Review:**
- Can agreement be assigned in proposed transaction?
- Is consent required? From whom?
- What is the standard for consent?
- Does CoC trigger termination or renegotiation rights?
- Are there competitor restrictions?

### Seller Considerations

**Negotiating for M&A Flexibility:**
```
ASSIGNMENT FOR CORPORATE TRANSACTIONS

Either party may assign this Agreement without consent in
connection with: (a) merger or consolidation; (b) sale of
substantially all assets; (c) sale of business unit or
product line to which this Agreement relates.

Notice shall be provided within [30] days of closing.
Agreement continues with successor on same terms.
```

## Special Assignment Scenarios

### Assignment for Financing

```
FINANCING ASSIGNMENT

Vendor may assign its right to receive payments under this
Agreement to a financing party without Customer's consent,
provided that:
(a) Customer's obligations are not increased;
(b) Customer may continue to assert defenses;
(c) Notice provided to Customer;
(d) Vendor remains liable for all performance obligations.
```

### Subcontracting vs. Assignment

```
SUBCONTRACTING

(a) Vendor may subcontract Services to qualified subcontractors
    without Customer consent.

(b) Subcontracting does not constitute assignment.

(c) Vendor remains fully responsible for subcontractor
    performance as if performed by Vendor.

(d) Subcontractors shall be bound by confidentiality and
    data protection requirements no less protective.

(e) Upon request, Vendor shall identify key subcontractors.
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Termination Right

> "We selected you specifically based on your team, technology,
> and reputation. If you're acquired by a competitor or a
> company we don't have confidence in, we need the ability to
> exit. We're asking for a termination right within 60 days of
> change of control notification, without penalty."

### Vendor Seeking Flexibility

> "We need flexibility to grow our business, which may include
> acquisitions, being acquired, or internal reorganizations.
> We can't give every customer a termination right on change
> of control - that would make us un-acquirable. We can commit
> to notification and agree to a termination right only if the
> acquirer is your direct competitor."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's agree to this: you can assign to affiliates and in
> connection with any corporate transaction, but you'll notify
> us within 30 days. We'll have a termination right only if
> the acquirer is one of the five competitors we'll name in an
> exhibit. For any other change of control, the agreement
> continues with no termination right."
