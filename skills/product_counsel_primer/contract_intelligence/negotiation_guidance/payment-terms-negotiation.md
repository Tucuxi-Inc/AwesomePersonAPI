## Payment Timing Negotiations

### Payment Term Standards

| Payment Term | Working Capital Impact | When Used |
|--------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Net 15 | Very vendor-favorable | Strong vendor leverage |
| Net 30 | Industry standard | Balanced relationships |
| Net 45 | Moderately customer-favorable | Large customers |
| Net 60 | Customer-favorable | Enterprise customers |
| Net 90 | Very customer-favorable | Government, large enterprise |

### Customer-Favorable Payment Terms

```
PAYMENT TERMS

(a) INVOICING. Vendor shall invoice Customer monthly in arrears
    for Services performed in the prior month.

(b) PAYMENT. Payment is due [60] days from receipt of a proper
    invoice that includes: purchase order number, itemized
    services, applicable rates, and supporting documentation.

(c) DISPUTED AMOUNTS. Customer may withhold disputed amounts
    in good faith. Customer shall pay undisputed amounts when
    due and notify Vendor of disputed amounts within [30] days.

(d) SET-OFF. Customer may set off amounts owed to Customer
    (including service credits) against amounts due to Vendor.
```

### Vendor-Favorable Payment Terms

```
PAYMENT

All fees are due [15] days from invoice date. Invoices are
deemed accepted unless Customer provides written objection
within [10] days.

Customer may not withhold payment for disputed amounts. Disputes
shall be resolved per the dispute resolution procedures while
payments continue.

Set-off is not permitted except for undisputed amounts expressly
agreed in writing.
```

## Late Payment Consequences

### Late Fee Structures

**Vendor Position (Aggressive):**
```
LATE PAYMENT

Overdue amounts bear interest at [1.5%] per month ([18%] annual)
or the maximum legal rate, whichever is lower.

If payment is [30] days overdue, Vendor may:
(a) Suspend Services upon [5] days notice;
(b) Require prepayment for future Services;
(c) Accelerate all amounts due under the Agreement.

Customer shall reimburse Vendor's collection costs, including
reasonable attorneys' fees.
```

**Customer Counter (Moderate):**
```
LATE PAYMENT

Undisputed amounts not paid when due shall bear interest at
[0.5%] per month or the maximum legal rate, whichever is lower.

Vendor may suspend Services only if:
(a) Payment is [60] days or more overdue;
(b) Vendor provides [15] days written notice;
(c) The overdue amount exceeds $[10,000].

Suspension shall not relieve Vendor of obligations that survive
suspension, including data security and preservation.
```

### Industry Benchmarks

| Industry | Typical Late Fee | Suspension Trigger |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| SaaS | 1.0-1.5%/month | 30-45 days |
| Professional Services | 1.0%/month | 60 days |
| Manufacturing | 0.5-1.0%/month | 45-60 days |
| Construction | 1.0-1.5%/month | 30-45 days |
| Government | Statutory rate | Rarely applicable |

## Pricing Model Negotiations

### Fixed Price vs. Time & Materials

**Fixed Price (Customer-Favorable for Defined Scope):**
```
FIXED PRICING

The total fee for the Services is $[X] (the "Fixed Fee"), payable:
- [25%] upon execution
- [25%] upon completion of Phase 1
- [25%] upon completion of Phase 2
- [25%] upon Final Acceptance

The Fixed Fee includes all labor, materials, travel, and
expenses. No additional fees shall be charged without a
signed Change Order.

Risk of cost overruns is Vendor's responsibility.
```

**Time & Materials (Vendor-Favorable for Uncertain Scope):**
```
TIME AND MATERIALS

Services are billed at the rates in Exhibit A:
- Principal: $[X]/hour
- Senior Consultant: $[Y]/hour
- Consultant: $[Z]/hour

Vendor shall invoice monthly for time incurred plus
reimbursable expenses. Expenses marked up [10%].

Estimates are non-binding and provided for planning only.
Actual costs may vary based on project requirements.
```

### Hybrid Models

**Capped T&M:**
```
Services are billed on a time and materials basis, subject to
a not-to-exceed cap of $[X] ("Budget Cap"). Vendor shall not
exceed the Budget Cap without Customer's prior written approval.

Vendor shall notify Customer when [75%] of Budget Cap is reached.
```

**Fixed Fee with T&M Overages:**
```
The Fixed Fee covers the scope in Exhibit A. Out-of-scope
work requested by Customer shall be billed at the T&M rates
in Exhibit B, subject to prior approval via Change Order.
```

## Price Adjustment Negotiations

### Annual Increases

**Vendor Position (Automatic Increases):**
```
PRICE ADJUSTMENTS

Fees shall increase annually by [5%] or the Consumer Price Index
increase, whichever is greater, effective on each anniversary.

For multi-year agreements, Year 2 and beyond pricing reflects
these increases. No additional notice required.
```

**Customer Counter (Controlled Increases):**
```
PRICE ADJUSTMENTS

Fees may not increase during the Initial Term.

For Renewal Terms, fees may increase by the lesser of:
(a) [3%]; or
(b) The annual change in CPI-U (not less than zero).

Price increases require [90] days advance written notice and
shall not apply to in-progress SOWs.

Customer may terminate upon notice if proposed increase exceeds
[5%] in any year.
```

### MFN Clauses

**Customer Protection:**
```
MOST FAVORED CUSTOMER

If Vendor offers more favorable pricing or terms to any similarly
situated customer for substantially similar services, Vendor
shall offer the same pricing or terms to Customer.

Vendor shall notify Customer within [30] days if more favorable
terms become available.
```

## Expense Reimbursement

### Customer-Favorable Expense Terms

```
EXPENSES

(a) PRE-APPROVAL. Expenses exceeding $[500] individually or
    $[5,000] monthly require prior written approval.

(b) TRAVEL. Travel expenses reimbursed at actual cost, economy
    class. Business class requires approval for flights over
    [6] hours.

(c) LODGING. Lodging at [GSA rates / reasonable mid-range hotels].

(d) MEALS. Per diem of $[75]/day or actuals with receipts.

(e) NO MARKUP. Expenses reimbursed at cost with no markup.

(f) DOCUMENTATION. All expenses require itemized receipts.
```

### Vendor-Favorable Expense Terms

```
EXPENSES

Customer shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable travel and
out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing Services.
Expenses marked up [15%] for administrative handling.

No pre-approval required for individual expenses under $[2,000].
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Customer Can Demand:**
- Net 60-90 payment terms
- No late fees or minimal
- Fixed pricing with change order protection
- Price freezes or caps on increases
- Expense pre-approval and no markup
- Payment tied to acceptance
- Withholding and set-off rights

### High Vendor Leverage

**Vendor Can Maintain:**
- Net 15-30 payment terms
- Aggressive late fees (1.5%/month)
- T&M pricing
- Automatic annual increases
- Expense markup
- Advance payment or deposits
- No withholding

### Balanced Negotiations

**Typical Outcomes:**
- Net 30-45 days
- 1%/month late fees
- Hybrid pricing models
- CPI-based increases with caps
- Reasonable expense policies
- Progress-based payments
- Good faith dispute resolution

## Total Cost Analysis (BI5)

### Payment Term Economics

**Cost of Extending Terms:**
```
If Vendor offers Net 60 instead of Net 30:
- Vendor financing cost: ~1% additional (at 12% annual rate)
- May be offset by reduced risk of disputes
- Should be reflected in base pricing
```

**Customer Decision:**
```
If annual spend is $1M and cost of capital is 8%:
- Net 30 vs Net 60: ~$2,200 annual financing benefit
- Consider vs. relationship and administrative costs
```

### Hidden Costs

| Cost Element | Often Overlooked |
|--------------|------------------|
| Invoice processing | $15-50 per invoice |
| Dispute resolution | Staff time, delays |
| Audit costs | Right-to-audit exercises |
| Currency risk | International payments |
| Timing mismatch | Fiscal year considerations |

## Tax Provisions

### Standard Allocation

```
TAXES

(a) VENDOR TAXES. Vendor is responsible for all taxes on
    Vendor's income and property.

(b) CUSTOMER TAXES. Customer shall pay all sales, use, VAT,
    GST, and similar transaction taxes imposed on the Services.

(c) WITHHOLDING. If Customer is required to withhold taxes,
    Customer shall deduct such taxes from payment and provide
    Vendor with documentation.

(d) EXEMPTION. If Customer provides valid exemption certificates,
    Vendor shall not collect covered taxes.
```

### International Considerations

```
INTERNATIONAL TAX

(a) Pricing is exclusive of VAT/GST. Customer shall pay
    applicable VAT/GST in addition to fees.

(b) If withholding tax applies, fees shall be grossed up so
    Vendor receives the full contracted amount.

(c) Vendor shall provide documentation necessary for Customer
    to claim treaty benefits or reduced withholding.
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Extended Terms

> "Our standard payment terms are Net 60 from receipt of proper
> invoice. This aligns with our procurement cycle and allows
> adequate time for invoice verification. We're happy to commit
> to a defined approval process to ensure invoices are processed
> promptly."

### Vendor Seeking Faster Payment

> "Our pricing assumes Net 30 terms. Extended payment terms
> affect our cash flow and require us to factor in financing
> costs. We can offer Net 45 if you commit to electronic
> payment and a streamlined approval process, or we can adjust
> pricing for Net 60."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's meet at Net 45 with a defined invoice format and
> approval process. We'll implement electronic invoicing and
> payment to reduce processing time. For any disputed amounts,
> you'll pay the undisputed portion on time while we resolve
> the dispute."
