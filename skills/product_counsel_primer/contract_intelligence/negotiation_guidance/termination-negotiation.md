## Core Termination Rights

### 1. Termination for Convenience

**Customer-Favorable (Broad Rights):**
```
TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE

Customer may terminate this Agreement or any SOW at any time
for any reason or no reason upon [30] days prior written notice.

Upon termination for convenience:
(a) Customer shall pay for Services performed through the
    effective date of termination;
(b) Vendor shall refund any prepaid fees for Services not
    rendered;
(c) No termination fees, penalties, or early termination
    charges shall apply.
```

**Vendor-Favorable (Limited/No Convenience):**
```
TERM

This Agreement shall continue for the Initial Term and shall
automatically renew for successive Renewal Terms unless either
party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [90] days
before the end of the then-current term.

[No termination for convenience during a term.]
```

**Balanced Approach:**
```
TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE

Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon
[90] days prior written notice, effective at the end of a
calendar quarter.

Upon Customer termination for convenience during the Initial
Term, Customer shall pay a termination fee equal to [3] months
of the then-current Monthly Fees.

No termination fee applies after the Initial Term.
```

### 2. Termination for Cause

**Standard Cause Triggers:**
```
TERMINATION FOR CAUSE

Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if:

(a) MATERIAL BREACH. The other party materially breaches this
    Agreement and fails to cure within [30] days after written
    notice specifying the breach (or [10] days for payment breach);

(b) INSOLVENCY. The other party becomes insolvent, files for
    bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed, or ceases operations;

(c) REPEATED BREACHES. The other party commits [3] or more
    material breaches within any [12] month period, regardless
    of cure;

(d) FAILURE TO PERFORM. The other party fails to perform
    material obligations for [60] consecutive days.
```

## Cure Period Negotiations

### Cure Period Variables

| Breach Type | Vendor Position | Customer Position | Typical Resolution |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Payment breach | 10 days | 30 days | 15-30 days |
| Service failure | 30-60 days | 10-15 days | 30 days |
| Data breach | 30 days | Immediate | 10 days + mitigation |
| Confidentiality | 30 days | No cure | 15 days if curable |
| General material | 30-45 days | 15-30 days | 30 days |

### Cure Period Language

**Vendor-Favorable (Longer Cure):**
```
Material breaches (other than payment) shall be subject to a
[45] day cure period. Vendor shall have additional reasonable
time to cure if: (a) the breach cannot reasonably be cured
within [45] days; and (b) Vendor is diligently pursuing cure.
```

**Customer-Favorable (Shorter Cure):**
```
Material breaches shall be cured within [15] days. No cure
period applies to: (a) breaches incapable of cure; (b) breaches
of confidentiality or data protection; (c) repeated breaches.
```

## Exit Strategy (S8)

### Transition Assistance

**Customer-Favorable:**
```
TRANSITION ASSISTANCE

Upon any termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide
transition assistance for up to [6] months at Vendor's
then-current rates (or at no charge if terminated for
Vendor's breach), including:

(a) Continued provision of Services during transition;
(b) Knowledge transfer to Customer or successor vendor;
(c) Data extraction in industry-standard formats;
(d) Documentation and training materials;
(e) Reasonable cooperation with successor vendor;
(f) Assignment of third-party licenses where permitted.
```

**Vendor-Favorable:**
```
TRANSITION ASSISTANCE

Upon request made within [30] days of termination notice,
Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance at
Vendor's then-current professional services rates, provided
Customer has paid all amounts due and is not in breach.
```

### Data Return and Destruction

**Customer Requirements:**
```
DATA RETURN

Within [30] days of termination, Vendor shall:
(a) Return all Customer Data in [format specified];
(b) Provide certification of return/destruction;
(c) Delete Customer Data from all systems (except backup
    retention required by law);
(d) Provide reasonable assistance accessing Customer Data.

Vendor shall retain Customer Data for [90] days post-termination
to facilitate transition, then permanently delete.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Customer Can Demand:**
- Termination for convenience without penalty
- Short notice periods (30 days)
- Short cure periods (15 days)
- Extended transition assistance at no cost
- Refund of prepaid fees pro-rata
- No minimum commitment periods

### High Vendor Leverage

**Vendor Can Maintain:**
- No termination for convenience
- Long initial terms (3+ years)
- Extended cure periods (45-60 days)
- Termination fees for early exit
- Limited transition assistance
- Non-refundable prepaid fees

### Balanced Negotiations

**Typical Outcomes:**
- Mutual convenience termination with 60-90 day notice
- 30-day cure for most breaches
- Reasonable termination fees during initial term
- Transition assistance at standard rates
- Pro-rata refund of unused prepaid fees

## Total Cost Analysis (BI5)

### Exit Cost Components

| Cost Category | Considerations |
|---------------|---------------|
| Termination fees | Direct contractual penalties |
| Transition costs | Migration, implementation with new vendor |
| Lost prepaid fees | Non-refundable amounts |
| Stranded costs | Integrations, training, customizations |
| Productivity loss | Disruption during transition |
| Data migration | Extraction, transformation, loading |

### Negotiating to Minimize Exit Costs

**Key Provisions:**
1. Pro-rata refund of prepaid fees
2. Caps on termination fees
3. Free data extraction in usable format
4. Transition assistance included
5. Reasonable notice periods (not excessive)
6. Right to wind-down services gradually

## Temporal Dynamics (S4)

### Notice Period Strategy

**Considerations:**
- Longer notice = more transition time but less flexibility
- Shorter notice = more flexibility but rushed transitions
- Match to actual transition requirements

**Typical Periods:**

| Agreement Type | Convenience Notice | Cause Notice |
|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| SaaS subscription | 30-60 days | 30 days |
| Professional services | 30 days | 15-30 days |
| Outsourcing | 90-180 days | 30-60 days |
| Manufacturing | 90-180 days | 30-60 days |

### Auto-Renewal Negotiations

**Customer-Favorable:**
```
This Agreement shall renew for successive [1] year terms unless
either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least
[30] days before the end of the then-current term.
```

**Vendor-Favorable:**
```
This Agreement shall renew for successive terms equal to the
Initial Term unless Customer provides written notice of non-
renewal at least [90] days before the end of the then-current
term. Fees for Renewal Terms shall be Vendor's then-current
rates (or prior rates plus [5%], whichever is greater).
```

## Special Termination Events

### Change of Control

**Customer Protective:**
```
CHANGE OF CONTROL

Customer may terminate this Agreement within [60] days following
notice that Vendor has undergone a Change of Control, if:
(a) The acquirer is a direct competitor of Customer; or
(b) Customer reasonably determines the change materially
    adversely affects Customer's interests.

"Change of Control" means acquisition of [50%] or more voting
interest, merger, or sale of substantially all assets.
```

### Insolvency Provisions

**Protective Language:**
```
INSOLVENCY

Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if
the other party: (a) files for bankruptcy or has bankruptcy
filed against it not dismissed within [60] days; (b) becomes
insolvent; (c) makes assignment for benefit of creditors;
(d) has a receiver appointed; (e) ceases operations.

Upon Vendor insolvency, Customer shall have the right to:
(i) access source code from escrow; (ii) hire key personnel;
(iii) obtain assignment of necessary third-party licenses.
```

## Effects of Termination

### Survival Clauses

**Typical Survivors:**
```
SURVIVAL

The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration:
- Confidentiality (for stated period)
- Limitation of Liability
- Indemnification (for claims arising pre-termination)
- Payment obligations (for Services rendered)
- Intellectual Property ownership
- Audit rights (for [2] years post-termination)
- Dispute resolution
- General provisions
```

### Accrued Rights

**Protective Language:**
```
ACCRUED RIGHTS

Termination shall not affect: (a) any rights or obligations
that accrued before termination; (b) the coming into force of
provisions intended to survive; (c) Customer's right to use
Deliverables paid for before termination; (d) either party's
right to recover damages for breaches occurring before
termination.
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Convenience Rights

> "Given the evolving nature of our business needs, we need the
> flexibility to terminate if this solution no longer fits our
> requirements. We're asking for termination for convenience
> with 60 days notice. We understand you need some predictability,
> so we're open to discussing a reasonable termination fee during
> the initial term."

### Vendor Protecting Commitment

> "Our pricing assumes a committed term, which allows us to
> invest in implementation and support. Unrestricted termination
> for convenience doesn't work economically. We can offer
> termination for convenience after the first year, or during
> the first year with a fee equal to the remaining commitment."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's structure this with a 2-year initial term. During year
> one, early termination requires 3 months' fees. During year
> two and any renewals, either party can terminate with 90 days
> notice and no fee. You get the commitment you need to justify
> investment, we get flexibility after proving value."
