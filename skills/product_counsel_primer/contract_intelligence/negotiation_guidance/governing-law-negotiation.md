## Choice of Law Considerations

### Common Governing Law Choices

| Jurisdiction | Pros | Cons | Common For |
|--------------|------|------|------------|
| **Delaware** | Sophisticated courts, predictable | No jury for chancery | Corporate, PE, tech |
| **New York** | Strong commercial tradition | Complex procedure | Finance, international |
| **California** | Tech-friendly | Consumer protections | Technology, entertainment |
| **Texas** | Business-friendly, no income tax | Varies by county | Energy, manufacturing |
| **England** | Well-developed, predictable | Brexit uncertainty | International |

### Neutral Jurisdiction

When parties can't agree on either's home jurisdiction:
- Delaware (US)
- New York (US, for financial)
- Singapore (Asia-Pacific)
- London (international)
- Switzerland (neutral, arbitration)

## Governing Law Language

### Standard Choice of Law

```
GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance
with the laws of the State of [State], without regard to its
conflict of laws principles.
```

### Comprehensive Choice of Law

```
GOVERNING LAW

(a) SUBSTANTIVE LAW. This Agreement and any dispute arising out
    of or relating to this Agreement, including its formation,
    interpretation, performance, breach, or termination, shall
    be governed by the laws of the State of [State].

(b) CONFLICTS. Without giving effect to any choice or conflict
    of law provision that would cause application of the laws
    of any other jurisdiction.

(c) EXCLUSIONS. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for
    the International Sale of Goods (CISG) and the Uniform
    Computer Information Transactions Act (UCITA) shall not
    apply to this Agreement.
```

### Why "Without Regard to Conflicts"

This language prevents a court from applying another jurisdiction's
law based on conflicts principles, ensuring the chosen law applies.

## Jurisdiction Negotiations

### Exclusive vs. Non-Exclusive

**Exclusive Jurisdiction:**
```
The parties irrevocably submit to the EXCLUSIVE jurisdiction
of the [federal and state] courts located in [City, State].
```
- Certainty of forum
- May limit enforcement options
- Defendant must litigate in chosen forum

**Non-Exclusive Jurisdiction:**
```
The parties consent to the NON-EXCLUSIVE jurisdiction of the
courts of [State] for any action arising out of this Agreement.
```
- More flexibility
- Permits suit in other jurisdictions
- May face motion to dismiss for forum non conveniens

### Carve-Outs

```
JURISDICTION CARVE-OUTS

Notwithstanding exclusive jurisdiction:

(a) Either party may seek injunctive relief in any court of
    competent jurisdiction;

(b) Either party may bring an action in any jurisdiction where
    the other party has assets to enforce a judgment;

(c) Either party may bring claims in any jurisdiction required
    by applicable law.
```

## Venue Negotiations

### Venue Waiver Language

```
VENUE

Each party irrevocably waives any objection it may have to
the laying of venue in the [courts] based on:
(a) Forum non conveniens; or
(b) Any other basis.
```

### Why Venue Matters

- Venue determines specific courthouse within jurisdiction
- Local rules and procedures vary
- Practical considerations (travel, local counsel)

## Jury Waiver

### Standard Jury Waiver

```
JURY WAIVER

EACH PARTY HEREBY IRREVOCABLY WAIVES ALL RIGHT TO A TRIAL BY
JURY IN ANY ACTION, PROCEEDING, OR COUNTERCLAIM ARISING OUT OF
OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE TRANSACTIONS CONTEMPLATED
HEREBY.
```

### Considerations

**Pros of Jury Waiver:**
- Faster resolution
- More predictable outcomes
- Judge may better understand complex issues
- Reduces emotional/sympathy factors

**Cons of Jury Waiver:**
- May benefit defendants (smaller verdicts)
- Enforceability varies by jurisdiction
- California disfavors (Civil Code §1751)

## Federal vs. State Court

### Federal Court Preference

```
FEDERAL COURT

To the extent federal subject matter jurisdiction exists, any
action shall be brought in the United States District Court for
the [District of ___].

If federal jurisdiction does not exist, venue shall be in the
state courts of [County, State].
```

### State Court Preference

```
STATE COURT

Any action shall be brought exclusively in the state courts
of [County, State].

Neither party shall remove to federal court any action filed
in state court unless the action independently satisfies
federal jurisdictional requirements.
```

### Considerations

| Factor | Federal Court | State Court |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Discovery | Federal rules (uniform) | State rules (vary) |
| Judge quality | Generally high | Varies |
| Speed | Often slower | Varies |
| Diversity | Required or federal question | None |
| Appeal | Circuit courts | State appellate |

## International Considerations

### Cross-Border Clause

```
GOVERNING LAW; INTERNATIONAL

(a) GOVERNING LAW. This Agreement shall be governed by and
    construed in accordance with the laws of [England and
    Wales / State of Delaware].

(b) EXCLUSIONS. The United Nations Convention on Contracts
    for the International Sale of Goods and UCITA shall not
    apply.

(c) JURISDICTION. The parties submit to the exclusive
    jurisdiction of the courts of [jurisdiction].

(d) ENFORCEMENT. Each party waives any immunity from
    jurisdiction or enforcement to which it may be entitled.

(e) LANGUAGE. This Agreement is in English. Any translation
    is for convenience only; the English version controls.
```

### Judgment Enforcement

**Key Considerations:**
- US judgments not automatically enforceable abroad
- Must domesticate in foreign jurisdiction
- Arbitration awards easier to enforce (New York Convention)
- Consider jurisdiction where defendant has assets

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Can Demand:**
- Customer's home state law
- Customer's home venue
- Exclusive jurisdiction
- Federal court preference
- Jury waiver

### High Vendor Leverage

**Can Maintain:**
- Vendor's home state law
- Vendor's home venue
- Exclusive jurisdiction
- Arbitration requirement
- Class action waiver

### Balanced Approach

**Common Resolutions:**
- Neutral jurisdiction (Delaware, NY)
- Defendant's jurisdiction (loser travels)
- Mutual convenience consideration
- Non-exclusive jurisdiction

## Service of Process

### Standard Service Provision

```
SERVICE OF PROCESS

Service of process may be effected:
(a) By personal delivery;
(b) By registered or certified mail to the notice address;
(c) By overnight courier;
(d) In any manner permitted by applicable law.

Service by mail shall be complete upon deposit.
```

### Agent for Service

```
PROCESS AGENT

Each party appoints [Name/CT Corporation/etc.] as its agent
for service of process. Either party may change its agent by
written notice to the other party.
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Home Forum

> "We need disputes resolved in our home jurisdiction. Our
> legal team is here, we know the local courts, and it reduces
> our cost to litigate. We're willing to accept your state's
> substantive law if we can have our home forum."

### Vendor Seeking Home Forum

> "Our standard terms require disputes in our home state. We
> deal with dozens of customers across the country; we can't
> litigate everywhere. Delaware law and courts provide
> predictable, sophisticated handling of commercial disputes."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's split it: Delaware law for substantive questions -
> it's predictable for both of us - but defendant's home state
> for venue. That way neither of us has to travel unless we
> choose to bring a claim. For injunctive relief, either party
> can go to any court of competent jurisdiction."

## Anti-SLAPP and Special Statutes

### California Anti-SLAPP

```
CALIFORNIA PROVISION

If any claim is brought in California, the responding party
may file a special motion to strike under California Code of
Civil Procedure Section 425.16 (Anti-SLAPP statute).
```

### Consumer Protections

Some jurisdictions void choice of law/forum in consumer
contracts - California, Massachusetts, Washington have
strong consumer protection statutes.
