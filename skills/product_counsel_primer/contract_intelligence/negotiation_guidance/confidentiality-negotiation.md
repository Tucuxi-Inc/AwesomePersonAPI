## Core Negotiation Variables

### 1. Scope of Confidential Information

**Disclosing Party (Broad Definition):**
```
"Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by
Discloser to Recipient, whether orally, in writing, or
electronically, including technical data, trade secrets,
know-how, business information, customer lists, financial
data, and any information that a reasonable person would
consider confidential.
```

**Receiving Party (Narrow Definition):**
```
"Confidential Information" means only information that is:
(a) disclosed in writing and marked "Confidential" at time
    of disclosure; or
(b) disclosed orally and summarized in writing within 30
    days with confidential designation.
```

**Balanced Approach:**
```
"Confidential Information" means information disclosed by
either party that: (a) is marked confidential; (b) is
identified as confidential at time of oral disclosure;
or (c) by its nature would reasonably be considered
confidential (including source code, pricing, customer
data, and business plans).
```

### 2. Duration

| Party Position | Term | Survival |
|----------------|------|----------|
| Discloser-favorable | Perpetual | Indefinite survival |
| Balanced | 3-5 years | 2-3 years post-termination |
| Recipient-favorable | 2-3 years | 1 year post-termination |
| Trade secrets | Perpetual | Until no longer qualifies |

## Permitted Disclosure Negotiations

### Standard Permitted Recipients

**Discloser's Starting Position:**
- Only employees with need to know
- No contractors or affiliates

**Recipient's Counter:**
- Employees, contractors, and advisors
- Affiliates under common control
- Potential acquirers (under separate NDA)

**Typical Resolution:**
```
Recipient may disclose Confidential Information to:
(a) employees with a need to know who are bound by
    confidentiality obligations no less protective;
(b) contractors engaged in connection with the Purpose,
    bound by written confidentiality agreements;
(c) professional advisors (legal, accounting) under
    professional duty of confidentiality;
(d) affiliates, provided Recipient remains responsible.
```

### Compelled Disclosure

**Discloser-Favorable:**
```
Recipient shall: (i) provide prompt notice to Discloser
(where legally permitted); (ii) cooperate with Discloser
to seek protective order; (iii) disclose only the minimum
required; (iv) seek confidential treatment. Discloser may
assume defense of any proceeding.
```

**Recipient-Favorable:**
```
Recipient may disclose Confidential Information as required
by law, regulation, or legal process, provided Recipient
gives reasonable advance notice where practicable.
```

## Information Classification Strategies

### Tiered Confidentiality

**When Useful:**
- Large volume of information exchanged
- Mixed sensitivity levels
- Need for practical marking compliance

**Structure:**
```
CLASSIFICATION LEVELS

"Highly Confidential" - Trade secrets, source code, pricing
formulas, customer PII. Perpetual protection. Limited to
named individuals.

"Confidential" - Business plans, financial projections,
technical specifications. 5-year protection.

"Internal Use Only" - General business information.
3-year protection. No marking required.
```

## Use Restriction Negotiations

### Purpose Limitation

**Discloser's Position:**
```
Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely for the
Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever. Recipient shall
not use Confidential Information to develop competing
products or services.
```

**Recipient's Counter:**
```
Recipient may use Confidential Information for: (a) the
Purpose; (b) improving Recipient's existing products;
(c) general industry knowledge (residuals clause).
```

### Residuals Clause

**Recipient's Request:**
```
Nothing in this Agreement shall restrict Recipient from
using general knowledge, skills, experience, ideas, concepts,
or techniques retained in the unaided memory of Recipient's
personnel who have had access to Confidential Information.
```

**Discloser's Counter:**
```
The residuals provision does not apply to: (a) trade secrets;
(b) source code or algorithms; (c) customer data;
(d) information subject to third-party restrictions.
Recipient shall not assign personnel to memorize information.
```

## Return and Destruction

### Discloser-Favorable

```
Upon termination or Discloser's request, Recipient shall:
(a) return or destroy all Confidential Information;
(b) certify destruction in writing within 10 days;
(c) permanently delete from all systems;
(d) return all copies, notes, and derivatives.
```

### Recipient-Favorable

```
Upon termination, Recipient shall return or destroy
Confidential Information, except:
(a) copies in routine backup systems (subject to continued
    confidentiality until overwritten);
(b) copies required to be retained by law or regulation;
(c) copies in archived communications and files (subject to
    continued confidentiality).

Recipient shall certify compliance within 30 days of request.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### One-Way NDA Scenarios

**When Appropriate:**
- Only one party disclosing
- Significant information asymmetry
- Pre-transaction due diligence

**Discloser Advantages:**
- Broader scope definitions
- Longer protection periods
- Stricter use restrictions
- Injunctive relief provisions

### Mutual NDA Balance

**Even Exchange:**
- Mirror image obligations
- Identical definitions
- Same duration both directions
- Mutual injunctive relief

**Uneven Exchange:**
- One party discloses more sensitive information
- Asymmetric terms may be appropriate
- Consider tiered approach

## Temporal Dynamics (S4)

### Duration Considerations

| Information Type | Recommended Duration |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Trade secrets | Perpetual (until public) |
| Source code | 5-7 years |
| Business plans | 3-5 years |
| Pricing/financial | 3-5 years |
| Technical specs | 3-5 years |
| Customer lists | 3-5 years |
| Marketing materials | 2-3 years |

### Survival After Termination

**Issue:** Agreement terminates but information remains sensitive

**Solution:**
```
Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination or
expiration of this Agreement for a period of [X] years,
provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets
shall survive for so long as such information qualifies
as a trade secret under applicable law.
```

## Competitive Landscape (BI4)

### Anti-Competitive Use

**Discloser's Concern:** Information used to compete

**Protective Provisions:**
```
Recipient shall not: (a) use Confidential Information to
develop competing products; (b) reverse engineer products
based on Confidential Information; (c) use Confidential
Information in any patent application; (d) disclose to
any competitor of Discloser.
```

### Competitive Analysis Carve-Out

**Recipient's Need:** Business involves analyzing competitors

**Negotiated Language:**
```
Nothing herein shall restrict Recipient from conducting
independent competitive analysis or research that does
not utilize Confidential Information. Recipient may
benchmark publicly available features and functionality.
```

## Common Negotiation Mistakes

### Discloser Mistakes

1. Marking requirements too onerous to follow
2. Duration too short for actual sensitivity
3. No carve-out for standard exceptions
4. Failing to address derivative works
5. No remedies for breach

### Recipient Mistakes

1. Accepting perpetual obligations unnecessarily
2. No backup/archival exception
3. No residuals clause for general knowledge
4. Certification requirements too burdensome
5. No notice before compelled disclosure

## Negotiation Scripts

### Recipient Seeking Broader Exceptions

> "While we understand the need to protect your confidential
> information, the current marking requirements aren't practical
> for how we'll be working together. Can we agree that certain
> categories - like pricing and source code - are automatically
> confidential, while other information requires marking?"

### Discloser Protecting Trade Secrets

> "Our trade secrets need perpetual protection since they maintain
> value indefinitely. We're comfortable with a 5-year term for
> general business information, but source code, algorithms, and
> proprietary methodologies need to remain protected as long as
> they qualify as trade secrets."

### Resolution on Duration

> "Let's use a tiered approach: trade secrets remain protected
> perpetually, technical information for 5 years, and general
> business information for 3 years. We'll define each category
> clearly in the agreement."

## Special Scenarios

### Pre-Acquisition Due Diligence

**Key Terms:**
- Very broad scope
- Strict limitations on recipients
- Return/destruction upon deal termination
- No residuals clause
- Standstill provisions

### Joint Development

**Key Terms:**
- Mutual obligations
- Clear ownership of developed IP
- Feedback provisions
- Publication restrictions
- Post-project confidentiality

### Employee Mobility

**Key Terms:**
- Reasonable duration (2-3 years)
- Clear residuals carve-out
- No restriction on general skills
- Permitted new employment
- Focus on documents, not knowledge
