## Core Warranty Types

### 1. Performance Warranty

**Customer-Favorable (Comprehensive):**
```
PERFORMANCE WARRANTY

Vendor warrants that the Software shall:
(a) Perform substantially in accordance with the Documentation
    for [12] months following Acceptance;
(b) Meet all functional requirements in the Specifications;
(c) Be free from material defects in design and workmanship;
(d) Not degrade in performance during the warranty period.

If the Software fails to conform to this warranty, Vendor shall,
at Customer's option: (i) repair the non-conformance; (ii) replace
the non-conforming component; or (iii) if neither is commercially
practicable, refund the fees paid.
```

**Vendor-Favorable (Limited):**
```
WARRANTY

Vendor warrants that the Software will perform substantially in
accordance with the Documentation for [90] days following delivery.
Vendor's sole obligation and Customer's exclusive remedy for
breach of this warranty is Vendor's reasonable efforts to repair
the reported non-conformance.
```

### 2. Services Warranty

**Customer-Favorable:**
```
SERVICES WARRANTY

Vendor warrants that Services shall be performed:
(a) In a professional and workmanlike manner;
(b) By qualified personnel with requisite skills and experience;
(c) In accordance with the Statement of Work and Specifications;
(d) In compliance with applicable laws and industry standards.

Vendor shall re-perform non-conforming Services at no charge.
If re-performance fails, Customer may obtain services from a
third party and recover reasonable costs from Vendor.
```

**Vendor-Favorable:**
```
WARRANTY

Vendor warrants that Services will be performed in a professional
manner consistent with industry standards. Vendor's sole obligation
for non-conforming Services is re-performance. No warranty claims
may be made more than [30] days after Service completion.
```

## Warranty Period Negotiations

### Standard Periods by Product Type

| Product/Service Type | Vendor Position | Customer Position | Typical |
|---------------------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| Enterprise software | 90 days | 24 months | 12 months |
| SaaS | During subscription | Extended | During term |
| Custom development | 90 days | 18 months | 6-12 months |
| Professional services | 30 days | 90 days | 30-60 days |
| Hardware | 12 months | 36 months | 12-24 months |
| Integrations | 30 days | 12 months | 90 days |

### Warranty Period Language

**Extending the Period:**
```
EXTENDED WARRANTY

The Warranty Period shall be: (a) [12] months from Acceptance;
or (b) [18] months from Delivery, whichever occurs first.

If defects are discovered and corrected during the Warranty
Period, the warranty shall be extended by a period equal to
the time the Software was non-conforming, plus an additional
[90] days for the corrected component.
```

## Warranty Disclaimers

### Standard Disclaimer

```
WARRANTY DISCLAIMER

EXCEPT FOR THE EXPRESS WARRANTIES SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT,
VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
INCLUDING THE IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS
FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, TITLE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

VENDOR DOES NOT WARRANT THAT THE SOFTWARE WILL BE ERROR-FREE,
UNINTERRUPTED, OR MEET CUSTOMER'S REQUIREMENTS.
```

### Customer Pushback on Disclaimers

**Narrowing Disclaimers:**
```
The disclaimer of implied warranties shall not apply to:
(a) Defects known to Vendor at time of delivery;
(b) Representations made in Vendor's marketing materials;
(c) Specifications in the Documentation;
(d) Any warranties that cannot be disclaimed under law.

Vendor represents it has disclosed all known material defects.
```

## Warranty Remedies

### Remedy Hierarchy

**Customer-Favorable:**
```
WARRANTY REMEDIES

If the Software fails to conform to the warranty:

(a) REPAIR. Vendor shall first attempt to repair the defect
    within [10] business days of notice.

(b) REPLACE. If repair is unsuccessful, Vendor shall replace
    the non-conforming component within [15] business days.

(c) REFUND. If neither repair nor replacement is practicable,
    Customer may terminate and receive a refund of all fees
    paid for the non-conforming Software.

Customer's remedies are cumulative and in addition to any other
remedies available at law or equity.
```

**Vendor-Favorable:**
```
EXCLUSIVE REMEDY

Vendor's sole obligation and Customer's exclusive remedy for
breach of warranty is, at Vendor's option: (i) repair of the
non-conforming item; or (ii) replacement of the non-conforming
item. If neither is commercially practicable within [60] days,
Vendor may refund the fees paid for the non-conforming item.

This remedy is Customer's sole and exclusive remedy for any
warranty claim.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Customer Can Demand:**
- 12-24 month warranty periods
- Full refund as remedy option
- Cumulative remedies (not exclusive)
- Narrow disclaimers
- Third-party repair rights if vendor fails
- Extended warranty on repaired components

### High Vendor Leverage

**Vendor Can Maintain:**
- 90-day warranty
- Repair-only remedy
- Exclusive remedy provisions
- Broad disclaimers
- Time limits on claims
- Conditions and exclusions

### Balanced Negotiation

**Typical Outcomes:**
- 6-12 month warranty
- Repair first, then replace, then refund
- Exclusive remedy for warranty but not for fraud/gross negligence
- Reasonable disclaimers with carve-outs
- 30-day claim window

## Risk Allocation (S3)

### Allocating Warranty Risk

**Vendor Risk Concerns:**
- Unknown defects
- Customer misuse
- Integration issues
- Environmental factors
- Scope creep on fixes

**Customer Risk Concerns:**
- Defective deliverables
- Business disruption
- Implementation investment
- Switching costs if fails
- Ongoing dependency

### Conditions and Exclusions

**Vendor Protective Conditions:**
```
WARRANTY CONDITIONS

The warranty does not apply to defects caused by:
(a) Customer's modification of the Software without approval;
(b) Use not in accordance with Documentation;
(c) Combination with non-Vendor products;
(d) Customer's failure to install updates;
(e) Force majeure events;
(f) Customer-provided specifications or materials.

Customer must notify Vendor in writing of warranty claims within
[30] days of discovery and provide reasonable assistance.
```

**Customer Counter:**
```
The foregoing exclusions shall not apply if:
(a) Vendor approved the modification or combination;
(b) Documentation was inadequate or inaccurate;
(c) The defect would have occurred regardless of Customer action;
(d) Vendor had knowledge of the defect.
```

## Quality Assurance (S9)

### Acceptance Testing

**Relationship to Warranty:**
```
ACCEPTANCE AND WARRANTY

Customer acceptance following successful completion of
Acceptance Testing does not waive warranty rights for:
(a) Defects not reasonably discoverable during testing;
(b) Defects that manifest only under production conditions;
(c) Latent defects;
(d) Regression defects from updates or patches.
```

### Ongoing Quality Obligations

**SaaS Warranty Model:**
```
SAAS WARRANTY

During the Subscription Term, Vendor warrants that:
(a) The Service will meet the SLA availability requirements;
(b) The Service will perform substantially as documented;
(c) Vendor will provide updates and patches as necessary;
(d) Security measures will meet industry standards.

Failure to meet SLA triggers service credits per Schedule A.
Material failures trigger termination rights per Section X.
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Stronger Warranty

> "A 90-day warranty doesn't give us enough time to thoroughly
> validate the software in our production environment. Given our
> implementation timeline and the complexity of our use case,
> we need at least 12 months to identify issues that may only
> surface under real-world conditions."

### Vendor Response

> "Our 90-day warranty reflects our confidence in our testing
> process. After 90 days, we transition to our standard support
> agreement which covers defect fixes. We can discuss extending
> the warranty period if that's important to you, but it would
> need to be reflected in the pricing."

### Resolution Approach

> "Let's structure a 12-month warranty that covers defects
> substantially affecting functionality. For minor issues,
> we'll rely on your standard support. We'll define 'material
> defect' clearly and include a reasonable cure period. In
> exchange, we'll commit to a longer-term agreement."

## Special Warranty Scenarios

### Third-Party Component Warranties

```
THIRD-PARTY WARRANTIES

Software incorporates third-party components subject to their
respective warranties. Vendor shall: (a) pass through all
available third-party warranties; (b) use commercially reasonable
efforts to enforce such warranties on Customer's behalf;
(c) replace third-party components with equivalent alternatives
if warranties are unavailable.

Vendor makes no independent warranty for third-party components
beyond the pass-through warranties.
```

### Data Accuracy Warranties

```
DATA WARRANTY

Vendor warrants that data provided in connection with the
Services: (a) is obtained from reliable sources; (b) is
processed using industry-standard methods; (c) is materially
accurate as of the stated date.

Vendor does not warrant data completeness or fitness for
Customer's specific purposes. Customer is responsible for
validating data for critical business decisions.
```
