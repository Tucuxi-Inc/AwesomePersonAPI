## Core Ownership Models

### 1. Customer Owns All Work Product

**Customer Position:**
```
WORK PRODUCT OWNERSHIP

All Work Product created by Vendor in performance of the
Services shall be the sole and exclusive property of Customer.
"Work Product" includes all inventions, discoveries, works of
authorship, designs, software, documentation, and materials,
whether or not patentable or copyrightable.

Vendor hereby assigns to Customer all right, title, and interest
in and to the Work Product, including all intellectual property
rights therein. Vendor agrees to execute all documents and take
all actions necessary to perfect Customer's ownership.

Work Product shall be deemed "work made for hire" to the maximum
extent permitted by law.
```

**When Appropriate:**
- Customer paying significant development fees
- Custom solution for customer's specific needs
- Customer needs exclusive competitive advantage
- High-value strategic projects

### 2. Vendor Retains IP (License Model)

**Vendor Position:**
```
INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

Vendor retains all right, title, and interest in and to the
Deliverables, including all intellectual property rights
therein. Subject to payment, Vendor grants Customer a
non-exclusive, perpetual license to use the Deliverables for
Customer's internal business purposes.

All tools, methodologies, processes, know-how, and pre-existing
materials used in creating the Deliverables remain Vendor's
exclusive property. Customer receives only the license rights
expressly granted herein.
```

**When Appropriate:**
- Vendor using existing platform/tools
- Deliverables will be reused for other customers
- Lower price point reflecting license vs. ownership
- SaaS and productized services

### 3. Split Ownership Model

**Negotiated Compromise:**
```
INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ALLOCATION

(a) CUSTOMER-SPECIFIC DELIVERABLES. Customer shall own all
    Deliverables that are specific to Customer's business,
    including customizations, configurations for Customer's
    use cases, and Customer Data integrations.

(b) VENDOR TECHNOLOGY. Vendor retains ownership of: (i) all
    pre-existing intellectual property; (ii) tools and
    methodologies; (iii) generic components not specific to
    Customer; (iv) improvements to Vendor's platform.

(c) LICENSES. Vendor grants Customer a perpetual license to
    use Vendor Technology embedded in Customer-Specific
    Deliverables. Customer grants Vendor a license to use
    generic concepts and approaches for other customers.
```

## Pre-Existing IP Negotiations

### Vendor Pre-Existing IP

**Vendor Protection:**
```
PRE-EXISTING MATERIALS

"Vendor Pre-Existing Materials" means all intellectual property
owned or licensed by Vendor prior to the Effective Date, or
developed by Vendor outside this Agreement, including:
- Software platforms and frameworks
- Libraries, modules, and code components
- Tools, utilities, and development environments
- Methodologies, processes, and best practices
- Generic templates and documentation

Vendor Pre-Existing Materials remain Vendor's exclusive property.
To the extent incorporated into Deliverables, Vendor grants
Customer a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use
such materials solely as part of the Deliverables.
```

**Customer Counter:**
```
Customer acknowledges Vendor Pre-Existing Materials remain
Vendor's property, provided that Vendor: (a) identifies such
materials in writing before incorporation; (b) provides Customer
with a perpetual, irrevocable license to use, modify, and
create derivative works; (c) ensures no third-party restrictions
limit Customer's use of Deliverables.
```

### Customer Pre-Existing IP

**Customer Protection:**
```
CUSTOMER MATERIALS

All Customer-provided materials, data, specifications, and
intellectual property ("Customer Materials") remain Customer's
exclusive property. Vendor receives only a limited license to
use Customer Materials for performing the Services.

Upon termination, Vendor shall return or destroy all Customer
Materials and certify compliance in writing.
```

## Joint Development Scenarios

### Co-Development Agreement

**Key Negotiation Points:**

| Issue | Customer Position | Vendor Position | Resolution |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|------------|
| Joint IP ownership | Equal ownership | Sole ownership with license | Field-of-use split |
| Commercialization | Exclusive in field | Non-exclusive broadly | Territory/field division |
| Improvements | Joint ownership | Improver owns | Background vs. foreground |
| Cost sharing | 50/50 | Customer pays more | Based on expected value |

**Sample Joint Ownership:**
```
JOINT INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

(a) JOINT IP. Inventions and works conceived or created jointly
    by employees of both parties ("Joint IP") shall be owned
    jointly by the parties. Each party may exploit Joint IP
    without accounting to the other, except as restricted below.

(b) FIELD RESTRICTIONS. Notwithstanding joint ownership:
    - Customer has exclusive rights in [Customer's Field]
    - Vendor has exclusive rights in [Vendor's Field]
    - Both may use in non-exclusive fields

(c) LICENSING. Neither party may grant exclusive licenses to
    Joint IP without the other's consent. Non-exclusive licenses
    require notice but not consent.
```

## Leverage-Based Negotiation (BI2)

### High Customer Leverage

**Indicators:**
- Large contract value
- Customer funding R&D
- Strategic importance to vendor
- Competitive alternatives

**Customer Can Demand:**
- Full ownership of all work product
- Assignment of any arising patents
- No vendor reuse of concepts
- Broad license to vendor pre-existing IP

### High Vendor Leverage

**Indicators:**
- Unique proprietary technology
- Standard product/platform
- Strong market position
- High switching costs

**Vendor Can Maintain:**
- License-only model
- Narrow use restrictions
- Ownership of improvements
- Limitations on modifications

### Balanced Leverage

**Typical Outcomes:**
- Split ownership based on contribution
- Perpetual licenses both directions
- Clear pre-existing IP carve-outs
- Reasonable derivative work rights

## Value Creation Analysis (S10)

### IP Value Assessment

**Questions to Consider:**
1. What is the competitive value of the IP?
2. Can it be reused or monetized?
3. What is the development investment?
4. Who is better positioned to commercialize?
5. What are the ongoing maintenance costs?

### Pricing IP Ownership

| Ownership Model | Typical Pricing Impact |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Customer full ownership | Premium (1.5-2x) |
| Exclusive license | Moderate premium |
| Non-exclusive license | Standard pricing |
| Vendor ownership | Discount possible |

## Competitive Landscape (BI4)

### Competitive Restrictions

**Customer Concern:** Vendor using learnings for competitors

**Protective Language:**
```
COMPETITIVE RESTRICTIONS

Vendor shall not: (a) use Work Product or Customer Confidential
Information for any other customer in [Customer's Industry];
(b) provide substantially similar deliverables to Customer's
direct competitors for [18] months; (c) assign personnel who
worked on this project to competitor projects for [12] months.

"Direct Competitors" means: [List or Definition].
```

**Vendor Counter:**
```
Vendor may use general knowledge, skills, and experience
gained during the engagement for any purpose. Vendor shall
not use or disclose Customer Confidential Information or
Customer-owned Work Product for competitors.

[Competitive restrictions apply only to specific deliverables
listed in Exhibit A.]
```

## Common Ownership Scenarios

### Custom Software Development

**Default:** Customer owns, vendor retains tools/frameworks

**Key Terms:**
- Clear definition of what's "custom"
- License back for vendor tools
- Source code ownership/escrow
- Maintenance considerations

### Professional Services

**Default:** Deliverables to customer, methodology to vendor

**Key Terms:**
- Work product vs. methodology distinction
- Report/analysis ownership
- Data and findings use
- Publication rights

### SaaS Customization

**Default:** Vendor owns platform, customer owns configs/data

**Key Terms:**
- Integration code ownership
- Custom feature ownership
- Data portability
- Exit rights to configurations

## Negotiation Scripts

### Customer Seeking Full Ownership

> "Given the significant investment we're making and the strategic
> importance of this project, we need to own all work product
> created under this agreement. We understand you have pre-existing
> tools and frameworks - we're happy to acknowledge those remain
> yours with a license to us - but anything created for this
> project needs to be our property."

### Vendor Protecting IP

> "Our ability to serve customers efficiently depends on reusing
> our frameworks and methodologies. We can't assign ownership of
> our core platform, but we can ensure you have perpetual rights
> to everything you need. The customizations specific to your
> business can be yours, but the underlying technology remains
> ours with a license to you."

### Resolution - Split Model

> "Let's distinguish between customer-specific deliverables and
> your platform technology. You'll own the custom integrations,
> configurations, and anything specific to our business. We'll
> have a perpetual license to your embedded tools. For any joint
> improvements to your platform based on our requirements, we'll
> have a royalty-free license and you can use them for other
> customers."
