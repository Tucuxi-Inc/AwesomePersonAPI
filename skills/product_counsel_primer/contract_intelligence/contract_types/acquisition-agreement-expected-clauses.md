# Acquisition Agreement - Expected Clauses

## Overview

Acquisition agreements govern the purchase of businesses, whether through asset purchases, stock/equity purchases, or mergers. They address purchase price, representations, closing conditions, and post-closing obligations. This skill documents expected structure and provisions based on analysis of M&A agreement samples.

## Transaction Types

| Type | Description | Liabilities | Tax Treatment |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Asset Purchase** | Buyer acquires specific assets | Only assumed liabilities | Buyer gets step-up basis |
| **Stock Purchase** | Buyer acquires equity interests | All liabilities transfer | Seller capital gains |
| **Merger** | Companies combine into one | Surviving entity assumes all | Varies by structure |
| **Reverse Merger** | Target survives; acquires buyer | Target shell survives | Often tax-free |

## Required Clauses

### 1. Purchase and Sale

**Asset Purchase**:
```
SALE OF ASSETS
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, at the Closing,
Seller shall sell, transfer, convey, assign, and deliver to Buyer, and
Buyer shall purchase and acquire from Seller, all of Seller's right,
title, and interest in and to the Purchased Assets, free and clear
of all Encumbrances, except Permitted Encumbrances.

"Purchased Assets" means all assets used in or related to the Business,
including: (a) inventory; (b) equipment; (c) intellectual property;
(d) contracts (as set forth in Schedule X); (e) accounts receivable;
(f) goodwill; (g) books and records.

"Excluded Assets" means: (a) cash and cash equivalents; (b) tax refunds;
(c) corporate records; (d) assets listed in Schedule Y.
```

**Stock Purchase**:
```
SALE OF SHARES
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, at the Closing,
Seller shall sell, transfer, and deliver to Buyer, and Buyer shall
purchase and acquire from Seller, all of the issued and outstanding
shares of capital stock of the Company (the "Shares"), free and clear
of all Encumbrances.
```

### 2. Purchase Price

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Base purchase price
- [ ] Adjustment mechanisms
- [ ] Payment terms
- [ ] Escrow/holdback

**Example**:
```
PURCHASE PRICE
The aggregate purchase price for the Purchased Assets shall be
$[X] (the "Purchase Price"), subject to adjustment as set forth below.

Payment:
(a) At Closing: $[X] by wire transfer
(b) Escrow: $[X] deposited with the Escrow Agent
(c) Seller Note: $[X] evidenced by the Promissory Note
(d) Earnout: Up to $[X] per Section [Y]

WORKING CAPITAL ADJUSTMENT
Within [90] days after Closing, Buyer shall prepare a statement of
Closing Working Capital. If Closing Working Capital exceeds/is less
than the Target Working Capital by more than $[X], the Purchase Price
shall be adjusted accordingly.
```

### 3. Earnout Provisions

```
EARNOUT
Seller may receive additional consideration ("Earnout Payments") based
on the Business achieving the following milestones during the Earnout
Period ([24] months following Closing):

(a) If Revenue equals or exceeds $[X]: $[Y]
(b) If EBITDA equals or exceeds $[X]: $[Y]

Buyer shall operate the Business in good faith and in the ordinary
course. Buyer shall provide quarterly earnout reports. Disputes shall
be resolved by an independent accountant.
```

### 4. Representations and Warranties - Seller

**Organization and Authority**:
```
SELLER REPRESENTATIONS
Seller represents and warrants to Buyer as follows:

(a) Organization. The Company is duly organized, validly existing,
    and in good standing under the laws of [State].

(b) Authority. Seller has full power and authority to execute and
    deliver this Agreement and to consummate the transactions
    contemplated hereby.

(c) No Conflicts. The execution and performance of this Agreement
    will not conflict with any law, agreement, or organizational
    document to which Seller is bound.
```

**Financial Statements**:
```
(d) Financial Statements. The Financial Statements attached as
    Exhibit [X]: (i) were prepared in accordance with GAAP; (ii)
    fairly present the financial condition of the Company; (iii)
    reflect all liabilities required to be reflected under GAAP.
```

**Material Adverse Change**:
```
(e) Absence of Changes. Since the Balance Sheet Date, there has
    been no Material Adverse Change in the Business, and the Company
    has conducted the Business only in the ordinary course.
```

**Key Business Representations**:
```
(f) Title to Assets. Seller has good and marketable title to the
    Purchased Assets, free of all Encumbrances except Permitted
    Encumbrances.

(g) Intellectual Property. Schedule [X] lists all IP. The Company
    owns or has valid licenses to all IP necessary to conduct the
    Business. No IP infringes any third-party rights.

(h) Contracts. Schedule [X] lists all Material Contracts. All
    Material Contracts are valid and in full force. No party is
    in breach.

(i) Litigation. Except as disclosed, there is no pending or
    threatened litigation against the Company.

(j) Compliance. The Company is in compliance with all applicable
    laws. The Company has all necessary permits and licenses.

(k) Employees. Schedule [X] lists all employees with compensation.
    No union or collective bargaining agreement. No pending labor
    disputes.

(l) Taxes. All tax returns have been filed. All taxes have been
    paid. No audits pending.

(m) Environmental. The Company is in compliance with environmental
    laws. No Hazardous Materials have been released on the property.
```

### 5. Representations and Warranties - Buyer

```
BUYER REPRESENTATIONS
Buyer represents and warrants to Seller:

(a) Organization. Buyer is duly organized and in good standing.
(b) Authority. Buyer has full power to execute and perform.
(c) No Conflicts. Execution will not conflict with any law or agreement.
(d) Financing. Buyer has sufficient funds to pay the Purchase Price.
(e) Investment Intent. Buyer is acquiring the Shares for investment
    and not with a view to distribution (stock purchase).
```

### 6. Closing Conditions

**Mutual Conditions**:
```
CONDITIONS TO EACH PARTY'S OBLIGATIONS
The obligations of each party to consummate the Closing are subject to:

(a) No Injunction. No governmental order preventing the transaction.
(b) Regulatory Approvals. All required governmental approvals obtained.
(c) HSR Clearance. Any applicable HSR waiting period expired or terminated.
```

**Buyer's Conditions**:
```
CONDITIONS TO BUYER'S OBLIGATIONS
Buyer's obligation to close is subject to:

(a) Representations True. Seller's representations true as of Closing.
(b) Covenants Performed. Seller has performed all pre-closing covenants.
(c) No MAC. No Material Adverse Change since execution.
(d) Third-Party Consents. All required consents obtained.
(e) Due Diligence. Satisfactory completion of due diligence.
(f) Key Employees. Employment agreements executed with key employees.
(g) Non-Compete. Seller has executed the Non-Competition Agreement.
```

**Seller's Conditions**:
```
CONDITIONS TO SELLER'S OBLIGATIONS
Seller's obligation to close is subject to:

(a) Representations True. Buyer's representations true as of Closing.
(b) Covenants Performed. Buyer has performed all pre-closing covenants.
(c) Payment. Buyer has delivered the Closing Payment.
```

### 7. Indemnification

```
INDEMNIFICATION

Seller Indemnification. Seller shall indemnify Buyer against Losses
arising from: (a) breach of any representation or warranty; (b)
breach of any covenant; (c) Excluded Liabilities; (d) pre-Closing
taxes; (e) matters set forth in Schedule [X].

Buyer Indemnification. Buyer shall indemnify Seller against Losses
arising from: (a) breach of any representation or warranty; (b)
breach of any covenant; (c) Assumed Liabilities; (d) post-Closing
operation of the Business.

Limitations:
(a) Basket: No indemnification until aggregate Losses exceed $[X]
    (Basket), then indemnification from dollar one [or only excess].
(b) Cap: Total indemnification shall not exceed $[X] (or [X%] of
    Purchase Price).
(c) Exceptions: Basket and Cap do not apply to: (i) fraud;
    (ii) intentional breach; (iii) Fundamental Representations;
    (iv) tax indemnification.

Survival:
(a) General representations: [18-24] months from Closing
(b) Fundamental representations: [statute of limitations + 60 days]
(c) Tax representations: [statute of limitations + 60 days]
(d) Covenants: Per their terms or [24] months if no term specified
```

## Common Clauses

### 8. Pre-Closing Covenants

```
CONDUCT OF BUSINESS
Between signing and Closing, Seller shall:
(a) Operate the Business in the ordinary course consistent with past practice;
(b) Preserve relationships with customers, suppliers, and employees;
(c) Maintain insurance coverages;
(d) Not enter into Material Contracts without Buyer consent;
(e) Not incur debt or liens outside ordinary course;
(f) Not declare dividends or distributions;
(g) Provide Buyer reasonable access for due diligence.
```

### 9. Employee Matters

```
EMPLOYEES
Buyer shall offer employment to the employees listed in Schedule [X]
(the "Transferred Employees") on terms substantially similar to
current terms. Seller shall terminate employment of Transferred
Employees effective as of Closing.

Buyer shall provide Transferred Employees with credit for service
with Seller for purposes of vacation, PTO, and eligibility (but not
benefit accrual) under Buyer's benefit plans.

Seller shall be responsible for all WARN Act obligations and severance
for employees not offered employment by Buyer.
```

### 10. Non-Competition

```
NON-COMPETITION
For [5] years following Closing, Seller and its principals shall not,
directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, or consult
for any Competing Business within the Territory.

"Competing Business" means any business that [description].

This covenant is a material inducement to Buyer and is supported by
adequate consideration (the Purchase Price). The parties agree the
scope is reasonable and necessary.
```

### 11. Escrow

```
ESCROW
At Closing, $[X] of the Purchase Price shall be deposited with the
Escrow Agent to secure Seller's indemnification obligations. The
Escrow shall be held for [18] months following Closing.

Claims against the Escrow shall be made per the Escrow Agreement.
Upon expiration of the escrow period, remaining funds (less pending
claims) shall be released to Seller.
```

## Optional Clauses

### 12. R&W Insurance

```
REPRESENTATION AND WARRANTY INSURANCE
Buyer shall obtain a representation and warranty insurance policy
(the "R&W Policy") with coverage of at least $[X]. The R&W Policy
shall be the primary source of recovery for breaches of Seller's
representations (except for fraud), and Seller's liability shall
be limited to the Retention amount.
```

### 13. Transition Services

```
TRANSITION SERVICES
Following Closing, Seller shall provide the transition services
described in Exhibit [X] for up to [6] months. Buyer shall pay
Seller's costs plus [X%]. Either party may terminate specific
services upon [30] days notice.
```

## Red Flags - Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Risk Level | Implication |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| No MAC definition | HIGH | Unclear closing risk |
| No escrow/holdback | HIGH | No recovery mechanism |
| No survival periods | HIGH | Claims time-barred early |
| No basket/cap | MEDIUM | Unlimited exposure |
| Missing schedules | HIGH | Incomplete disclosure |
| No R&W bring-down | MEDIUM | Stale representations |
| No non-compete | MEDIUM | Seller competition |
| Vague earnout terms | HIGH | Manipulation disputes |

## Key Decision Points

1. **Transaction Structure**: Asset, stock, or merger?
2. **Purchase Price**: Fixed, adjusted, or earnout?
3. **R&W Scope**: Extensive or limited schedules?
4. **Indemnification**: Basket, cap, and survival?
5. **Escrow**: Amount and duration?
6. **MAC Definition**: What triggers MAC?
7. **Closing Conditions**: How much conditionality?
8. **Employee Treatment**: Who transfers and on what terms?
9. **Non-Compete**: Scope and duration?
10. **Financing**: Committed or contingent?
