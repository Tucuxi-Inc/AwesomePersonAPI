# Supply/Manufacturing Agreement - Expected Clauses

## Overview

Supply and manufacturing agreements govern the production and delivery of goods, components, or finished products. These agreements are critical for OEM relationships, contract manufacturing, and component sourcing. This skill documents expected structure and provisions based on analysis of supply agreement samples.

## Agreement Types

| Type | Description | Key Features |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Component Supply** | Purchase of parts/components | Specifications, quality, delivery |
| **Contract Manufacturing** | Third-party production | Tooling, quality control, IP |
| **OEM Agreement** | Products for resale | Branding, warranty pass-through |
| **Private Label** | Supplier's product with buyer's brand | Packaging, minimum orders |
| **Toll Manufacturing** | Buyer provides materials | Processing, yield |

## Required Clauses

### 1. Products and Specifications

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Product identification
- [ ] Specifications reference
- [ ] Change control process
- [ ] Documentation requirements

**Example**:
```
Products. Supplier shall manufacture and supply the Products described
in Exhibit A ("Specifications"). Products shall conform to the
Specifications in all material respects.

Change Control. Neither party shall make changes to the Specifications
without the other's prior written consent. Supplier shall provide
[60] days notice of any proposed changes. Changes affecting form,
fit, or function require Buyer's written approval.
```

### 2. Ordering and Forecasts

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Purchase order process
- [ ] Binding vs. non-binding forecasts
- [ ] Lead times
- [ ] Minimum order quantities

**Example**:
```
Forecasts. Buyer shall provide rolling [12]-month forecasts on a
[monthly] basis. The first [3] months of each forecast are binding
commitments; remaining months are non-binding estimates.

Purchase Orders. Buyer shall issue purchase orders at least [30]
days before requested delivery. Each PO shall specify quantity,
delivery date, and shipping instructions. Supplier shall accept or
reject POs within [5] business days.

Minimum Orders. Each PO shall be for at least [X] units. Annual
minimum purchase commitment is [X] units.
```

### 3. Pricing

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Unit pricing
- [ ] Volume discounts
- [ ] Price adjustment mechanisms
- [ ] Tooling/NRE costs

**Example**:
```
Pricing. Buyer shall pay the unit prices set forth in Exhibit B.
Prices are [FOB Origin / DDP Destination] and exclude taxes.

Volume Discounts:
- 1-999 units: $[X] per unit
- 1,000-9,999 units: $[X] per unit
- 10,000+ units: $[X] per unit

Price Adjustments. Supplier may adjust prices annually, effective
[January 1], upon [90] days notice. Increases shall not exceed [X%]
per year unless due to documented increases in raw material costs
exceeding [X%].
```

### 4. Delivery and Shipping

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Delivery terms (Incoterms)
- [ ] Lead times
- [ ] Partial shipments
- [ ] Risk of loss

**Example**:
```
Delivery. Supplier shall deliver Products [FOB Supplier's facility /
DDP Buyer's facility] per the schedule in each PO.

Lead Time. Standard lead time is [X] weeks from PO acceptance.
Expedited delivery may be available at additional cost.

Partial Shipments. Supplier may make partial shipments with Buyer's
consent. Each shipment shall be invoiced separately.

Risk of Loss. Risk of loss passes to Buyer upon [delivery to carrier /
delivery to Buyer's facility].
```

### 5. Inspection and Acceptance

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Incoming inspection rights
- [ ] Acceptance/rejection period
- [ ] Rejection process
- [ ] Return procedures

**Example**:
```
Inspection. Buyer may inspect Products within [30] days of delivery.
Products not rejected within such period are deemed accepted.

Rejection. If Products fail to conform to Specifications, Buyer shall
notify Supplier in writing with reasonable detail. Supplier shall,
at its option: (a) replace non-conforming Products; (b) repair
non-conforming Products; or (c) refund the purchase price.

Returns. Buyer shall hold rejected Products for Supplier's disposition
instructions. Supplier shall bear return shipping costs for non-
conforming Products.
```

### 6. Quality Requirements

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Quality standards
- [ ] Testing requirements
- [ ] Certifications
- [ ] Audit rights

**Example**:
```
Quality Standards. Supplier shall maintain a quality management
system compliant with [ISO 9001 / IATF 16949 / AS9100] and provide
certificates upon request.

Testing. Supplier shall test Products per the test procedures in
Exhibit C and retain test records for [X] years.

Audit Rights. Buyer may audit Supplier's facilities and quality
systems upon [30] days notice, no more than [annually]. Supplier
shall correct any audit findings within [30] days.
```

### 7. Warranties

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Product warranty scope
- [ ] Warranty period
- [ ] Exclusions
- [ ] Remedy

**Example**:
```
Warranty. Supplier warrants that Products shall: (a) conform to
Specifications; (b) be free from defects in material and workmanship;
(c) be manufactured in compliance with applicable laws; (d) be free
of liens and encumbrances; (e) not infringe third-party IP rights.

Warranty Period. [18] months from delivery or [12] months from
installation by end customer, whichever is earlier.

Remedy. For breach of warranty, Supplier shall, at Buyer's option:
(a) repair; (b) replace; or (c) refund. Supplier shall reimburse
Buyer's reasonable costs for warranty claims, including labor,
shipping, and disposal.
```

## Common Clauses

### 8. Tooling and Equipment

```
Tooling. Buyer shall pay for tooling as set forth in Exhibit [X].
Tooling shall be Buyer's property and marked as such. Supplier shall
maintain tooling in good condition and insure for replacement value.

Upon termination, Supplier shall deliver tooling to Buyer within
[30] days or, at Buyer's option, destroy and certify destruction.
Supplier grants Buyer a license to use tooling with alternative
suppliers if Supplier fails to deliver.
```

### 9. Supply Continuity

```
Continuity of Supply. Supplier shall maintain at least [X] weeks of
finished goods inventory and [X] weeks of critical component inventory.

End of Life. Supplier shall provide at least [12] months notice
before discontinuing any Product. Buyer may place a last-time buy
order for up to [X] months of forecasted demand.

Disaster Recovery. Supplier shall maintain a business continuity
plan and provide a summary upon request.
```

### 10. Epidemic Failure

```
If defects in Products affect more than [X%] of units delivered in
any [90]-day period ("Epidemic Failure"), Supplier shall:
(a) immediately notify Buyer and implement containment;
(b) conduct root cause analysis within [X] days;
(c) replace all potentially affected units;
(d) reimburse Buyer's recall and field replacement costs.
```

### 11. Compliance and Social Responsibility

```
Supplier represents and warrants that: (a) Products are manufactured
in compliance with all applicable laws; (b) no forced labor, child
labor, or human trafficking is used; (c) Supplier complies with
applicable environmental laws; (d) Supplier maintains records
demonstrating compliance.
```

### 12. Indemnification

```
Supplier shall indemnify Buyer from claims arising from: (a) Product
defects; (b) Supplier's negligence or willful misconduct; (c) violation
of law in manufacturing; (d) infringement of IP rights.

Buyer shall indemnify Supplier from claims arising from: (a) Buyer's
modifications to Products; (b) Buyer's specifications causing
infringement; (c) Buyer's marketing or sale of Products.
```

## Optional Clauses

### 13. Exclusivity

```
During the term, Supplier shall not manufacture products substantially
similar to the Products for any competitor of Buyer in the Territory.

During the term, Buyer shall purchase [X%] of its requirements for
similar products from Supplier.
```

### 14. Cost Reduction

```
The parties shall collaborate on cost reduction initiatives.
Cost savings shall be shared [50/50]. Supplier shall implement
approved cost reductions within [X] days.
```

### 15. Capacity Reservation

```
Supplier shall reserve manufacturing capacity sufficient to meet
Buyer's forecasted demand plus [X%]. If demand exceeds reserved
capacity, Supplier shall use reasonable efforts to increase
production. Buyer may pay for additional capacity reservation.
```

## Red Flags - Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Risk Level | Implication |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| No specifications | HIGH | Quality disputes |
| No quality standards | HIGH | Unreliable supply |
| No change control | HIGH | Unauthorized changes |
| No warranty | HIGH | No recourse for defects |
| No audit rights | MEDIUM | Cannot verify quality |
| No end-of-life notice | HIGH | Supply disruption |
| No epidemic failure provision | MEDIUM | Field failure exposure |
| No tooling ownership | HIGH | Stranded if relationship ends |
| No delivery terms | MEDIUM | Shipping disputes |
| No force majeure | MEDIUM | Supply chain disruption |

## Negotiation Priorities

### Buyer Priorities
1. **Quality assurance**: ISO certification; audit rights; testing
2. **Supply security**: Inventory; capacity; continuity
3. **Warranty protection**: Duration; coverage; remedy
4. **Price stability**: Caps on increases; cost-down commitments
5. **IP protection**: Tooling ownership; confidentiality
6. **Exit rights**: Termination for convenience; transition support
7. **Indemnification**: Product liability; IP infringement

### Supplier Priorities
1. **Volume commitment**: Minimum orders; binding forecasts
2. **Price adjustments**: Raw material pass-through
3. **Payment terms**: Short terms; deposits for tooling
4. **Liability limits**: Caps; consequential exclusion
5. **Change management**: Compensation for spec changes
6. **IP protection**: Background IP; improvement ownership
7. **Term security**: Minimum term; termination notice
