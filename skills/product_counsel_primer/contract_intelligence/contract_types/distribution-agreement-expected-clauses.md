# Distribution Agreement - Expected Clauses

## Overview

Distribution agreements establish relationships for reselling products or services through channel partners. They define territories, pricing, marketing responsibilities, and terms of the commercial relationship. This skill documents expected structure and provisions based on analysis of distribution agreement samples.

## Agreement Types

| Type | Description | Key Features |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Distributor** | Buys inventory, resells to dealers/retailers | Takes title, inventory risk |
| **Reseller** | Authorized to resell, may not hold inventory | Pass-through or markup |
| **VAR** | Adds value (integration, customization) | Enhanced services |
| **Agent** | Facilitates sales, doesn't take title | Commission-based |
| **OEM** | Embeds in own products | White-label, integration |
| **Referral** | Refers leads only | Referral fees |

## Required Clauses

### 1. Appointment and Territory

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Nature of appointment (exclusive/non-exclusive)
- [ ] Territory definition
- [ ] Customer restrictions (if any)
- [ ] Reservation of rights

**Example**:
```
Appointment. Supplier hereby appoints Distributor as [exclusive /
non-exclusive] distributor of the Products in the Territory.

Territory. "Territory" means [Country / Region / Named Accounts].

Exclusivity. During the term, Supplier shall not: (a) appoint other
distributors in the Territory; (b) sell directly to customers in
the Territory, except for [Named Accounts / Government / OEM].

Reservation. Supplier reserves the right to sell directly to
[house accounts] listed in Exhibit [X].
```

### 2. Products and Pricing

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Product description
- [ ] Pricing structure
- [ ] Discount from list price
- [ ] Price change mechanism

**Example**:
```
Products. "Products" means the products listed in Exhibit A, as
updated by Supplier from time to time.

Pricing. Distributor shall purchase Products at [X%] discount from
Supplier's then-current list price. Current pricing is in Exhibit B.

Price Changes. Supplier may change list prices upon [30] days notice.
Price changes shall not affect accepted purchase orders.

Resale Pricing. Distributor is free to establish its own resale
prices; provided, however, Supplier may publish suggested retail
prices.
```

### 3. Orders and Delivery

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Ordering process
- [ ] Acceptance/rejection
- [ ] Delivery terms
- [ ] Risk of loss

**Example**:
```
Orders. Distributor shall submit purchase orders specifying Products,
quantities, and requested delivery dates. Supplier shall accept or
reject orders within [5] business days.

Minimum Orders. Each order shall be for at least [X] units or $[X].

Delivery. Supplier shall deliver Products [FOB Supplier's facility /
DDP Distributor's warehouse]. Delivery dates are estimates only.

Title and Risk. Title and risk of loss pass to Distributor upon
[shipment / delivery].
```

### 4. Payment Terms

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Payment terms
- [ ] Credit terms
- [ ] Late payment consequences
- [ ] Security (if applicable)

**Example**:
```
Payment. Distributor shall pay invoices within [30] days of invoice
date. All amounts are in [USD] and exclusive of taxes.

Credit. Supplier may extend credit in its discretion and may require
security, prepayment, or letter of credit.

Late Payment. Overdue amounts accrue interest at [1.5%] per month.
Supplier may suspend shipments if amounts are more than [30] days
overdue.
```

### 5. Marketing and Promotion

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Marketing obligations
- [ ] Promotional materials
- [ ] Trade shows
- [ ] Advertising approval

**Example**:
```
Marketing Efforts. Distributor shall use commercially reasonable
efforts to promote and market the Products in the Territory, including:
(a) maintaining trained sales personnel;
(b) participating in trade shows;
(c) advertising in industry publications;
(d) maintaining a product-focused website.

Materials. Supplier shall provide marketing materials at no charge.
Distributor may not create marketing materials without Supplier's
prior approval.

Co-Marketing Fund. Supplier shall provide co-marketing funds equal
to [X%] of Distributor's purchases, subject to Supplier's approval
of marketing activities.
```

### 6. Trademarks and Branding

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Trademark license
- [ ] Usage guidelines
- [ ] Quality control
- [ ] No ownership transfer

**Example**:
```
Trademark License. Supplier grants Distributor a non-exclusive license
to use Supplier's trademarks ("Marks") solely to market and distribute
Products in accordance with Supplier's brand guidelines.

Quality Control. Distributor shall use Marks only in the form provided
by Supplier and in compliance with Supplier's trademark usage policy.
Supplier may inspect Distributor's use of Marks upon request.

Ownership. Distributor acquires no rights in the Marks except the
limited license granted herein. All goodwill inures to Supplier's
benefit.
```

### 7. Performance Requirements

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Minimum purchase commitments
- [ ] Sales targets
- [ ] Consequences of failure
- [ ] Reporting requirements

**Example**:
```
Minimum Purchases. Distributor shall purchase at least the following
minimum quantities in each year:
- Year 1: $[X]
- Year 2: $[X]
- Year 3: $[X]

Failure to Meet Minimums. If Distributor fails to meet minimum
commitments, Supplier may: (a) convert exclusivity to non-exclusive;
(b) reduce Territory; (c) terminate for cause.

Reporting. Distributor shall provide quarterly reports including:
sales by product and customer; inventory levels; pipeline and forecasts.
```

## Common Clauses

### 8. Training and Support

```
Training. Supplier shall provide initial training for Distributor's
sales and technical personnel at Supplier's facility at no charge.
Additional training is available at Supplier's standard rates.

Support. Supplier shall provide [Tier 2/3] technical support.
Distributor shall provide [Tier 1] customer-facing support.
```

### 9. Inventory and Returns

```
Inventory. Distributor shall maintain inventory sufficient to meet
customer demand, but no less than [X] weeks of forecasted sales.

Returns. Distributor may return unsold Products in original condition
within [90] days of purchase for credit toward future orders, subject
to [X%] restocking fee. Defective Products may be returned at any
time for full credit or replacement.
```

### 10. Warranty Pass-Through

```
Supplier Warranty. Supplier warrants Products to Distributor as set
forth in Exhibit [X].

End Customer Warranty. Distributor shall pass through Supplier's
warranty to end customers without modification. Supplier shall
fulfill warranty claims for end customers.

Distributor Warranty Disclaimer. Distributor shall not make any
warranty on Supplier's behalf beyond the standard warranty.
```

### 11. Non-Compete

```
During the term and for [12] months thereafter, Distributor shall
not distribute products that directly compete with the Products
in the Territory without Supplier's prior written consent.

"Competing Products" means [description].
```

## Optional Clauses

### 12. Sub-Distributors

```
Distributor may appoint sub-distributors with Supplier's prior
written consent. Distributor remains responsible for sub-distributor
performance and compliance. Sub-distributor agreements must include
terms substantially similar to this Agreement.
```

### 13. Most Favored Distributor

```
Supplier represents that the pricing and terms offered to Distributor
are no less favorable than those offered to any other distributor
for similar volumes and territories. If Supplier offers better terms
to another distributor, Supplier shall offer such terms to Distributor.
```

### 14. Records and Audit

```
Distributor shall maintain accurate records of sales, inventory,
and customer information for [3] years. Supplier may audit such
records upon [30] days notice, no more than annually.
```

## Post-Termination Obligations

### Wind-Down Rights

```
Upon termination: (a) Distributor may sell remaining inventory for
[90] days; (b) Supplier shall accept return of unsold inventory
(purchased within [6] months) for full credit; (c) Distributor shall
cease using Marks and return all materials; (d) Supplier may purchase
Distributor's inventory at Distributor's cost.
```

### Customer Transition

```
Upon termination, Distributor shall: (a) provide Supplier with
customer contact information; (b) cooperate in transitioning
customer relationships; (c) not disparage Supplier or Products.
```

### No Compensation for Termination

```
Distributor acknowledges that termination or non-renewal in accordance
with this Agreement shall not entitle Distributor to compensation,
indemnification, or damages for loss of goodwill, investment, or
future profits.
```

## Red Flags - Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Risk Level | Implication |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| Unclear exclusivity | HIGH | Competitive distribution |
| No territory definition | HIGH | Overlap disputes |
| No minimum commitments | MEDIUM | Passive distributor |
| No price protection | MEDIUM | Margin erosion |
| No termination rights | HIGH | Locked in relationship |
| No inventory return right | HIGH | Stranded inventory |
| No trademark guidelines | MEDIUM | Brand damage |
| Missing reporting | MEDIUM | No visibility |
| No non-compete | MEDIUM | Competitive conflicts |
| No post-termination wind-down | HIGH | Abrupt termination |

## Negotiation Priorities

### Supplier Priorities
1. **Performance requirements**: Minimums; sales targets
2. **Territory control**: Define scope; reservation of rights
3. **Brand protection**: Trademark controls; approval rights
4. **Reporting**: Sales data; inventory; customers
5. **Flexibility**: Conversion to non-exclusive; termination rights
6. **Customer access**: End customer information

### Distributor Priorities
1. **Exclusivity**: Territory protection; account protection
2. **Pricing**: Discounts; price protection; MFN
3. **Marketing support**: Co-op funds; materials
4. **Inventory rights**: Return rights; stock rotation
5. **Term security**: Minimum term; renewal rights
6. **Post-termination**: Wind-down period; inventory repurchase
7. **Investment protection**: Recoup investments; goodwill
