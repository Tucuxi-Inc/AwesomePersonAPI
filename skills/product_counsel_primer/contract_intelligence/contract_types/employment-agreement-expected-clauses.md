# Employment Agreement - Expected Clauses

## Overview

Employment agreements formalize the relationship between employer and employee, particularly for executives and key employees. They define compensation, duties, protections, and post-employment obligations. This skill documents expected structure and provisions based on analysis of employment agreement samples.

## Agreement Types

| Type | Typical Use | Key Features |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Offer Letter** | Standard employees | Basic terms, at-will |
| **Employment Agreement** | Executives/key employees | Detailed terms, protections |
| **Executive Agreement** | C-suite | Change of control, severance |
| **Independent Contractor** | Non-employees | No benefits, limited control |

## Standard Structure

### Typical Section Order

1. Position and Duties
2. Term and At-Will Status
3. Compensation
4. Benefits

## Required Clauses

### 1. Position and Duties

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Title and reporting
- [ ] Duties description
- [ ] Full-time commitment
- [ ] Location
- [ ] Outside activities

**Example**:
```
POSITION AND DUTIES

Title. Employee shall serve as [Title], reporting to the [CEO/Board].

Duties. Employee shall perform duties customary for such position and
as assigned by [supervisor/Board], including [specific responsibilities].

Commitment. Employee shall devote substantially all business time and
best efforts to the Company. Employee shall not engage in other
employment or business activities without prior written approval.

Location. Employee's primary work location shall be [City, State],
subject to reasonable travel requirements.

Board/Committee Service. [For executives:] Employee shall serve on the
Board of Directors and such committees as the Board determines.
```

### 2. Term and Employment Status

**At-Will Employment**:
```
AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT
Employee's employment is at-will and may be terminated by either party
at any time, with or without cause or notice, subject to the severance
provisions herein. This Agreement does not guarantee employment for
any specific period.
```

**Fixed Term**:
```
TERM
The initial term of employment shall be [3] years from the Effective
Date (the "Initial Term"). The term shall automatically renew for
successive [1]-year periods unless either party provides written
notice of non-renewal at least [90] days before the end of the
then-current term.
```

### 3. Compensation

**Base Salary**:
```
BASE SALARY
Employee shall receive an initial base salary of $[X] per year,
payable in accordance with the Company's standard payroll practices,
less applicable withholdings. Base salary shall be reviewed annually
and may be increased (but not decreased without consent) in the
Company's discretion.
```

**Bonus**:
```
BONUS
Employee shall be eligible for an annual bonus with a target of [X%]
of base salary (the "Target Bonus"), based on achievement of individual
and Company performance objectives established by the [Board/
Compensation Committee]. The actual bonus may range from 0% to [150%]
of the Target Bonus. Bonuses are discretionary and require continued
employment through the payment date.

[Guaranteed Bonus:] For the first year, Employee shall receive a
minimum bonus of $[X] regardless of performance.
```

**Equity Compensation**:
```
EQUITY
Subject to Board approval, Employee shall be granted:

(a) Stock Options: An option to purchase [X] shares at fair market
    value on the grant date, vesting [25%] on the first anniversary
    and [monthly/quarterly] thereafter over [4] years, subject to
    continued employment and the Stock Plan terms.

(b) RSUs: [X] restricted stock units vesting [ratably over 4 years /
    based on performance metrics], subject to continued employment.

Equity grants shall be governed by the Company's Equity Incentive Plan
and individual award agreements.
```

**Signing Bonus**:
```
SIGNING BONUS
Employee shall receive a signing bonus of $[X], payable within [30]
days of the start date. If Employee voluntarily terminates employment
or is terminated for Cause within [12] months, Employee shall repay
a pro-rata portion of the signing bonus.
```

### 4. Benefits

```
BENEFITS
Employee shall be eligible to participate in all benefit programs
generally available to similarly situated employees, including:
(a) health, dental, and vision insurance; (b) life and disability
insurance; (c) 401(k) plan; (d) paid time off per Company policy.

[Executive Perks:]
Employee shall also receive: (a) [car allowance / company vehicle];
(b) [club membership]; (c) [financial planning services]; (d)
[executive health program].
```

### 5. Confidentiality

```
CONFIDENTIALITY
Employee shall maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential
Information during and after employment. "Confidential Information"
includes all non-public information relating to the Company's business,
including trade secrets, customer information, financial data, business
plans, and technical information.

Upon termination, Employee shall return all Company property and
Confidential Information and shall certify compliance in writing.

This obligation survives termination indefinitely for trade secrets
and for [2] years for other Confidential Information.
```

### 6. Intellectual Property Assignment

```
INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
Employee assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in
any Inventions (ideas, discoveries, inventions, improvements, works
of authorship, software) conceived, developed, or reduced to practice
by Employee during employment, whether alone or with others, that:
(a) relate to the Company's business or actual/anticipated research;
(b) result from work performed for the Company; or (c) are developed
using Company resources.

Employee shall promptly disclose all Inventions and execute all
documents necessary to perfect the Company's ownership. This assignment
includes all patents, copyrights, and other IP rights worldwide.

Works made within the scope of employment are "works made for hire."
To the extent not works for hire, Employee assigns all rights.

[State-specific exclusions:] This Agreement does not apply to
Inventions developed entirely on Employee's own time without Company
resources that do not relate to Company business (per [State] law).
```

### 7. Restrictive Covenants

**Non-Competition**:
```
NON-COMPETITION
During employment and for [12] months following termination (the
"Restricted Period"), Employee shall not, directly or indirectly,
engage in, be employed by, consult for, or have a financial interest
in any Competing Business within the Territory.

"Competing Business" means [definition].
"Territory" means [geographic scope].

[Garden Leave:] During the Restricted Period following termination,
Company shall continue to pay Employee's base salary as consideration
for the non-compete obligation.
```

**Non-Solicitation**:
```
NON-SOLICITATION
During the Restricted Period, Employee shall not:

(a) Customers: Solicit business from, or provide services to, any
    customer or prospective customer with whom Employee had contact
    during the [24] months preceding termination;

(b) Employees: Solicit, recruit, or hire any Company employee with
    whom Employee worked or about whom Employee received confidential
    information. General public advertisements are not prohibited.
```

## Termination Provisions

### 8. Termination by Company

**For Cause**:
```
TERMINATION FOR CAUSE
The Company may terminate Employee for Cause immediately upon notice.
"Cause" means: (a) material breach of this Agreement not cured within
[30] days; (b) conviction of or plea to a felony or crime of moral
turpitude; (c) fraud, dishonesty, or gross misconduct; (d) willful
failure to perform duties after written notice; (e) breach of
fiduciary duty; (f) material violation of Company policy.

Upon termination for Cause, Employee shall receive only: (a) accrued
base salary through termination; (b) accrued but unused PTO; (c)
vested benefits. No severance or bonus shall be payable.
```

**Without Cause**:
```
TERMINATION WITHOUT CAUSE
The Company may terminate Employee without Cause upon [30] days notice
(or pay in lieu). Upon such termination, Employee shall receive the
Severance Benefits described below.
```

### 9. Termination by Employee

**For Good Reason**:
```
GOOD REASON
Employee may terminate for Good Reason, which means: (a) material
reduction in base salary or target bonus; (b) material diminution
in duties, title, or reporting; (c) relocation of more than [50]
miles; (d) material breach by Company not cured within [30] days.

Employee must provide notice within [90] days of the triggering event.
Company has [30] days to cure. If not cured, Employee may terminate
within [30] days after the cure period. Termination for Good Reason
entitles Employee to Severance Benefits.
```

**Voluntary Resignation**:
```
Employee may resign at any time upon [30] days written notice.
The Company may waive the notice period and accelerate the
termination date. Voluntary resignation without Good Reason does
not entitle Employee to severance.
```

### 10. Severance Benefits

```
SEVERANCE
Upon termination by Company without Cause or by Employee for Good
Reason, Employee shall receive (subject to Release):

(a) Severance Pay: [12] months of base salary, paid in installments
    per normal payroll or in a lump sum;
(b) Bonus: Pro-rata bonus for the year of termination based on
    actual performance;
(c) Benefits: [12] months of COBRA premium reimbursement;
(d) Equity: [Accelerated vesting of [X] months / treatment per Plan].

"Release" means execution of a general release of claims in a form
provided by the Company, which becomes irrevocable within [60] days
of termination.
```

### 11. Change of Control

```
CHANGE OF CONTROL
Upon a Change of Control, [all/50%] of Employee's unvested equity
shall accelerate.

"Double Trigger": If Employee is terminated without Cause or resigns
for Good Reason within [12] months following a Change of Control,
Employee shall receive:
(a) [18] months severance (vs. standard [12]);
(b) Full acceleration of all unvested equity;
(c) [18] months COBRA reimbursement;
(d) Target bonus for year of termination (vs. pro-rata).

"Change of Control" means: (a) acquisition of [50%]+ voting securities;
(b) merger where shareholders own less than [50%] after; (c) sale of
substantially all assets; (d) change in Board majority.
```

### 12. Section 280G Safe Harbor

```
SECTION 280G
If any payments would constitute "parachute payments" under Section
280G, they shall be reduced to the Safe Harbor Amount if reduction
would result in greater after-tax benefit to Employee. Otherwise,
Employee shall receive full payments and be responsible for excise tax.

[Alternative: Gross-up] Company shall pay Employee an additional
amount to offset any excise tax under Section 4999.
```

## General Provisions

### 13. Representations

```
REPRESENTATIONS
Employee represents that: (a) Employee is not bound by any agreement
that would prevent performance of duties; (b) Employee will not bring
or use any confidential information from prior employers; (c) Employee
will comply with all Company policies.
```

### 14. Dispute Resolution

```
DISPUTES
Any dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration under [AAA/JAMS]
rules in [City]. The arbitrator may award attorneys' fees to the
prevailing party. This Agreement shall be governed by [State] law.
```

## Key Decision Points

1. **At-Will vs. Term**: Fixed term or at-will?
2. **Severance Triggers**: Cause/Good Reason definitions?
3. **Severance Amount**: Months of pay and benefits?
4. **Equity Treatment**: Acceleration on termination/CIC?
5. **Non-Compete Scope**: Geographic and time limits?
6. **Non-Compete Consideration**: Garden leave?
7. **Change of Control**: Single or double trigger?
8. **280G Treatment**: Cut-back, gross-up, or best net?
9. **Arbitration**: Mandatory ADR?
10. **Clawback**: Bonus/equity recovery provisions?

## Red Flags - Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Risk Level | Implication |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| No Cause definition | HIGH | Disputes on termination |
| No Good Reason | MEDIUM | Employee trapped |
| Vague non-compete | HIGH | Unenforceable |
| No IP assignment | HIGH | Ownership unclear |
| No release requirement | MEDIUM | Severance without protection |
| No CIC protections | MEDIUM | Vulnerable in M&A |
| Missing 280G | MEDIUM | Tax surprise |
| No severance cap | MEDIUM | Excessive exposure |
