# Services Agreement - Expected Clauses

## Overview

Professional Services Agreements govern consulting, development, and implementation engagements. They typically consist of a Master Services Agreement (MSA) that establishes general terms and Statements of Work (SOWs) that define specific projects. Based on analysis of 100+ services agreement samples, this skill documents expected structure and provisions.

## Agreement Structure Types

### Master Services Agreement (MSA) + SOW
- MSA: General terms applicable to all engagements
- SOW: Project-specific scope, deliverables, pricing
- Most common for ongoing relationships

### Standalone Professional Services Agreement (PSA)
- Single agreement for specific engagement
- Combines general terms and project scope
- Used for one-time projects

### Consulting Agreement
- Similar to PSA, often for individual consultants
- May include employee-like terms (non-compete, assignment of inventions)

## Required Clauses

### 1. Scope of Services

**Must Include**:
- [ ] General description of services
- [ ] Reference to SOW for specifics
- [ ] Change order process
- [ ] Out-of-scope handling

**Example**:
```
Provider shall perform the professional services ("Services") described
in each Statement of Work executed by the parties. Each SOW shall
describe the scope, deliverables, timeline, and fees. Any changes to
the scope shall be documented in a written Change Order signed by
both parties.
```

### 2. Fees and Payment

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Fee structure (T&M, fixed price, or hybrid)
- [ ] Expense reimbursement
- [ ] Invoicing schedule
- [ ] Payment terms
- [ ] Late payment consequences

**Time & Materials**:
```
Customer shall pay Provider at the hourly rates specified in the SOW.
Provider shall invoice monthly for time worked. Customer shall
reimburse reasonable, pre-approved travel and expenses. Payment is
due within thirty (30) days of invoice.
```

**Fixed Price**:
```
Customer shall pay the fixed fee specified in the SOW according to
the milestone payment schedule. Payments are due within thirty (30)
days of milestone acceptance.
```

### 3. Intellectual Property Ownership

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Pre-existing IP definition
- [ ] Work product ownership
- [ ] License grants (if any)
- [ ] Customer data ownership

**Customer Owns (Common)**:
```
All work product created specifically for Customer under this Agreement
("Deliverables") shall be the sole property of Customer. Provider
assigns all rights in Deliverables to Customer.

Provider shall retain ownership of all pre-existing materials,
methodologies, and tools ("Provider IP"). Provider grants Customer
a perpetual, royalty-free license to use Provider IP solely as
incorporated into the Deliverables.
```

**Provider Owns (Alternative)**:
```
Provider shall own all Deliverables and intellectual property created
under this Agreement. Subject to payment, Customer shall have a
non-exclusive license to use the Deliverables for Customer's internal
business purposes.
```

### 4. Acceptance and Milestones

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Acceptance criteria
- [ ] Acceptance period
- [ ] Rejection process
- [ ] Deemed acceptance

**Example**:
```
Customer shall have [fifteen (15)] days from delivery of each
Deliverable to evaluate against the Acceptance Criteria. If the
Deliverable does not meet the Criteria, Customer shall notify
Provider in writing. Provider shall correct deficiencies within
[ten (10)] days. If Customer does not provide notice within the
acceptance period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.
```

### 5. Customer Responsibilities

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Access and resources
- [ ] Decision-making authority
- [ ] Information and materials
- [ ] Cooperation

**Example**:
```
Customer shall: (a) provide timely access to personnel, facilities,
and systems as reasonably necessary; (b) designate a project manager
with authority to make binding decisions; (c) provide complete and
accurate information and materials; (d) respond to Provider requests
within [five (5)] business days.
```

### 6. Warranty

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Services warranty (workmanlike manner)
- [ ] Warranty period
- [ ] Remedy for breach
- [ ] Disclaimer of other warranties

**Example**:
```
Provider warrants that Services will be performed in a professional,
workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards. If Services
fail to meet this warranty, Customer must notify Provider within
[30] days of performance. Provider's sole obligation is to re-perform
the non-conforming Services at no additional charge.

EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH ABOVE, PROVIDER DISCLAIMS ALL WARRANTIES,
EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY AND
FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.
```

### 7. Limitation of Liability

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Cap (typically fees for specific project)
- [ ] Consequential damages exclusion
- [ ] Carve-outs

**Example**:
```
EXCEPT FOR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY,
OR GROSS NEGLIGENCE, PROVIDER'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER ANY SOW
SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID FOR THE SPECIFIC SOW GIVING RISE TO
THE CLAIM. NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES.
```

### 8. Independent Contractor

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Independent contractor relationship
- [ ] No employee benefits
- [ ] Tax responsibility
- [ ] No agency authority

**Example**:
```
Provider is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent,
partner, or joint venturer of Customer. Provider is responsible for
all taxes. Provider's personnel shall not be entitled to any Customer
employee benefits. Neither party may bind the other to any obligation.
```

## Common Clauses

### 9. Non-Solicitation

**Usually Present**:
```
During the term and for [twelve (12)] months thereafter, neither
party shall solicit for employment any employee of the other party
who was directly involved in the engagement. A party may hire an
employee who responds to a general job posting.
```

### 10. Insurance Requirements

**Usually Present**:
```
Provider shall maintain: (a) Commercial General Liability insurance
of at least [AMOUNT] per occurrence; (b) Professional Liability
(E&O) insurance of at least [AMOUNT]; (c) Workers' Compensation
as required by law. Provider shall provide certificates upon request.
```

### 11. Background Checks

**Usually Present** (Especially for on-site work):
```
Provider shall conduct background checks on all personnel assigned
to Customer's facilities or with access to Customer's systems, and
shall ensure that such personnel meet Customer's security requirements.
```

### 12. Conduct on Premises

**Usually Present**:
```
Provider personnel shall comply with Customer's reasonable workplace
policies, security requirements, and safety procedures while on
Customer's premises.
```

## Optional Clauses

### 13. Key Personnel

For critical resources:
```
The Key Personnel identified in the SOW shall be assigned to the
project. Provider shall not remove Key Personnel without Customer's
prior consent. If Key Personnel become unavailable, Provider shall
promptly propose qualified replacements for Customer approval.
```

### 14. Exclusivity

For dedicated resources:
```
During the project, Provider shall dedicate the identified personnel
exclusively to Customer's engagement and shall not assign them to
other projects.
```

### 15. Staff Augmentation Terms

When providing embedded staff:
```
Provider personnel assigned under this SOW shall work under Customer's
direction and supervision. Customer shall provide day-to-day task
assignments. Provider remains responsible for employment matters.
```

### 16. Transition Assistance

For wind-down:
```
Upon termination or expiration, Provider shall provide transition
assistance for up to [60] days at Provider's then-current rates to
facilitate orderly transition to Customer or a successor provider.
```

## Red Flags - Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Risk Level | Implication |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| No scope definition | HIGH | Unlimited work expected |
| No change order process | HIGH | Scope creep without compensation |
| No IP ownership clarity | HIGH | Disputes over deliverables |
| No acceptance criteria | MEDIUM | Subjective acceptance |
| No customer responsibilities | MEDIUM | Delays without recourse |
| No limitation of liability | HIGH | Unlimited exposure |
| No termination rights | HIGH | Cannot exit engagement |

## Fixed Price vs. Time & Materials

| Aspect | Fixed Price | Time & Materials |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Scope** | Well-defined, limited changes | Flexible, evolving |
| **Risk Allocation** | Provider bears overrun risk | Customer bears overrun risk |
| **Customer Control** | Less visibility during project | More visibility and control |
| **Change Management** | Formal change orders | Continuous adjustment |
| **Budget Certainty** | High (if scope stable) | Lower (depends on effort) |
| **Provider Incentive** | Efficiency (complete faster) | Thoroughness (bill more hours) |

## SOW Best Practices

### Essential SOW Elements
1. **Scope**: Detailed description of services
2. **Deliverables**: Specific, measurable outputs
3. **Acceptance Criteria**: Objective standards for acceptance
4. **Timeline**: Milestones with dates
5. **Fees**: Pricing and payment schedule
6. **Assumptions**: Basis for estimate
7. **Customer Dependencies**: Required customer inputs
8. **Out of Scope**: Explicitly excluded items

### SOW Checklist
- [ ] Clear deliverable descriptions
- [ ] Measurable acceptance criteria
- [ ] Realistic timeline with dependencies
- [ ] All assumptions documented
- [ ] Change order process defined
- [ ] Customer responsibilities listed
- [ ] Out-of-scope items identified
- [ ] Fees tied to milestones (if fixed price)

## Negotiation Priorities

### Provider Priorities
1. Clear scope boundaries
2. Change order process for scope changes
3. Customer cooperation obligations
4. Milestone-based payment (fixed price)
5. IP ownership or broad license rights
6. Liability cap at project fees

### Customer Priorities
1. Acceptance testing with clear criteria
2. Right to terminate for convenience
3. IP ownership of deliverables
4. Performance warranties
5. Key personnel provisions
6. Price caps or estimates (T&M)
7. Transition assistance
