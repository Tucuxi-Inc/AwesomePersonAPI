# Partnership/Joint Venture Agreement - Expected Clauses

## Overview

Partnership and joint venture agreements establish collaborative business arrangements between independent parties. They range from informal strategic alliances to formal joint ventures with separate legal entities. This skill documents expected structure and provisions based on analysis of partnership agreement samples.

## Agreement Types

| Type | Formality | Entity | Risk Sharing |
|------|-----------|--------|--------------|
| **Strategic Alliance** | Low | No new entity | Limited |
| **Joint Development** | Medium | No new entity | Project-specific |
| **Contractual JV** | Medium | No new entity | Shared by contract |
| **Equity JV** | High | New entity formed | Based on ownership |
| **General Partnership** | High | Partnership entity | Full joint liability |

## Required Clauses

### 1. Purpose and Scope

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Business purpose/objective
- [ ] Scope of collaboration
- [ ] Territory (if applicable)
- [ ] Exclusions

**Example**:
```
PURPOSE
The parties enter into this Agreement to [jointly develop / market /
manufacture] [Product/Service] in the [Territory].

SCOPE
The collaboration includes: (a) [development activities]; (b) [marketing
activities]; (c) [sales and distribution]. Activities outside this
scope require separate written agreement.
```

### 2. Contributions

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Each party's contributions
- [ ] Valuation of contributions
- [ ] Timing of contributions
- [ ] Consequences of failure to contribute

**Example**:
```
CONTRIBUTIONS

Party A shall contribute:
(a) [Technology/IP] valued at $[X]
(b) [Personnel]: [X] FTEs dedicated to the project
(c) [Funding]: $[X] over [term]

Party B shall contribute:
(a) [Manufacturing capability]
(b) [Distribution network]
(c) [Funding]: $[X] over [term]

Failure to make required contributions constitutes material breach.
```

### 3. Governance and Decision-Making

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Governance structure
- [ ] Voting rights
- [ ] Reserved matters requiring unanimous consent
- [ ] Deadlock resolution

**Example**:
```
GOVERNANCE

Steering Committee. The parties shall establish a Steering Committee
consisting of [2] representatives from each party. The Committee shall
meet [quarterly] and oversee strategic direction.

Day-to-Day Management. [Party A / Joint Program Manager] shall manage
daily operations per the approved Project Plan.

Voting. Decisions require [majority / unanimous] approval. Reserved
matters requiring unanimous consent include:
(a) Material scope changes
(b) Additional funding requirements exceeding [X%]
(c) Assignment or sublicensing
(d) Related party transactions

Deadlock. If the Committee cannot reach consensus on a material matter
after [30] days, the matter shall escalate to each party's [CEO].
If still unresolved after [30] days, either party may invoke the
dispute resolution procedures.
```

### 4. Financial Provisions

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Funding obligations
- [ ] Cost allocation
- [ ] Revenue/profit sharing
- [ ] Accounting and reporting

**Example**:
```
COSTS
Development costs shall be shared [50/50 / per the budget].
Each party bears its own overhead and personnel costs unless
otherwise agreed.

REVENUE SHARING
Net revenues shall be allocated as follows:
- Party A: [X%]
- Party B: [X%]

"Net Revenue" means gross revenue less: (a) cost of goods sold;
(b) direct sales expenses; (c) third-party royalties.

ACCOUNTING
The parties shall maintain separate books for the collaboration.
Each party shall provide quarterly financial reports. Annual
financial review by independent accountant.
```

### 5. Intellectual Property

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Background IP ownership
- [ ] Foreground/developed IP ownership
- [ ] License grants
- [ ] Improvement allocation

**Example**:
```
BACKGROUND IP
Each party retains ownership of its Background IP (IP existing before
this Agreement or developed outside the scope). Each party grants
the other a non-exclusive license to use its Background IP solely
for the purposes of this collaboration.

FOREGROUND IP
IP jointly developed under this Agreement ("Joint IP") shall be
jointly owned. Each party may use Joint IP without the other's
consent but may not license to third parties without consent.

IP developed solely by one party, even using the other's Background IP,
shall be owned by the developing party, subject to appropriate licenses.

IMPROVEMENTS
Improvements to a party's Background IP shall be owned by that party.
Improvements to Joint IP shall be Joint IP.
```

### 6. Exclusivity and Non-Compete

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Scope of exclusivity
- [ ] Duration
- [ ] Exceptions
- [ ] Consequences of breach

**Example**:
```
EXCLUSIVITY
During the term, neither party shall, directly or indirectly:
(a) develop competing products in the Field;
(b) collaborate with third parties on competing products;
(c) license its Background IP to competitors for competing purposes.

Exceptions: (a) existing agreements disclosed in Exhibit [X];
(b) activities outside the defined Field; (c) general research
not specifically targeted at competing products.
```

## Common Clauses

### 7. Program Management

```
PROJECT PLAN
The parties shall develop a Project Plan within [30] days of execution,
including: milestones, deliverables, timelines, resource allocation,
and budget. The Steering Committee shall approve material changes.

PROGRAM MANAGER
Each party shall designate a Program Manager responsible for day-to-day
coordination. Program Managers shall meet [weekly] and report to the
Steering Committee.
```

### 8. Personnel

```
DEDICATED PERSONNEL
Each party shall assign qualified personnel as specified in Exhibit [X].
Key Personnel may not be reassigned without [30] days notice and
reasonable transition.

SECONDMENT
Personnel seconded to the collaboration remain employees of their
respective parties. The receiving party shall provide workspace
and tools. The employing party remains responsible for compensation
and benefits.
```

### 9. Records and Reporting

```
Each party shall maintain accurate records of activities and expenditures.
Each party shall provide the other with [monthly] progress reports and
[quarterly] financial reports. All records shall be retained for [X]
years and available for audit.
```

### 10. Exit and Dissolution

```
DISSOLUTION
Upon termination or expiration:
(a) Wind down ongoing activities per a mutually agreed plan
(b) Return or destroy Confidential Information
(c) Allocate remaining assets per contribution ratios
(d) Settle outstanding financial obligations

BUYOUT OPTION
Upon termination by Party A for Party B's breach, Party A may
purchase Party B's interest in Joint IP at fair market value
determined by independent appraiser.
```

## Optional Clauses

### 11. Put/Call Rights (Equity JV)

```
PUT RIGHT
After [3] years, either party may require the other to purchase its
interest at fair market value upon [180] days notice.

CALL RIGHT
If a party breaches, the non-breaching party may purchase the breaching
party's interest at [80%] of fair market value.
```

### 12. Anti-Dilution

```
If the JV entity issues additional equity, each party shall have
the right to participate pro rata to maintain its ownership percentage.
```

### 13. Tag-Along / Drag-Along

```
TAG-ALONG
If Party A receives an offer to sell its interest to a third party,
Party B may participate in the sale on the same terms.

DRAG-ALONG
If parties holding [X%] agree to sell to a third party, they may
require remaining parties to sell on the same terms.
```

## Red Flags - Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Risk Level | Implication |
|-----------------|------------|-------------|
| Unclear purpose/scope | HIGH | Misaligned expectations |
| No IP ownership | HIGH | Ownership disputes |
| No governance structure | HIGH | Decision-making paralysis |
| No deadlock resolution | HIGH | Stalemate persists |
| Missing exit provisions | HIGH | Trapped in relationship |
| No non-compete | MEDIUM | Competitive conflicts |
| Vague contributions | HIGH | Unclear obligations |
| No financial terms | HIGH | Profit/cost disputes |

## Negotiation Priorities

### Larger Party Priorities
1. Control over key decisions (veto rights)
2. IP ownership of developed technology
3. Exit flexibility
4. Limitation of contributions/liability
5. Non-compete protection

### Smaller Party Priorities
1. Meaningful governance participation
2. Access to larger party's resources
3. IP rights and licenses
4. Fair revenue/profit sharing
5. Protection from termination
6. Buyout rights at fair value
