# Non-Compete and Restrictive Covenants Taxonomy

## Overview

Non-compete and restrictive covenant provisions limit a party's ability to compete, solicit customers/employees, or engage with competitors. They appear in employment, M&A, partnership, distribution, and services agreements. Enforceability varies significantly by jurisdiction and relationship type. This taxonomy catalogs restrictive covenant patterns observed across commercial agreements.

## Covenant Types

### A. Non-Competition

**Description**: Prohibition on engaging in competitive business activities.

```
NON-COMPETITION
During the term and for [X] months thereafter, [Party] shall not,
directly or indirectly: (a) engage in a Competing Business; (b) own,
manage, operate, or control a Competing Business; (c) be employed by
or consult for a Competing Business; (d) have a financial interest in
a Competing Business (excluding passive investments of less than [5%]
in publicly traded securities).
```

### B. Non-Solicitation of Customers

**Description**: Prohibition on soliciting the other party's customers.

```
NON-SOLICITATION OF CUSTOMERS
During the term and for [X] months thereafter, [Party] shall not,
directly or indirectly, solicit or attempt to solicit business from
any Customer with whom [Party] had contact or about whom [Party]
received Confidential Information during the relationship.
```

### C. Non-Solicitation of Employees

**Description**: Prohibition on recruiting the other party's employees.

```
NON-SOLICITATION OF EMPLOYEES
During the term and for [X] months thereafter, neither party shall,
directly or indirectly, solicit for employment or hire any employee
of the other party who was involved in the performance of this Agreement.
This restriction shall not apply to: (a) general public advertisements;
(b) unsolicited applications; (c) employees who have been terminated
by the other party for [6] months or more.
```

### D. Exclusivity

**Description**: Exclusive dealing or supplier arrangements.

```
EXCLUSIVITY
During the term, Customer shall purchase [Product] exclusively from
Vendor and shall not purchase competing products from any third party.
Vendor shall supply [Product] to Customer on a non-exclusive basis
unless otherwise agreed.
```

### E. Non-Interference

**Description**: Prohibition on interfering with business relationships.

```
NON-INTERFERENCE
[Party] shall not, directly or indirectly, interfere with, disrupt,
or attempt to disrupt any business relationship between [Other Party]
and its customers, suppliers, vendors, or business partners.
```

## Scope Limitations

### 1. Geographic Scope

```
GEOGRAPHIC SCOPE
This restriction applies within: [choose one]
(a) [City/County/State]
(b) The United States
(c) Worldwide
(d) Any geographic area in which [Company] conducts business
(e) Any territory in which [Party] performed services during employment
```

**Enforceability Note**: Overly broad geographic restrictions are often unenforceable.

### 2. Temporal Scope

```
DURATION
This restriction applies: [choose one]
(a) During the term only
(b) During the term and for [6/12/18/24] months thereafter
(c) For [X] years following termination for any reason
(d) For [X] years following termination, reduced to [Y] months if
    terminated without cause
```

**Market Standards**:
- Employment: 6-24 months (jurisdiction dependent)
- M&A (seller): 2-5 years
- Distribution: Duration of term plus 6-12 months
- Partnership: 1-3 years post-dissolution

### 3. Activity Scope

```
COMPETING BUSINESS DEFINITION
"Competing Business" means any business that: (a) develops, manufactures,
markets, or sells [specific products/services]; (b) competes with
[Company's] business as conducted during the term.

Exclusions: [Party] may: (a) invest passively in public securities;
(b) serve on boards of non-competing entities; (c) engage in
[specified activities].
```

### 4. Customer Scope

```
PROTECTED CUSTOMERS
This non-solicitation applies to: [choose one]
(a) All customers of [Company]
(b) Customers with whom [Party] had material contact
(c) Customers about whom [Party] received Confidential Information
(d) Customers listed in Exhibit [X]
(e) Customers during the [24] months preceding termination
```

## Context-Specific Provisions

### Employment Context

```
EMPLOYEE NON-COMPETE
Employee agrees that during employment and for [12] months following
termination for any reason, Employee shall not: (a) work for a
Competitor in a role substantially similar to Employee's role at
Company; (b) solicit Company's customers with whom Employee had
contact; (c) solicit Company's employees for employment.

In consideration of these restrictions, Company shall pay Employee
[garden leave / severance / other consideration] equal to [X].
```

**Enforceability Varies By State**:
- California: Generally unenforceable (except for sale of business)
- Some states: Require consideration beyond at-will employment
- Most states: Must be reasonable in scope

### M&A Context (Seller Non-Compete)

```
SELLER NON-COMPETE
For [5] years following the Closing Date, Seller and its principals
shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate,
control, or provide services to any business that competes with the
Business as conducted at Closing, anywhere in [Territory].

This restriction is a material inducement to Buyer and is supported
by adequate consideration (the Purchase Price).
```

### Distribution/Reseller Context

```
EXCLUSIVE DEALING
During the term: (a) Distributor shall not distribute products that
compete with the Products; (b) Supplier shall not appoint other
distributors in the Territory. Post-termination, Distributor shall
not solicit Supplier's other customers for [12] months.
```

### Partnership/JV Context

```
PARTNER NON-COMPETE
During the term and for [2] years following dissolution, no Partner
shall: (a) engage in a business competitive with the Partnership
within the Territory; (b) solicit the Partnership's customers;
(c) hire Partnership employees. Partners may engage in businesses
outside the Partnership's defined scope.
```

## Enforceability Considerations

### 1. Legitimate Business Interest

```
The parties acknowledge that [Company] has legitimate business interests
in protecting: (a) Confidential Information and trade secrets;
(b) customer relationships and goodwill; (c) investment in employee
training; (d) the value of the transaction. These restrictions are
reasonably necessary to protect such interests.
```

### 2. Reasonableness

```
The parties agree that the scope, duration, and geographic limitations
of these restrictions are reasonable and necessary. If any restriction
is deemed unenforceable, the parties request that the court modify
(rather than void) the restriction to the minimum extent necessary
for enforceability.
```

### 3. Blue Pencil / Reformation

```
REFORMATION
If any court determines that any restriction is unenforceable as
written, the parties authorize the court to reform such provision
to the maximum extent enforceable, and as reformed, such provision
shall remain in full force and effect.
```

### 4. Injunctive Relief

```
INJUNCTIVE RELIEF
[Party] acknowledges that breach of these restrictions would cause
irreparable harm not adequately compensable by monetary damages.
[Company] shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific
performance, and other equitable remedies without posting bond,
in addition to all other remedies available at law or equity.
```

## Exceptions and Carve-Outs

```
EXCEPTIONS
Notwithstanding the foregoing, [Party] may:
(a) Own up to [5%] of publicly traded securities of a Competitor;
(b) Respond to general public job advertisements;
(c) Provide services to Competitors in areas unrelated to [Company's]
    business, provided no Confidential Information is used or disclosed;
(d) Engage in activities approved in writing by [Company].
```

## Key Decision Points

1. **Covenant Types**: Non-compete, non-solicit, or both?
2. **Geographic Scope**: Local, national, or global?
3. **Duration**: How long post-termination?
4. **Activity Definition**: How to define "competing"?
5. **Customer Scope**: All customers or limited subset?
6. **Employee Scope**: All employees or key personnel?
7. **Consideration**: What supports the restriction?
8. **Enforcement**: Injunction, damages, or both?
9. **Reformation**: Allow court modification?
10. **Jurisdiction**: What law governs enforceability?

## Jurisdiction Considerations

| Jurisdiction | Employee Non-Compete | Sale of Business | Notes |
|--------------|---------------------|------------------|-------|
| California | Unenforceable | Enforceable | Bus. & Prof. Code § 16600 |
| New York | Disfavored; narrow | Enforceable | Must protect legitimate interest |
| Texas | Enforceable if reasonable | Enforceable | Must be ancillary to agreement |
| Delaware | Generally enforceable | Enforceable | Reasonable restrictions |
| Massachusetts | Restricted | Enforceable | Garden leave may be required |

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Overbroad scope**: Geographic or activity scope too wide
2. **Excessive duration**: Longer than necessary
3. **No legitimate interest**: Restriction protects nothing
4. **Inadequate consideration**: No value exchanged
5. **Wrong jurisdiction**: Unenforceable where party resides
6. **Vague definitions**: "Competitor" undefined
7. **No reformation clause**: Entire provision voided
8. **Missing injunction language**: Damages inadequate remedy

## Sample Provisions

### Balanced (Employment)
```
NON-COMPETE
For [12] months following termination, Employee shall not work for
a direct Competitor in a substantially similar role involving
[specific function] within [State]. Employee may work for Competitors
in unrelated roles. Company shall provide [6] months base salary as
consideration if enforcing this restriction following termination
without cause.
```

### Narrow Non-Solicitation Only
```
NON-SOLICITATION
For [18] months following termination, [Party] shall not: (a) solicit
customers with whom [Party] had direct contact for business competing
with [Company]; (b) solicit [Company] employees who reported to [Party]
or worked on the same team. General advertising is permitted.
```

### M&A Seller (Broad)
```
Seller covenants that for [5] years following Closing, Seller and
its affiliates shall not directly or indirectly compete with the
Business anywhere in [Territory], solicit any customers or employees
of the Business, or interfere with the Business's relationships.
This covenant is a material part of the Purchase Price allocation.
```
