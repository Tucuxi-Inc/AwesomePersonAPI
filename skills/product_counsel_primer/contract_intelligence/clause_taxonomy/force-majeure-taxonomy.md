# Force Majeure Taxonomy

## Overview

Force majeure clauses excuse performance when extraordinary events beyond a party's control prevent fulfillment of contractual obligations. Post-COVID, these provisions receive heightened scrutiny and negotiation. This taxonomy catalogs force majeure patterns observed across commercial agreements.

## Core Elements

### A. Definition of Force Majeure Event

**Enumerated Events**
```
"Force Majeure Event" means any event beyond the reasonable control
of the affected party, including but not limited to:
(a) acts of God, including earthquake, flood, hurricane, or tornado;
(b) war, invasion, armed conflict, or terrorist attack;
(c) government action, including embargo, sanctions, or regulation;
(d) labor disputes, including strike or lockout;
(e) pandemic, epidemic, or quarantine;
(f) failure of third-party telecommunications or power supply;
(g) fire, explosion, or other casualty.
```

**Catch-All with Standards**
```
"Force Majeure Event" means any cause beyond the reasonable control
of the affected party that could not have been avoided or mitigated
through the exercise of reasonable diligence.
```

### B. Common Force Majeure Events

| Category | Specific Events |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Natural Disasters** | Earthquake, flood, hurricane, tornado, volcanic eruption, tsunami |
| **Weather** | Severe storms, extreme temperatures, drought |
| **War/Conflict** | War, invasion, hostilities, civil war, terrorism, insurrection |
| **Government Action** | Embargo, sanctions, regulation, expropriation, permit denial |
| **Labor** | Strike, lockout, work stoppage, labor shortage |
| **Health** | Pandemic, epidemic, quarantine, public health emergency |
| **Infrastructure** | Power failure, telecommunications failure, internet outage |
| **Casualty** | Fire, explosion, structural collapse |
| **Supply Chain** | Shortage of materials, supplier failure, transportation disruption |

### C. Events Typically EXCLUDED

```
Force Majeure Event shall NOT include:
(a) events caused by the affected party's negligence or misconduct;
(b) economic hardship or changes in market conditions;
(c) currency fluctuations or inability to pay;
(d) changes in law that make performance more expensive;
(e) labor disputes involving only the affected party's employees;
(f) failure of the party's subcontractors or suppliers (unless such
    failure is itself caused by a Force Majeure Event);
(g) events that were foreseeable at the time of contracting.
```

**Key Exclusions Post-COVID**:
- Pandemics/epidemics (now foreseeable; often carved out)
- Economic conditions
- Financial inability to perform
- Foreseeable government actions

## Operative Provisions

### A. Excuse of Performance

**Standard Excuse**
```
Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing
its obligations (other than payment obligations) to the extent such
failure or delay results from a Force Majeure Event.
```

**Payment Obligations**
Force majeure typically does NOT excuse payment obligations:
```
Notwithstanding the foregoing, Force Majeure shall not excuse
Customer's obligation to pay amounts due under this Agreement.
```

### B. Notice Requirements

```
The affected party shall provide written notice to the other party
within [five (5)] days of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event,
specifying:
(a) the nature of the Force Majeure Event;
(b) the expected duration and impact on performance;
(c) the steps being taken to mitigate the effects.

The affected party shall provide updates at least [weekly] and
prompt notice when the Force Majeure Event ends.
```

### C. Mitigation Obligations

```
The affected party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to:
(a) mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event;
(b) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable;
(c) perform obligations not affected by the Force Majeure Event.
```

### D. Proportional Relief

```
The affected party's obligations shall be suspended only to the
extent and for the duration that such performance is prevented
by the Force Majeure Event.
```

### E. Allocation of Resources

```
If Vendor's capacity to perform is limited by a Force Majeure Event,
Vendor may allocate its available capacity among its customers in
a fair and non-discriminatory manner.
```

## Termination Rights

### Extended Force Majeure Termination

```
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [ninety (90)] days,
either party may terminate this Agreement upon [thirty (30)] days
written notice. Upon such termination, neither party shall have any
further liability except for:
(a) payment for services rendered prior to termination;
(b) obligations that by their nature survive termination.
```

**Duration Thresholds**:
- 30-60 days: Aggressive; quick exit right
- 90 days: Standard for most commercial agreements
- 180 days: Long-term strategic relationships
- No threshold: No termination right (risk for customer)

### Pro-Rata Refund on Termination

```
If Customer terminates due to an extended Force Majeure Event,
Vendor shall refund pre-paid fees for the period following the
effective date of termination on a pro-rata basis.
```

## Special Considerations

### Pandemic-Specific Language (Post-COVID)

**Pandemic Carve-Out**
```
Notwithstanding the foregoing, "Force Majeure Event" shall not
include the COVID-19 pandemic or any similar pandemic or epidemic,
except to the extent of specific government-mandated closures
issued after the Effective Date directly affecting performance.
```

**Future Pandemic Coverage**
```
"Force Majeure Event" includes a pandemic or epidemic declared by
the World Health Organization or applicable governmental authority,
to the extent such pandemic or epidemic prevents performance and
was not in existence as of the Effective Date.
```

### Essential Services Exception

```
Notwithstanding any Force Majeure Event, Vendor shall maintain
business continuity and disaster recovery capabilities sufficient
to continue providing [essential services/SLA commitments] during
foreseeable disruptions.
```

### Insurance Interaction

```
A party claiming Force Majeure shall cooperate with the other party's
efforts to recover under any applicable insurance and shall assign
proceeds to the extent they relate to the affected obligations.
```

### Customer's Alternative Sourcing

```
If a Force Majeure Event prevents Vendor from performing for more
than [thirty (30)] days, Customer may, without breaching this
Agreement, engage alternative sources to obtain the affected
products or services for the duration of the Force Majeure Event.
```

## Force Majeure by Contract Type

| Contract Type | Key Considerations |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Supply/Manufacturing** | Supply chain disruption; alternative sourcing rights |
| **SaaS/Cloud** | Business continuity expectations; SLA interaction |
| **Services** | Remote work capability; personnel availability |
| **Construction** | Weather delays; permit delays; material shortages |
| **Events** | Cancellation; force majeure refund policies |
| **Leases** | Rent abatement during forced closure |

## Key Decision Points

1. **Enumerated vs. Catch-All**: Specific list or general standard?
2. **Pandemic Coverage**: Include or exclude (post-COVID)?
3. **Payment Obligations**: Excused or not?
4. **Notice Requirements**: Timing and content of notice?
5. **Mitigation Standard**: Reasonable efforts or best efforts?
6. **Termination Threshold**: Duration before termination right?
7. **Refund on Termination**: Pro-rata or forfeiture?
8. **Alternative Sourcing**: Customer right to source elsewhere?
9. **Allocation Rights**: Vendor may allocate limited capacity?
10. **Business Continuity**: Required disaster recovery capabilities?

## Common Pitfalls

1. **No force majeure clause**: Common law frustration/impossibility harder to invoke
2. **Overbroad exclusions**: Too many events excluded; little protection
3. **No notice requirement**: Disputes over when force majeure was claimed
4. **No mitigation obligation**: Party can sit on hands indefinitely
5. **No termination right**: Stuck in suspended contract forever
6. **Payment obligations excused**: Vendor never gets paid
7. **No pandemic language**: Uncertainty post-COVID
8. **No alternative sourcing**: Customer stranded during event
9. **Vague duration threshold**: When can party terminate?
10. **No business continuity expectation**: Vendor makes no effort to prepare

## Sample Provisions

### Standard Force Majeure
```
FORCE MAJEURE
Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing
its obligations (other than payment obligations) caused by events
beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, war, terrorism,
government action, labor disputes, pandemic, or failure of third-party
infrastructure ("Force Majeure Event").

The affected party shall: (a) provide prompt written notice describing
the event and expected duration; (b) use reasonable efforts to mitigate
effects and resume performance; (c) provide updates at least bi-weekly.

If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [90] days, either
party may terminate this Agreement upon [30] days written notice.
Upon such termination, Vendor shall refund pre-paid fees pro-rata.
```

### Customer-Favorable (SaaS)
```
FORCE MAJEURE
Vendor shall not be excused from performance by reason of any Force
Majeure Event unless: (a) Vendor has implemented reasonable business
continuity measures; (b) Vendor provides notice within [48] hours;
(c) Vendor uses best efforts to restore service.

Customer may terminate if service is unavailable for more than
[30] days due to Force Majeure and shall receive a full refund of
pre-paid fees for the affected period.

Vendor's invocation of Force Majeure shall not affect Customer's
right to [SLA credits / terminate for convenience / source alternatives].
```

### Supply Agreement
```
FORCE MAJEURE
If Supplier's ability to deliver is prevented or delayed by a Force
Majeure Event, Supplier shall: (a) notify Buyer within [24] hours;
(b) allocate available inventory fairly among customers; (c) resume
delivery as soon as practicable.

If delivery is delayed more than [30] days, Buyer may: (a) cancel
affected purchase orders without penalty; (b) source from alternative
suppliers; (c) terminate this Agreement upon [15] days notice.

Supplier's Force Majeure claim shall not affect Buyer's right to
warranty claims, returns, or price adjustments.
```
