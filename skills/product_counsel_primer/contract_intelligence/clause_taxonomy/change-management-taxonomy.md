# Change Management Taxonomy

## Overview

Change management provisions establish procedures for modifying project scope, deliverables, timelines, or pricing after contract execution. They balance flexibility to adapt to evolving requirements with protection against scope creep and unauthorized changes. This taxonomy catalogs change control patterns observed across services and project agreements.

## Change Types

### A. Scope Changes

**Description**: Modifications to deliverables, features, or requirements.

```
SCOPE CHANGE
Any modification to the Specifications, deliverables, or scope of work
described in a SOW constitutes a Scope Change requiring a Change Order.
```

### B. Schedule Changes

**Description**: Adjustments to timelines or milestones.

```
SCHEDULE CHANGE
Any modification to milestone dates, delivery schedules, or project
timelines requires mutual agreement documented in a Change Order.
```

### C. Pricing Changes

**Description**: Adjustments to fees or rate structures.

```
PRICING CHANGE
Fees may be adjusted only by written amendment. Rate increases shall
not exceed [X%] annually and require [60] days notice.
```

### D. Regulatory/Compliance Changes

**Description**: Changes required by law or regulation.

```
REGULATORY CHANGE
If changes to applicable law require modifications to the Services or
Deliverables, Vendor shall notify Customer and provide a Change Order.
Customer shall not unreasonably withhold approval for regulatory changes.
```

## Change Order Process

### 1. Change Request

```
CHANGE REQUEST
Either party may request a change by submitting a written Change Request
describing: (a) the proposed change in reasonable detail; (b) the
reason for the change; (c) the requested implementation date.
```

### 2. Impact Assessment

```
IMPACT ASSESSMENT
Within [10] business days of receiving a Change Request, Vendor shall
provide an Impact Assessment including: (a) technical feasibility;
(b) impact on scope, schedule, and fees; (c) estimated additional costs;
(d) impact on other deliverables or milestones.
```

### 3. Change Order Approval

```
CHANGE ORDER
No change shall be effective until documented in a written Change Order
signed by authorized representatives of both parties. The Change Order
shall specify: (a) the change description; (b) revised Specifications
(if applicable); (c) fee adjustment; (d) schedule adjustment;
(e) effective date.
```

### 4. Expedited Changes

```
EXPEDITED CHANGE
For urgent changes, Customer may authorize Vendor to proceed pending
formal Change Order execution, provided Customer's authorized
representative provides written (including email) approval of the
estimated scope and fees.
```

## Pricing for Changes

### 1. Time and Materials

```
T&M CHANGES
Changes shall be priced at Vendor's then-current hourly rates as set
forth in Exhibit [X], plus materials at cost plus [X%].
```

### 2. Fixed Price Changes

```
FIXED PRICE CHANGES
For each Change Request, Vendor shall provide a fixed price quote.
Upon Customer approval, the Change Order price is firm regardless
of actual effort required.
```

### 3. Cost-Plus

```
COST-PLUS CHANGES
Changes shall be priced at Vendor's actual costs plus [X%] markup.
Vendor shall provide detailed cost breakdowns upon request.
```

### 4. Rate Card

```
RATE CARD
Changes shall be priced using the Rate Card in Exhibit [X]. The Rate
Card shall remain fixed for [12] months from the Effective Date.
```

### 5. Credit for Removed Scope

```
CREDIT
If a change removes scope, Customer shall receive a credit equal to
[X%] of the originally allocated fees for the removed scope.
```

## Schedule Impact

### 1. Automatic Extension

```
SCHEDULE EXTENSION
Approved Change Orders extending scope shall automatically extend
affected milestone dates by a period reasonably proportional to
the additional scope, as agreed in the Change Order.
```

### 2. Delay Allocation

```
DELAY ALLOCATION
Delays caused by Customer-requested changes shall not be counted
against Vendor's delivery obligations. Vendor shall notify Customer
of anticipated delays within [5] days of identifying the impact.
```

### 3. Acceleration

```
ACCELERATION
If Customer requires acceleration to maintain the original schedule
despite approved changes, Vendor shall provide acceleration pricing
which may include overtime, additional resources, or expedite fees.
```

## Authority and Approval

### 1. Approval Authority

```
APPROVAL AUTHORITY
Change Orders must be approved by designated representatives with
authority to bind their respective parties. For Customer:
- Under $[X]: Project Manager
- $[X] to $[Y]: Director level
- Over $[Y]: VP or above

Customer shall notify Vendor of authorized representatives in writing.
```

### 2. Steering Committee Escalation

```
ESCALATION
Change Requests exceeding $[X] or [Y] days of additional effort shall
be escalated to the Steering Committee for approval.
```

### 3. Deemed Approval

```
DEEMED APPROVAL
If Customer does not respond to a Change Order within [15] business
days, [the Change Order shall be deemed rejected / Customer shall
be deemed to have approved the Change Order].
```

## Vendor-Initiated Changes

### 1. Technical Recommendations

```
VENDOR RECOMMENDATIONS
Vendor may recommend changes to improve quality, performance, or
efficiency. Such recommendations require Customer approval and shall
not obligate additional fees unless Customer agrees.
```

### 2. Out-of-Scope Discovery

```
OUT-OF-SCOPE WORK
If Vendor discovers that requested work falls outside the SOW scope,
Vendor shall promptly notify Customer and cease out-of-scope work
pending Change Order approval. Vendor shall not perform out-of-scope
work without a signed Change Order except in emergencies.
```

### 3. Mandatory Changes

```
MANDATORY CHANGES
Vendor may implement changes required for: (a) regulatory compliance;
(b) security vulnerabilities; (c) safety issues. Vendor shall notify
Customer and provide a Change Order for any fee impact.
```

## Customer-Initiated Changes

### 1. Change Directive

```
CHANGE DIRECTIVE
Customer may direct Vendor to proceed with a change pending pricing
agreement ("Change Directive"). Vendor shall proceed and track time
and costs. The parties shall negotiate pricing in good faith. If
unable to agree within [30] days, the dispute shall be resolved per
the dispute resolution procedures.
```

### 2. No-Cost Changes

```
NO-COST CHANGES
Customer may make minor clarifications that do not materially affect
scope, schedule, or cost without a formal Change Order, provided
documented in project meeting minutes approved by both parties.
```

## Limitations

### 1. Change Volume Cap

```
CHANGE CAP
If aggregate Change Orders exceed [25%] of the original SOW value,
either party may request renegotiation of the overall project terms.
```

### 2. Schedule Protection

```
SCHEDULE PROTECTION
Vendor is not obligated to accept changes that would require delivery
prior to the original schedule without Customer agreeing to acceleration
pricing.
```

### 3. No Unreasonable Withholding

```
Neither party shall unreasonably withhold, condition, or delay
approval of Change Orders that are reasonably necessary for project
success or compliance with applicable law.
```

## Documentation Requirements

```
CHANGE DOCUMENTATION
All changes shall be documented and maintained in a Change Log
including: Change Request date, description, Impact Assessment,
Change Order number, approval date, and status. The Change Log
shall be reviewed at each project status meeting.
```

## Key Decision Points

1. **Change Authority**: Who can approve and at what levels?
2. **Pricing Model**: T&M, fixed, or cost-plus for changes?
3. **Response Time**: How long to assess and respond?
4. **Schedule Impact**: Automatic extension or case-by-case?
5. **Scope Creep**: How to handle minor clarifications?
6. **Credit for Reduced Scope**: Percentage returned?
7. **Emergency Changes**: Process for urgent situations?
8. **Dispute Resolution**: What if parties cannot agree on pricing?

## Common Pitfalls

1. **No change process**: Disputes over scope
2. **Informal changes**: Verbal agreements without documentation
3. **Unclear authority**: Unauthorized approvals
4. **No pricing mechanism**: Cannot agree on fees
5. **No schedule adjustment**: Unrealistic deadlines persist
6. **Change creep**: Small changes accumulate without control
7. **Delayed assessments**: Impact unknown until too late
8. **Missing credits**: Removed scope not deducted

## Sample Provisions

### Balanced Change Control
```
CHANGE ORDERS
Either party may request changes via written Change Request. Vendor
shall provide an Impact Assessment within [10] business days. Changes
require a signed Change Order specifying scope, fee, and schedule
adjustments. Changes priced at current T&M rates. Customer Change
Requests extending scope automatically extend affected milestones.
Neither party shall unreasonably withhold approval.
```

### Customer-Favorable
```
Customer may direct changes at any time. Vendor shall provide pricing
within [5] days. If parties cannot agree on pricing, Vendor shall
proceed at T&M rates capped at Vendor's estimate plus [10%]. Customer
may reduce scope with proportional fee reduction.
```

### Vendor-Favorable
```
All changes require signed Change Order before work begins. Vendor
is not obligated to accept changes that materially impact project
architecture or timeline. Changes priced at Vendor's then-current
rates plus [15%] expedite premium for fast-track implementation.
```
