# PCP-MC21: Organizational Political Intelligence

## Pattern Definition

The meta-cognitive skill of understanding hidden power topologies, protected positions, and inter-departmental dynamics BEFORE engaging on issues that may threaten personal interests. This is distinct from simple stakeholder analysis (PCP-MC1) - it's about mapping the political terrain to avoid landmines and build coalitions strategically.

### Trigger Condition

Activate when:
- Proposing changes that may threaten someone's interests, position, or authority
- Engaging with individuals whose position/influence seems disproportionate to visible credentials
- Navigating situations where "the right answer" may not be politically viable
- Operating across departmental boundaries with competing interests
- Dealing with resistance that seems irrational from a purely business perspective

### Core Procedure

1. **Pre-Engagement Intelligence Gathering**
   - Research the individual's background, tenure, and organizational connections
   - Map reporting relationships and informal influence networks
   - Identify who sponsors/protects this person and why
   - Understand their career trajectory and what they're protecting

2. **Protected Position Recognition**
   - Detect when someone holds influence disproportionate to their visible role
   - Identify the source of protection (executive sponsor, tenure, specialized knowledge)
   - Assess what threatens their position and triggers defensive behavior
   - Recognize "untouchable" status and its boundaries

3. **Threat Assessment**
   - Determine if your proposal threatens their interests (budget, authority, reputation)
   - Assess their capacity to undermine your efforts
   - Map their allies who might join opposition
   - Evaluate whether confrontation or accommodation serves your goals

4. **Inter-Departmental Dynamic Mapping**
   - Understand natural tensions (sales vs legal, engineering vs product, etc.)
   - Identify whose ox gets gored by your proposal
   - Recognize competing metrics and incentive structures
   - Map historical conflicts and alliances between groups

5. **Coalition Strategy Development**
   - Identify potential allies whose interests align with yours
   - Determine what they need in exchange for support
   - Plan the sequence of building support (who to approach first)
   - Prepare for counterarguments that opponents will raise

6. **Audience-Adapted Framing**
   - Tailor messaging based on political landscape
   - Frame proposals to minimize perceived threat to key players
   - Identify win-win framings that reduce opposition
   - Prepare different pitches for different stakeholder groups

7. **Tactical Engagement Selection**
   - Choose: direct engagement vs. coalition-building vs. working around
   - Decide: public forum vs. private conversations
   - Time engagement for maximum advantage
   - Prepare contingencies for each response scenario

### Expert Heuristic

> "Before engaging on anything that might threaten someone's position or interests, I research their connections, understand who protects them, and assess whether they can undermine me. The 'right' business answer is worthless if it gets torpedoed by political opposition. I build coalitions before battles, not during them."

## Sub-Pattern Components

### PCP-MC21.1: Pre-Engagement Intelligence
The practice of researching individuals' backgrounds, connections, and political positions before substantive engagement.

**Key Question:** "Who is this person connected to, and what are they protecting?"

### PCP-MC21.2: Protected Position Recognition
Detecting when influence exceeds visible credentials, indicating political protection.

**Key Question:** "Why does this person have this level of influence given their role?"

### PCP-MC21.3: Threat/Interest Assessment
Analyzing how proposals impact others' interests and their capacity to oppose.

**Key Question:** "Whose ox gets gored by this proposal, and what can they do about it?"

### PCP-MC21.4: Inter-Departmental Dynamic Mapping
Understanding structural tensions between organizational units.

**Key Question:** "What are the natural alliance and opposition patterns between these groups?"

### PCP-MC21.5: Coalition Building Strategy
Strategically building support before engagement.

**Key Question:** "Who benefits from my proposal and how do I secure their support first?"

### PCP-MC21.6: Audience-Adapted Framing
Tailoring presentation to political landscape.

**Key Question:** "How do I frame this to minimize threat perception while achieving my goal?"

### PCP-MC21.7: Tactical Engagement Selection
Choosing engagement modality based on political assessment.

**Key Question:** "Should I engage directly, build coalition first, or work around this obstacle?"

## Decision Criteria

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Unknown stakeholder with outsized influence | Pause. Research connections before engaging |
| Proposal threatens powerful interests | Build coalition BEFORE visible engagement |
| Inter-departmental resistance | Map incentive conflicts, find aligned interests |
| Rational argument meeting irrational resistance | Political dynamics at play - shift to coalition mode |
| Need executive support | Understand executive's own political constraints |
