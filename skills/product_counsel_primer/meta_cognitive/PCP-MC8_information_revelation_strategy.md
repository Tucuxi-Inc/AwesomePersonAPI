# PCP-MC8: Information Revelation Strategy

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Optimizing timing and sequence of information disclosure
**Domain Independence:** Universal for asymmetric information situations
**Orchestration Role:** Controls what information flows when to whom

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
Asymmetric information situation where timing and sequence of disclosure matter:
- Negotiations with information asymmetry
- Due diligence and deal processes
- Crisis communication and disclosure
- Regulatory submissions and compliance
- Strategic announcements and market communication

### Core Procedure

Given information you possess, determine:

1. **Required vs. Discretionary Disclosure**
   - What must be revealed (legal, ethical, contractual requirements)?
   - What can legitimately be withheld?
   - What are the boundaries of strategic non-disclosure?

2. **Optimal Timing Analysis**
   - When does each piece of information have maximum positive/minimum negative impact?
   - What external events affect optimal timing?
   - What dependencies exist between disclosures?

3. **Framing Strategy**
   - How should information be framed for desired interpretation?
   - What context should accompany disclosure?
   - How to present negative information constructively?

4. **Information Exchange Calculus**
   - What information do you need from others?
   - What information can be exchanged to get what you need?
   - How to structure information exchanges for mutual benefit?

### Expert Heuristic

> "Information revealed can never be un-revealed - sequence disclosures strategically. The order in which people learn things shapes how they interpret everything."

### Application Contexts

- M&A negotiations and due diligence
- Settlement discussions and dispute resolution
- Regulatory investigations and compliance
- Product announcements and market timing
- Personnel decisions and organizational changes
- Crisis communication and damage control

## Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Required Disclosure Compliance**
- IF disclosure is legally/ethically required, THEN disclose (optimize timing and framing, not whether)
- Rationale: Non-disclosure of required information creates liability and trust damage

**Criterion 2: Sequence Optimization**
- IF information A affects interpretation of information B, THEN sequence A before B for desired framing
- IF negative information exists, THEN embed in context that enables constructive interpretation
- Rationale: Sequence shapes interpretation; use sequence strategically

**Criterion 3: Timing for Impact**
- IF external events will affect information interpretation, THEN time disclosure accordingly
- IF information loses value over time (e.g., competitive intelligence), THEN factor decay into timing
- Rationale: Same information has different impact at different times

**Criterion 4: Exchange Value**
- IF you need information from others, THEN consider what you can offer in exchange
- IF information exchange is mutually beneficial, THEN structure reciprocal disclosure
- Rationale: Information has exchange value; use it strategically

## Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Full transparency - disclose everything immediately.
**Expert Approach**: Strategic transparency - disclose required information optimally timed and framed.

**Naive Approach**: Withhold as much as possible for maximum advantage.
**Expert Approach**: Balance disclosure for trust-building against retention for negotiating leverage.

**Naive Approach**: Disclose information in order it becomes available.
**Expert Approach**: Sequence disclosure for optimal interpretation and impact.

**Naive Approach**: Treat information as one-way (disclosure to others).
**Expert Approach**: Recognize information exchange as two-way; use disclosure to obtain needed information.

## Ethical Boundaries

### What This Pattern Is NOT

- **NOT deception**: Strategic timing ≠ lying or misleading
- **NOT suppression of required disclosure**: Legal/ethical requirements override strategy
- **NOT manipulation**: Framing truth ≠ creating false impressions

### Ethical Application

Expert practitioners apply PCP-MC8 within ethical bounds:
- Never lie or create false impressions
- Always meet disclosure obligations
- Frame truthfully even when strategically
- Recognize when transparency serves better than strategy
