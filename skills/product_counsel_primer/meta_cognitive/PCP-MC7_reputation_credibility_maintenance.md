# PCP-MC7: Reputation and Credibility Maintenance

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Protecting long-term credibility over short-term gains
**Domain Independence:** Universal across all repeated-interaction contexts
**Orchestration Role:** Constrains short-term pattern application to protect long-term assets

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
Decision affects future interactions or long-term relationships, including:
- Actions visible to stakeholders you'll interact with again
- Precedent-setting decisions that will be referenced later
- Situations where short-term gain conflicts with long-term reputation
- Choices that affect how others perceive your reliability/integrity

### Core Procedure

1. **Current Credibility Assessment**
   - Assess current credibility with each relevant stakeholder
   - Identify what your reputation rests on (expertise, reliability, fairness, etc.)
   - Recognize credibility variations across stakeholder groups

2. **Impact Evaluation**
   - Evaluate how proposed action affects credibility
   - Consider both direct effects and second-order signaling
   - Assess credibility impact with current AND future stakeholders

3. **Reputational Second-Order Effects**
   - How will this action be interpreted by observers?
   - What precedent does it set for future interactions?
   - How will it be described to others not present?

4. **Long-Term Trade-Off Decision**
   - When short-term gain conflicts with long-term credibility, bias toward credibility
   - Accept short-term costs to maintain long-term credibility
   - Know when short-term needs genuinely override (rare)

### Expert Heuristic

> "Your reputation is your most valuable asset - never trade long-term credibility for short-term gain. The deal you win by compromising credibility costs you ten future deals."

### Application Contexts

- Professional service relationships (law, consulting, medicine)
- Leadership and management decisions
- Negotiation and deal-making
- Sales and business development
- Expert testimony and advisory roles
- Any repeated-game interaction

## Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Credibility Preservation Priority**
- IF action damages credibility AND short-term gain < 3x estimated long-term cost, THEN reject or modify action
- Rationale: Credibility compounds; short-term gains rarely justify long-term damage

**Criterion 2: Precedent Awareness**
- IF action sets precedent that will constrain future options, THEN factor precedent cost into decision
- IF exception is needed, THEN explicitly frame as exception with rationale
- Rationale: Precedents are invoked against you; establish them carefully

**Criterion 3: Observer Effect**
- IF action will be observed or reported to others, THEN consider reputational effect beyond direct parties
- Rationale: Reputation spreads through networks; direct parties aren't only audience

**Criterion 4: Crisis Exception**
- IF genuine crisis requires credibility sacrifice, THEN make conscious choice with mitigation plan
- IF survival requires reputation damage, THEN survive first, rebuild second
- Rationale: Rare situations justify reputation costs; recognize when in one

## Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Optimize each interaction independently; maximize immediate outcome.
**Expert Approach**: Treat reputation as an asset that compounds; optimize across all future interactions.

**Naive Approach**: Assume private actions stay private.
**Expert Approach**: Assume anything can become known; act accordingly.

**Naive Approach**: Trust that explanations will preserve reputation after questionable actions.
**Expert Approach**: Recognize that actions speak louder than explanations; avoid needing to explain.
