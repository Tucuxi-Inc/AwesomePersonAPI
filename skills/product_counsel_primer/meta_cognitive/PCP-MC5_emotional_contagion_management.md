# PCP-MC5: Emotional Contagion Management

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Managing emotional dynamics through strategic self-regulation
**Domain Independence:** Universal across all high-stakes interpersonal contexts
**Orchestration Role:** Sets emotional context for how other patterns are received

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
High-stakes emotional situation affecting group dynamics, including:
- Crisis situations with elevated anxiety or fear
- Conflicts with anger or defensiveness present
- High-pressure decisions with stress contagion risk
- Team morale issues affecting performance
- Difficult conversations with emotional stakes

### Core Procedure

1. **Emotional State Assessment**
   - Identify current emotional state of each party
   - Recognize emotional contagion already in progress
   - Assess which emotions are productive vs. counterproductive

2. **Contagion Trajectory Prediction**
   - Predict emotional contagion effects if no intervention
   - Model how current emotional states will propagate
   - Identify tipping points and feedback loops

3. **Strategic Tone Selection**
   - Select your emotional tone to shift system toward productive state
   - Match intensity to situation (too calm can dismiss; too intense can escalate)
   - Choose emotion that serves the needed outcome (calm for crisis, energy for motivation)

4. **Monitoring and Adjustment**
   - Observe whether emotional state is shifting as intended
   - Adjust tone based on feedback
   - Know when emotional management is succeeding vs. failing

### Expert Heuristic

> "Your emotional state becomes their emotional state - manage yours strategically to manage theirs. In crisis, calm is contagious; in celebration, enthusiasm is contagious."

### Application Contexts

- Crisis management and incident response
- Conflict resolution and mediation
- Team leadership during high-pressure periods
- Difficult conversations (terminations, performance issues)
- Negotiation under tension
- Change management during uncertainty

## Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Contagion Direction**
- IF current emotional trajectory is counterproductive, THEN intervene with contrasting emotion
- IF current emotional state is productive, THEN amplify rather than redirect
- Rationale: Ride productive emotions; redirect counterproductive ones

**Criterion 2: Intensity Calibration**
- IF emotions are running very high, THEN gradual de-escalation (too sudden feels dismissive)
- IF energy is too low, THEN gradual escalation (too sudden feels forced)
- Rationale: Emotional shifts require pacing to feel authentic

**Criterion 3: Authenticity Threshold**
- IF strategic emotion conflicts severely with genuine feeling, THEN acknowledge genuine feeling first
- Rationale: Inauthentic emotional performance is detected and backfires

**Criterion 4: Know When to Step Back**
- IF emotional management is not working, THEN consider stepping out or bringing different leader
- Rationale: Sometimes the messenger is the problem; fresh energy helps

## Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Express whatever emotion you're feeling; "be authentic."
**Expert Approach**: Choose emotional expression strategically while maintaining authenticity through genuine care for outcomes.

**Naive Approach**: Try to suppress all emotions in professional settings.
**Expert Approach**: Channel appropriate emotions that serve the situation and group needs.

**Naive Approach**: Match the emotional intensity of the room.
**Expert Approach**: Strategically counter or redirect emotional dynamics toward productive states.
