# PCP-MC9: Expertise & Authority Boundary Recognition (Enhanced)

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Self-monitoring of competence limits and strategic routing with resource allocation optimization
**Domain Independence:** Universal across all professional contexts
**Orchestration Role:** Determines when to delegate vs. decide, to whom, and with optimal resource efficiency

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
Situations requiring decisions or analysis where:
- The domain falls partially or fully outside your core expertise
- Decisions may have implications beyond your authority level
- Multiple specialists could potentially provide input
- There's uncertainty about who actually has decision-making power
- Stakes are high enough that errors would be costly
- **[Enhanced]** Cognitive bandwidth is limited and optimal allocation matters
- **[Enhanced]** Multiple competing priorities require resource triage decisions

### Core Procedure

#### Original Competence & Authority Assessment

1. **Self-Assessment of Competence**
   - What is my actual expertise level in this specific area?
   - Am I a novice, competent practitioner, or expert here?
   - What don't I know that I don't know?

2. **Authority Mapping**
   - Who has formal authority to make this decision?
   - Who has informal influence that matters?
   - What approval chain exists (and is it being followed)?

3. **Expert Identification**
   - Who are the domain specialists for this specific issue?
   - Who has handled similar situations successfully before?
   - Who would I be embarrassed to have review my work?

#### Enhanced Resource Allocation Assessment

4. **Cognitive Resource Evaluation** **[NEW]**
   - What is my current cognitive bandwidth and existing commitments?
   - Is this the highest-value use of my attention right now?
   - How much time/effort would competent handling require?
   - What opportunity cost exists if I handle this vs. delegate?

5. **Resource-Optimized Routing Decision** **[ENHANCED]**
   - Should I decide, delegate, escalate, or defer based on both competence AND resource efficiency?
   - If delegating: who has both expertise AND bandwidth?
   - If escalating: what's the resource cost to the organization?
   - What's the minimum viable involvement to achieve good outcomes?

6. **Efficient Handoff Design** **[ENHANCED]**
   - How much context does the recipient need vs. how much time do I have?
   - Should I invest in detailed briefing or lightweight delegation?
   - What follow-up involvement optimizes both quality and efficiency?
   - How do I preserve optionality while minimizing ongoing resource drain?

### Expert Heuristic

> "If you wouldn't want to defend this decision to the domain expert, route it to them first. If you wouldn't want to explain to your boss why you decided alone, escalate it. **But also ask: Is this the best use of my cognitive resources, and how do I optimize the handoff for everyone's efficiency?**"

```

  ↓
PCP-MC9: Assess expertise/authority boundaries + Resource optimization
  ↓
```

## Enhanced Decision Criteria

#### Resource Allocation Decision Tree **[NEW]**

**Criterion 0: Resource Optimization Assessment**
- IF I have expertise but limited bandwidth AND others have both expertise and bandwidth, THEN delegate with lightweight oversight
- IF I have bandwidth but others have much higher expertise, THEN delegate with learning opportunity
- IF I have expertise and bandwidth but this is low-value use of my time, THEN delegate and use time for higher-value work
- Rationale: Optimize organizational resource allocation, not just correctness

**Criterion 1: Expertise Threshold** (Enhanced)
- IF unfamiliar with domain specifics, THEN route to specialist with sufficient bandwidth
- IF familiar but not expert, THEN decide with specialist review OR delegate if resource-optimal
- Rationale: Match expertise to task importance while optimizing resource allocation

**Criterion 2: Authority Threshold** (Enhanced)
- IF decision exceeds formal authority, THEN escalate via most efficient channel
- IF decision has organization-wide implications, THEN get executive buy-in with minimal briefing burden
- Rationale: Authority alignment with efficient organizational resource use

**Criterion 3: Resource-Value Alignment** **[NEW]**
- IF high-stakes decision requiring deep expertise, THEN invest heavily regardless of resource cost
- IF routine decision that others can handle well, THEN delegate to free up bandwidth for unique contributions
- IF teaching opportunity for team member, THEN delegate with structured oversight
- Rationale: Align resource investment with value creation and capability building

**Criterion 4: Efficient Handoff Design** **[NEW]**
- IF recipient is expert in area, THEN provide minimal context and clear success criteria
- IF recipient is learning, THEN invest in structured guidance with efficient check-ins
- IF urgent timeline, THEN provide targeted context focused on decision-critical information
- Rationale: Match handoff investment to recipient capability and timeline constraints

## Enhanced Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Handle everything yourself or delegate everything to avoid decisions.
**Expert Approach**: **Systematically optimize task-expertise-bandwidth matching across the organization.**

**Naive Approach**: Provide exhaustive context for every delegation.
**Expert Approach**: **Calibrate context transfer to recipient expertise and time constraints.**

**Naive Approach**: Only consider whether you CAN handle something.
**Expert Approach**: **Consider whether you SHOULD handle it given opportunity costs and resource optimization.**

**Naive Approach**: Route based purely on expertise without considering bandwidth.
**Expert Approach**: **Balance expertise, authority, bandwidth, and learning opportunities for optimal allocation.**
