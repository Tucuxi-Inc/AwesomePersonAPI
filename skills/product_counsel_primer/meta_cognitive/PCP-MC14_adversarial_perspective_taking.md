# PCP-MC14: Adversarial Perspective Taking

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Testing positions through adversarial lens and integrating competitive intelligence
**Domain Independence:** Universal across competitive and adversarial contexts
**Orchestration Role:** Stress-tests strategies by modeling opposition and competitors

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
Situations involving:
- Strategic positions that will face opposition
- Competitive dynamics where opponent moves matter
- Negotiations where counterparty interests diverge
- Regulatory or legal contexts with adversarial elements
- Any argument or position that needs to withstand challenge

### Core Procedure

1. **Genuine Perspective Adoption**
   - What would I believe if I were on the other side?
   - What's the strongest version of their argument?
   - What information do they have that I might not?

2. **Position Stress-Testing**
   - Where are the vulnerabilities in my position?
   - What counterarguments would I make against myself?
   - What evidence would undermine my case?

3. **Competitive Intelligence Integration**
   - What are competitors actually doing and saying?
   - What do their positions reveal about their strategy?
   - How does industry behavior inform our options?

4. **Anticipatory Defense**
   - What attacks should I prepare for?
   - How can I preempt the strongest objections?
   - What contingencies do I need?

5. **Competitive Advantage Assessment**
   - Can I actually win on this dimension?
   - Do new competitive arenas favor me or opponents?
   - What are my asymmetric advantages?

### Expert Heuristic

> "If you can't argue your opponent's position better than they can, you don't understand the situation well enough to win. The strongest position is one that survives your own best attack."

## Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Perspective Quality**
- IF can't articulate opponent's best argument, THEN insufficient analysis
- IF position survives own best attack, THEN ready to proceed
- Rationale: Positions that can't withstand internal challenge won't survive external challenge

**Criterion 2: Competitive Arena Entry**
- IF new dimension favors opponents, THEN avoid that competition
- IF asymmetric advantage exists, THEN compete aggressively
- Rationale: Choose battles where you can win

**Criterion 3: Intelligence Integration**
- IF competitors have taken positions, THEN analyze their reasoning
- IF industry benchmark exists, THEN leverage for legitimacy
- Rationale: Competitor behavior is information about market and regulatory expectations

**Criterion 4: Preemptive Defense**
- IF strong counterargument exists, THEN address proactively
- IF vulnerability identified, THEN shore up before engagement
- Rationale: Better to patch weaknesses before opponents exploit them

## Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Assume your position is strong because you believe in it.
**Expert Approach**: Attack your own position and see if it survives.

**Naive Approach**: Ignore competitor moves as irrelevant to your strategy.
**Expert Approach**: Integrate competitive intelligence as strategic input.

**Naive Approach**: Enter all competitive arenas to seem comprehensive.
**Expert Approach**: Choose competitive dimensions where you have advantage.

**Naive Approach**: Wait for attacks before developing defenses.
**Expert Approach**: Identify likely attacks and preempt them.
