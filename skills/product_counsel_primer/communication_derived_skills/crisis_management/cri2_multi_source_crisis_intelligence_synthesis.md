# CRI2 - Multi Source Crisis Intelligence Synthesis

**Type:** Crisis Intelligence - Multi-Domain Assessment
**Focus Area:** Post-launch product crisis management with distributed stakeholder coordination
**Complexity:** 9/10
**Uniqueness:** 8/10

## Description

This skill enables experts to synthesize disparate intelligence streams during critical product crises, transforming fragmented data into actionable insights for coordinated organizational response. The expert systematically integrates quantitative metrics, qualitative feedback, operational tickets, and behavioral analytics while managing information asymmetry across multiple stakeholder groups with conflicting priorities.

The skill operates through a layered intelligence architecture that combines signal processing principles with strategic resource deployment. Rather than treating all data sources equally, the expert applies weighted interpretation based on source reliability, temporal correlation patterns, and manipulation detection. This creates a unified intelligence picture that supports both tactical issue resolution and strategic escalation decisions.

The framework is particularly valuable during post-launch periods when multiple failure modes emerge simultaneously, requiring coordination across engineering teams, customer service, international operations, and executive leadership. The expert maintains operational granularity while providing strategic overview, enabling precise resource allocation and stakeholder-appropriate communication across large, distributed organizations.

## Keywords

- Multi-source intelligence synthesis, crisis management, stakeholder cascade communication, sentiment-weighted analytics, temporal pattern monitoring, resource deployment optimization, signal processing, information asymmetry management, escalation threshold determination, cross-functional coordination

## Application Guidance

Apply this skill when managing complex product crises involving:
- Multiple simultaneous technical failures across different domains
- Mixed or conflicting signals from various data sources
- Large stakeholder groups with diverse functional responsibilities
- Time-sensitive decisions requiring both tactical and strategic coordination
- Post-launch periods where customer experience degradation could cause viral negative impact

The skill is most effective when you have access to multiple data streams (customer reviews, support tickets, behavioral analytics, social media) and need to coordinate response across 50+ stakeholders spanning multiple time zones and business functions.

## 1. Detailed Pattern Description

### Theoretical Foundations

**Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) with Weighted Scoring**: Provides systematic framework for evaluating complex decisions involving multiple, often conflicting criteria by assigning weights and scores to different attributes. In crisis intelligence, each data source receives weighted interpretation based on verification status, temporal relevance, and manipulation detection rather than equal treatment.

Formula: Composite Score = Σ(wi × si) where wi = weight for criterion i, si = score for criterion i

**Information Theory and Signal Processing**: Distinguishes meaningful signals from noise in data streams by accounting for signal strength, temporal patterns, and source reliability. Each data source provides different temporal granularity and signal strength, requiring classification as leading vs lagging indicators with appropriate confidence intervals.

Signal-to-Noise Ratio = (Signal Power) / (Noise Power), applied to customer feedback streams

**Resource-Based View (RBV) and Dynamic Capabilities**: Optimizes deployment of organizational capabilities by matching specific technical problems to teams with appropriate domain expertise, considering both current capacity and escalation pathway optimization rather than simple availability-based assignment.

**Stakeholder Theory with Information Asymmetry Management**: Manages multiple stakeholder relationships while accounting for different information needs, decision authority levels, and functional responsibilities through tiered communication architecture and role-appropriate detail levels.

**Bayesian Probability and Sequential Learning**: Updates probability assessments as new information becomes available, accounting for prior beliefs and evidence strength. Particularly relevant for temporal pattern monitoring where trend velocity and pattern deviation significance must be continuously recalibrated.

P(H|E) = P(E|H) × P(H) / P(E) where H = hypothesis about crisis severity, E = new evidence

## 2. Step-by-Step Framework

### Phase 1: Multi-Source Data Collection and Validation

**Step 1.1: Establish Data Source Hierarchy**
Create systematic classification of available intelligence streams by reliability, temporal granularity, and signal strength. Assign baseline credibility scores to each source type.

Questions to ask:
- What is the verification status and manipulation potential of each data source?
- How does temporal lag affect the relevance of each intelligence stream?
- Which sources provide leading vs lagging indicators of system health?

**Step 1.2: Implement Signal Processing Filters**
Apply information theory principles to distinguish meaningful patterns from noise. Establish confidence intervals for each data stream and identify correlation patterns across sources.

Questions to ask:
- What constitutes statistically significant deviation from baseline patterns?
- How do seasonal/cyclical factors affect signal interpretation?
- Which data sources show temporal correlation that might indicate causal relationships?

**Step 1.3: Deploy Sentiment-Weighted Analytics**
Integrate quantitative ratings with qualitative sentiment analysis, accounting for review manipulation and providing weighted credibility scoring based on verification status and community validation.

Questions to ask:
- How do verified vs unverified reviews differ in sentiment and rating patterns?
- What behavioral indicators (helpfulness votes, comment patterns) suggest manipulation?
- How does sentiment polarity correlate with specific technical issues?

### Phase 2: Issue Classification and Resource Deployment

**Step 2.1: Create Granular Issue Classification Matrix**
Systematically categorize emerging issues by technical domain with specific routing to appropriate resolution teams. Each issue receives individual assessment rather than batch processing.

Questions to ask:
- Which technical domains require specialized expertise for resolution?
- How do issue complexity levels map to available team capabilities?
- What are the optimal escalation pathways for each issue category?

**Step 2.2: Apply Resource-Based View Optimization**
Match organizational capabilities to problem domains based on competency fit rather than availability alone. Consider both current capacity and capability development implications.

Questions to ask:
- Which teams have the deepest domain expertise for each technical failure mode?
- How do resource allocation decisions affect other concurrent priorities?
- What capability gaps might require external resources or escalation?

**Step 2.3: Establish Escalation Threshold Criteria**
Define clear decision criteria for when issues require escalation based on customer impact, technical complexity, and resource requirements.

Questions to ask:
- What customer impact metrics trigger escalation to executive leadership?
- How do technical complexity factors affect resource allocation decisions?
- Which issues have potential for viral negative impact requiring immediate attention?

### Phase 3: Temporal Pattern Analysis and Trend Detection

**Step 3.1: Implement Behavioral Pattern Monitoring**
Track user behavior patterns across time series data to identify emerging trends and usage pattern shifts that may indicate developing issues or user adaptation patterns.

Questions to ask:
- How do help page usage patterns correlate with emerging technical issues?
- What search theme evolution indicates changing user needs or frustrations?
- Which behavioral changes represent leading indicators of satisfaction degradation?

**Step 3.2: Apply Bayesian Sequential Learning**
Continuously update risk assessments based on incoming behavioral data, demonstrating sophisticated understanding of how to weight new evidence against established patterns.

Questions to ask:
- How should new evidence update prior probability assessments of crisis severity?
- What confidence intervals are appropriate for trend velocity calculations?
- How do pattern deviations compare to historical baseline variations?

**Step 3.3: Conduct Predictive Trend Analysis**
Project likely evolution of current patterns to anticipate resource needs and stakeholder communication requirements.

Questions to ask:
- What trajectory do current trends suggest for customer satisfaction metrics?
- How might emerging patterns affect resource requirements over the next 48-72 hours?
- Which trends require proactive stakeholder communication vs reactive response?

### Phase 4: Stakeholder Cascade Communication Architecture

**Step 4.1: Map Functional Relevance and Decision Authority**
Create structured information distribution across organizational layers with role-appropriate detail levels and clear action requirements.

Questions to ask:
- Which stakeholders have decision authority for different types of resource allocation?
- How do geographic and time zone factors affect communication timing?
- What information sensitivity levels require restricted distribution?

**Step 4.2: Design Tiered Communication Strategy**
Use primary (TO) vs secondary (CC) recipient classification to indicate stakeholder priority and expected action levels.

Questions to ask:
- Which stakeholders require immediate action vs situational awareness?
- How should technical detail levels vary across organizational functions?
- What communication frequency is appropriate for different stakeholder groups?

**Step 4.3: Implement Coordinated Response Protocols**
Establish clear coordination mechanisms across multiple teams and geographies with defined handoff procedures and status reporting requirements.

Questions to ask:
- How do different teams coordinate response activities across time zones?
- What status reporting frequency enables effective coordination without overwhelming stakeholders?
- Which decisions require cross-functional consensus vs individual team authority?

## 3. Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Data Source Credibility Assessment**
- IF verification rate < 90% AND sentiment deviation > 2 standard deviations, THEN apply manipulation detection protocols
- IF temporal correlation exists across multiple sources, THEN increase confidence weighting by 25%
- Rationale: Prevents decision-making based on manipulated or unreliable data streams

**Criterion 2: Issue Escalation Threshold**
- IF customer impact affects > 5% of user base OR technical complexity requires > 3 specialized teams, THEN escalate to executive level
- IF issue resolution time exceeds 48 hours AND customer satisfaction trend is negative, THEN activate crisis communication protocols
- Rationale: Ensures appropriate resource allocation and prevents minor issues from becoming major crises

**Criterion 3: Resource Deployment Optimization**
- IF technical domain expertise match score > 80% AND team capacity > 50%, THEN assign to primary domain team
- IF multiple teams required AND coordination complexity high, THEN establish dedicated integration lead
- Rationale: Maximizes resolution efficiency while maintaining coordination across complex technical domains

**Criterion 4: Stakeholder Communication Tiering**
- IF decision authority level = executive AND functional relevance = high, THEN include in primary recipient list with full detail
- IF geographic coordination required AND time sensitivity high, THEN implement follow-the-sun communication protocol
- Rationale: Ensures appropriate information flow while preventing communication overload

**Criterion 5: Temporal Pattern Significance**
- IF trend velocity > 20% change per day AND pattern deviation > 1.5 standard deviations, THEN classify as emerging crisis indicator
- IF behavioral pattern correlation coefficient > 0.7 across multiple metrics, THEN increase predictive confidence
- Rationale: Enables proactive response to developing issues before they reach crisis levels

## 4. Common Pitfalls and Expert Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Treating all customer feedback equally and making decisions based on simple averages or most recent complaints.
**Expert Approach**: Applying weighted credibility scoring based on verification status, manipulation detection, and temporal relevance to create sophisticated composite intelligence.

**Naive Approach**: Assigning issues to available teams based on current workload without considering domain expertise matching.
**Expert Approach**: Optimizing resource deployment through systematic capability-problem matching while considering escalation pathway efficiency and capability development implications.

**Naive Approach**: Broadcasting the same information to all stakeholders regardless of their functional role or decision authority.
**Expert Approach**: Creating tiered communication architecture with role-appropriate detail levels and clear action requirements based on functional relevance and decision authority mapping.

**Naive Approach**: Reacting to individual data points or complaints as they arise without systematic pattern analysis.
**Expert Approach**: Implementing continuous temporal pattern monitoring with Bayesian sequential learning to identify emerging trends and leading indicators before they manifest as major issues.

**Naive Approach**: Making escalation decisions based on subjective assessment or single metrics like complaint volume.
**Expert Approach**: Applying systematic multi-criteria decision analysis with clear threshold criteria based on customer impact, technical complexity, and resource requirements.
