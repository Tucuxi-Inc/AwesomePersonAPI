# URP-MC28: Performance Prediction

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Forecasting own performance outcomes
**Domain Independence:** Universal across all performance domains
**Orchestration Role:** Enables realistic commitments, appropriate preparation, and valid self-assessment

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
Situations involving:
- Deciding whether to attempt challenging tasks
- Making commitments about deliverables
- Setting performance expectations with others
- Allocating effort across performance opportunities
- Evaluating risks of failure

### Core Procedure

1. **Capability Assessment**
   - What are my relevant capabilities?
   - What's my track record on similar tasks?
   - What are my strengths and limitations here?
   - What skill gaps might affect performance?

2. **Demand-Capability Matching**
   - What does this task require?
   - How do requirements match my capabilities?
   - Where are the gaps?
   - What can I do about gaps?

3. **Contextual Factor Assessment**
   - What conditions will I be performing under?
   - What might enhance or degrade my performance?
   - What resources will I have?
   - What pressures or distractions might apply?

4. **Prediction Generation**
   - What's my expected performance level?
   - What's the range (best/worst/typical)?
   - How confident am I in this prediction?
   - What would it take to exceed/fall short of prediction?

5. **Calibration Check**
   - How have past predictions compared to actuals?
   - Do I tend to over- or underpredict?
   - What adjustment should I apply?
   - In what domains am I well/poorly calibrated?

### Expert Heuristic

> "Your past performance is the best predictor of future performance—but only if you actually track and remember it honestly. Don't predict based on your best day; predict based on your typical day."

## Evidence from Literature

### Example 1: Self-Assessment Accuracy
**Context:** Dunning-Kruger research (Kruger & Dunning, 1999)
**Evidence:** Low performers overestimate; high performers slightly underestimate
**Insight:** Performance prediction accuracy varies with actual ability

### Example 2: Motivated Reasoning
**Context:** Kunda (1990) motivated cognition research
**Evidence:** Desire for outcomes biases predictions upward
**Insight:** Motivation distorts performance prediction

### Example 3: Base Rate Neglect
**Context:** Kahneman & Tversky (1973) research
**Evidence:** People under-weight statistical base rates when predicting
**Insight:** Personal base rates should inform predictions

## Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Commitment Calibration**
- IF prediction is uncertain, THEN underpromise
- IF prediction is confident and based on track record, THEN can commit
- Rationale: Commitments should match realistic predictions

**Criterion 2: Preparation Response**
- IF gap between required and predicted performance, THEN prepare more
- IF predicted performance exceeds requirements, THEN acceptable risk
- Rationale: Use predictions to drive preparation

**Criterion 3: Motivation Adjustment**
- IF motivated to succeed, THEN apply skepticism to optimistic predictions
- IF anxious about task, THEN check for underprediction
- Rationale: Motivation biases prediction; correct accordingly

**Criterion 4: Domain Specificity**
- IF domain where prediction accuracy is high, THEN trust prediction
- IF domain with poor prediction track record, THEN widen range
- Rationale: Prediction accuracy varies by domain

## Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Predict based on best past performance.
**Expert Approach**: Predict based on typical performance in similar conditions.

**Naive Approach**: Assume motivation will enhance performance beyond base rate.
**Expert Approach**: Recognize motivation as prediction distorter, not performance enhancer.

**Naive Approach**: Single point prediction; "I'll do great/poorly."
**Expert Approach**: Range prediction with confidence; "I expect X but could range from Y to Z."

**Naive Approach**: Same prediction approach across all domains.
**Expert Approach**: Domain-specific prediction using domain-specific track record.
