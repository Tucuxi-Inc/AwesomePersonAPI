# URP-MC20: Post-Task Reflection

**Type:** Meta-Cognitive Pattern (Tier 3)
**Function:** Deliberate review of experience for learning
**Domain Independence:** Universal across all experiential learning
**Orchestration Role:** Converts experience into transferable knowledge and improved future performance

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
Situations involving:
- Completion of significant tasks or projects
- Unusual outcomes (especially failures or surprising successes)
- Experiences with potential transfer value
- Periodic review points
- Before repeating similar tasks

### Core Procedure

1. **Experience Reconstruction**
   - What actually happened, step by step?
   - What were the key decision points?
   - What was surprising or unexpected?
   - What were the critical moments?

2. **Causal Analysis**
   - What led to the outcomes observed?
   - What were the key success/failure factors?
   - What did I control vs. not control?
   - What role did luck play?

3. **Alternative Exploration**
   - What else could I have done?
   - What would have happened if...?
   - What options did I not consider?
   - What would I do differently?

4. **Insight Extraction**
   - What did I learn from this?
   - What principles emerge?
   - What patterns do I see?
   - What will I remember?

5. **Transfer Planning**
   - Where else does this learning apply?
   - What similar situations might I encounter?
   - How will I remember this when relevant?
   - What cues will trigger this learning?

### Expert Heuristic

> "Experience is not what happens to you—it's what you do with what happens to you. The same experience can teach one person everything and another nothing. The difference is reflection."

## Evidence from Literature

### Example 1: Reflective Practice
**Context:** Schön (1983) reflection in professional practice
**Evidence:** Expert practitioners engage in systematic reflection that transforms experience into expertise
**Insight:** Reflection is the mechanism for experiential learning

### Example 2: After-Action Reviews
**Context:** Ellis & Davidi (2005) learning from success vs. failure
**Evidence:** Systematic reflection improves future performance; works for both successes and failures
**Insight:** Reflection structure matters more than experience valence

### Example 3: Deliberate Practice
**Context:** Ericsson et al. (1993) expert performance
**Evidence:** Deliberate practice includes reflection on performance for improvement
**Insight:** Reflection is component of expertise development

## Decision Criteria

**Criterion 1: Reflection Investment**
- IF experience was significant/novel, THEN invest in deep reflection
- IF experience was routine, THEN light reflection or skip
- Rationale: Reflection has opportunity cost; invest where returns highest

**Criterion 2: Causal Attribution**
- IF clear causal factors, THEN form strong lessons
- IF causation unclear, THEN form tentative hypotheses
- Rationale: Lesson strength should match causal clarity

**Criterion 3: Transfer Scope**
- IF principles are general, THEN apply broadly
- IF lessons are context-specific, THEN note boundaries
- Rationale: Overgeneralization leads to misapplication

**Criterion 4: Implementation**
- IF lesson is actionable, THEN create specific implementation plan
- IF lesson is orientation change, THEN note mindset shift
- Rationale: Lessons need operationalization to affect behavior

## Contrast with Naive Approaches

**Naive Approach**: Move to next task without reflection; experience passes without learning.
**Expert Approach**: Deliberately pause to extract learning before moving on.

**Naive Approach**: Reflect only on failures; successes need no analysis.
**Expert Approach**: Reflect on both; understand why success occurred to replicate.

**Naive Approach**: Mental review only; no documentation.
**Expert Approach**: Structure reflection; externalize insights for future retrieval.

**Naive Approach**: Learn lessons; assume they'll transfer automatically.
**Expert Approach**: Explicitly plan transfer; identify cues for future application.

```
  ↓

  ↓
EVALUATION:
  - Experience reconstruction
  - Causal analysis
  - Alternative exploration
  - Insight extraction
  - Transfer planning
  ↓
OUTPUT: Lessons learned + principles + transfer triggers
```

## Expertise Level Indicators

| Level | URP-MC20 Application |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Novice** | No reflection; repeats mistakes; experiences without learning |
| **Competent** | Reflects after failures; some insight extraction; limited transfer |
| **Expert** | Systematic reflection; insight from success and failure; planned transfer |
| **Master** | Deep causal analysis; principle extraction; automatic transfer |
| **Sage** | Designs reflection practices; teaches experiential learning |
