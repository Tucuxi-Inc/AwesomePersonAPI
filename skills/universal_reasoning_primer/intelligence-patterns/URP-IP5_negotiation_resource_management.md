# URP-IP5: Negotiation Resource Management

**Type:** Intelligence Pattern (Tier 2)
**Function:** Allocating limited negotiation capital strategically
**Domain Independence:** Universal across any negotiation context
**Orchestration Role:** Ensures negotiation effort focuses on what matters most

## Pattern Definition

### Trigger Condition
- Multi-issue negotiations with limited capital
- Need to prioritize among negotiation objectives
- Resource constraints on negotiation effort
- Trade-offs between different negotiation goals
- Strategic allocation of relationship capital

### Core Procedure

1. **Capital Inventory**: What negotiation resources do I have? (Time, relationship capital, leverage, alternatives)
2. **Issue Prioritization**: Which issues matter most? What's the value of each?
3. **Capital Allocation**: How should capital be distributed across issues?
4. **Battle Selection**: Where do I fight hard? Where do I concede?
5. **Dynamic Reallocation**: How do I adjust as negotiation evolves?

### Expert Heuristic

> "Negotiation capital is finite—every battle you fight depletes resources for other battles. Win the wars that matter by choosing not to fight every skirmish."

## Evidence from Literature

- **Negotiation Capital Theory**: Limited resources require allocation (Malhotra & Bazerman)
- **Issue Prioritization**: Strategic focus on high-value issues
- **BATNA Theory**: Alternative options as negotiation resource
- **Relationship Capital**: Social capital in negotiation
