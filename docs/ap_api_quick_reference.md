## The STAR+ Method

S \- SITUATION: "Tell me about a time when..."

T \- TASK: "What was your specific responsibility?"

A \- ACTION: "What did YOU specifically do?"

R \- RESULT: "What happened?"

\+ REFLECTION: "What would you do differently?"

\+ RECURSION: "Tell me about another time..." (if needed)

## Core Traits: Probes & Signals

### 1\. CURIOSITY

**Key Probe**: "What's something you changed your mind about recently?"

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
| :---- | :---- |
| Has multiple examples ready | Struggles to name one thing |
| Describes the process of changing | Changed mind due to external pressure only |
| Shows excitement about learning | Learning only when required |
| Asks YOU questions | No questions for interviewer |

**Follow-ups**:

- "What prompted that change?"  
- "How did you discover the new information?"  
- "What have you learned recently that wasn't required?"

---

### 2\. ADAPTABILITY

**Key Probe**: "Tell me about a time your approach wasn't working."

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
| :---- | :---- |
| Recognizes failure quickly | Took too long to recognize |
| Describes specific pivot | Vague about what changed |
| Owns the decision to change | Others forced the change |
| Learned from the experience | Frames as external problem |

**Follow-ups**:

- "How did you know it wasn't working?"  
- "What did you try next?"  
- "How quickly did you make the change?"

---

### 3\. COMMUNICATION

**Key Probe**: "Explain \[X from their background\] to me as a non-expert."

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
| :---- | :---- |
| Checks if you're following | Monologues without checking |
| Uses appropriate analogies | Jargon-heavy even after simplifying |
| Adjusts based on your cues | Same explanation regardless |
| Structures information clearly | Disorganized, circles back |

**Follow-ups**:

- "Now explain it to me as an expert."  
- "Who was hardest to communicate with?"  
- "How do you know if someone understands?"

---

### 4\. COLLABORATION

**Key Probe**: "Tell me about a successful team project. What was YOUR contribution vs. the team's?"

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
| :---- | :---- |
| Clear individual \+ team attribution | Only "I" or only "we" |
| Acknowledges others' contributions | Takes/avoids all credit |
| Describes healthy disagreement | No mention of any friction |
| Adapted to team needs | Expected others to adapt |

**Follow-ups**:

- "Describe a disagreement with a teammate."  
- "How do you handle different working styles?"  
- "What makes someone hard to work with?"

---

### 5\. INITIATIVE

**Key Probe**: "What's something you did that wasn't part of your job?"

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
| :---- | :---- |
| Multiple examples readily available | Struggles to find one |
| Created value, not just activity | Activity without impact |
| Appropriate risk-taking | Reckless or overly cautious |
| Knows when to check first | Always acts or always asks |

**Follow-ups**:

- "What problem did you identify and fix unprompted?"  
- "How do you balance initiative vs. waiting?"  
- "When has your initiative caused problems?"

---

### 6\. RESILIENCE

**Key Probe**: "Tell me about your biggest professional failure."

| Green Flags | Red Flags |
| :---- | :---- |
| Discusses openly with specifics | Avoids, minimizes, or has none |
| Describes specific recovery actions | Vague about what they did next |
| Separates self-worth from outcome | Over-identifies with failure |
| Shows what they learned | No apparent learning |

**Follow-ups**:

- "What did you do immediately after?"  
- "How long until you felt back to normal?"  
- "What's something you almost gave up on?"

---

## Universal Red Flags

⚠️ **Stop and probe deeper if you notice**:

- Cannot provide specific examples (uses hypotheticals instead)  
- All stories are very recent (last 3-6 months only)  
- Stories contradict each other across traits  
- Never mentions any failure or mistake  
- Claims sole credit for team achievements  
- Defensive when asked for details  
- No questions for you at the end  
- Stories lack specific names, dates, numbers

---

## Universal Green Flags

✅ **Strong signals across any trait**:

- Specific details: names, dates, metrics, outcomes  
- Range of examples across time (years, not months)  
- Consistent patterns across different stories  
- Balanced self/team attribution  
- Discusses failures with insight  
- Asks thoughtful questions about the role  
- **Demonstrates the trait IN the interview** (e.g., shows curiosity by asking probing questions)

## Scoring Quick Guide

| Score | Meaning | Evidence Required |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **5** | Exceptional | Multiple strong examples \+ in-interview demonstration |
| **4** | Strong | Clear examples with good detail |
| **3** | Adequate | Decent example(s), some gaps |
| **2** | Concerning | Weak examples or contradictory evidence |
| **1** | Missing | No evidence or counter-evidence |

**Confidence Levels**:

- **HIGH**: Multiple examples, consistent, demonstrated in interview  
- **MEDIUM**: Good example(s), some consistency  
- **LOW**: Single example, thin detail, or inconsistent

## Probe Formulas

### When you need more specificity:

- "What did **you specifically** do?" (vs. the team)  
- "Can you give me a **concrete example**?"  
- "What **exactly** did you say/do?"  
- "What happened **immediately after**?"

### When you suspect surface answer:

- "What made that **difficult**?"  
- "What did you **consider but not do**?"  
- "What would have happened **if you hadn't**?"  
- "Who **disagreed** with that approach?"

### When you want to test consistency:

- "Tell me about **another time**..."  
- "Has that ever **not worked**?"  
- "What's an example where you did the **opposite**?"

## Pattern Applications

| Pattern | When to Use | How to Apply |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **MC24** (Assumption Surfacing) | Surface answers | "What are you assuming about what I want to hear?" |
| **MC44** (Solution Space) | Single interpretation | Consider 2-3 alternative explanations before scoring |
| **IP7** (Conflict Exploration) | Too-smooth stories | "Tell me about when that created friction" |
| **IP11** (Trust Calibration) | Self-report heavy | Weight BEHAVIORAL evidence over claims |
| **MC29** (Progress Monitoring) | Uncertain signal | "Am I getting enough information to score this?" |

---

## Post-Interview Checklist

Before finalizing scores:

□ Do I have **behavioral evidence** for each score? □ Have I considered **alternative interpretations** (MC44)? □ Am I weighting **self-report too heavily** (IP11)? □ Would a **6-month manager** see what I saw? □ Are my scores **internally consistent** across traits? □ Did I note **confidence level** for each score?

## One-Page Interview Structure

| Time | Phase | Actions |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 0-3 min | **Intro** | Welcome, explain format, build rapport |
| 3-35 min | **Core Assessment** | 5-6 traits × \~5 min each |
| 35-40 min | **Candidate Qs** | Note curiosity signal |
| 40-45 min | **Close** | Thank, explain next steps |
| Post | **Score** | Complete scorecard with evidence |
